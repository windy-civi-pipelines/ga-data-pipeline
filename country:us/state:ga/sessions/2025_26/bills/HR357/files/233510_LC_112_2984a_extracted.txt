25 LC 112 2984
House Resolution 357
156th, Jackson of the 128th, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing and commending Seth Millican of the Georgia Transportation Alliance for his
2 years of service and commitment to advancing transportation policy in Georgia; and for other
3 purposes.
4 WHEREAS, the Georgia Transportation Alliance (GTA) is the state's only organization
5 focused solely on identifying and promoting long-term transportation solutions specifically
6 connected to economic development and job creation; and
7 WHEREAS, the GTA serves as the transportation arm and affiliate of the Georgia Chamber,
8 working closely with the Georgia Department of Transportation, the Georgia Ports Authority,
9 and other key stakeholders to ensure an efficient and safe statewide multi-modal
10 transportation system; and
11 WHEREAS, Seth Millican served as Executive Director of the GTA from April 2013 to
12 November 2024, during which time he developed transportation policy solutions, supported
13 transportation SPLOST campaigns across the state, forecasted future infrastructure
14 investment needs, and educated community leaders to enhance Georgia's business ecosystem
15 and quality of life; and

16 WHEREAS, under Seth Millican's leadership, the GTA played a vital role in the passage of
17 HB 170, known as the Transportation Funding Act of 2015, which addressed critical funding
18 shortages by establishing dedicated recurring revenue for maintenance needs, advancing key
19 transportation projects, and planning for future growth; and
20 WHEREAS, as part of the Transportation Funding Act, the GTA advocated for and
21 supported two mechanisms for local transportation funding: a regional transportation
22 SPLOST and a single-county transportation SPLOST; and
23 WHEREAS, Seth Millican successfully collaborated with local, state, and federal
24 government leaders and effectively led advocacy efforts for strategic investments in
25 Georgia's transportation network, which have since benefited all Georgians; it is critical to
26 continue this momentum by further investing in the roads, bridges, ports, airports, and
27 railroads that have made Georgia the top state for business; and
28 WHEREAS, the Chairman and members of the House Committee on Transportation express
29 their gratitude to Seth Millican for his invaluable contributions to the committee's work and
30 success.
31 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
32 the members of this body recognize and commend Seth Millican for his dedication and
33 passion for advancing transportation infrastructure in Georgia as well as extend sincere best
34 wishes for future success in his new role.
35 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
36 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to Seth
37 Millican.
