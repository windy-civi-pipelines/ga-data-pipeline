Section 1:
25 LC 47 3330
By: Representatives Fleming of the 114th, LaHood of the 175th, Leverett of the 123rd, Gullett
of the 19th, Scoggins of the 14th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 2 of Title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 elections and primaries generally, so as to allow municipalities to opt in to providing advance
3 voting on Saturdays for municipal elections; to revise provisions related to the timelines for
4 calling special elections and the dates on which special elections can be held; to provide for
5 related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
7 SECTION 1.
8 Chapter 2 of Title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to elections and
9 primaries generally, is amended in Code Section 21-2-385, relating to procedure for voting
10 by absentee ballot and advance voting, by revising paragraph (1) of subsection (d) as follows:
11 "(1) There shall be a period of advance voting that shall commence:
12 (A) On the fourth Monday immediately prior to each primary or election; and
13 (B) As soon as possible prior to a runoff from any general primary or election but no
14 later than the second Monday immediately prior to such runoff
15 and shall end on the Friday immediately prior to each primary, election, or runoff.
16 Voting shall be conducted beginning at 9:00 A.M. and ending at 5:00 P.M. on weekdays,

17 other than observed state holidays, during such period and shall be conducted on the
18 second and third Saturdays during the hours of 9:00 A.M. through 5:00 P.M. and, if the
19 registrar or absentee ballot clerk so chooses, the second Sunday, the third Sunday, or both
20 the second and third Sundays prior to a primary or election during hours determined by
21 the registrar or absentee ballot clerk, but no longer than 7:00 A.M. through 7:00 P.M.;
22 provided, however, that, if such second Saturday is a public and legal holiday pursuant
23 to Code Section 1-4-1, if such second Saturday follows a public and legal holiday
24 occurring on the Thursday or Friday immediately preceding such second Saturday, or if
25 such second Saturday immediately precedes a public and legal holiday occurring on the
26 following Sunday or Monday, such advance voting shall not be held on such second
27 Saturday but shall be held on the third Saturday prior to such primary or election
28 beginning at 9:00 A.M. and ending at 5:00 P.M. Except as otherwise provided in this
29 paragraph, the registrars may extend the hours for voting to permit advance voting
30 from 7:00 A.M. until 7:00 P.M. and may provide for additional voting locations pursuant
31 to Code Section 21-2-382 to suit the needs of the electors of the jurisdiction at their
32 option; provided, however, that voting shall occur only on the days specified in this
33 paragraph and counties and municipalities shall not be authorized to conduct advance
34 voting on any other days. Notwithstanding any other provision of this paragraph to the
35 contrary, the period of advance voting in any municipal election shall not include any
36 Saturdays unless otherwise provided by the municipal election superintendent for the
37 relevant municipality."
38 SECTION 2.
39 Said chapter is further amended by revising Code Section 21-2-540, relating to conduct and
40 timing of special primaries and special elections generally, as follows:

53 (2) If a vacancy occurs in a partisan office to which the Governor is authorized to
54 appoint an individual to serve until the next general election, a special primary shall
55 precede the special election.

57 holding of same, and at least 29 60 days shall intervene between the call of a special
58 election and the holding of same. The period during which candidates may qualify to run

64 provided, however, that this requirement shall not apply to special elections to present
