25 LC 112 3250
Senate Resolution 339
28th, Parent of the 44th and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing and commending Colonel (Ret.) Harriet Staten; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, Colonel (Ret.) Harriet Staten honorably served for 24 years as a Logistics
3 Officer in the United States Army, commanding two Headquarters Companies, instructing
4 ROTC students at the University of North Carolina, and concluding her career as Executive
5 Officer to the Deputy Commanding General of the United States Army Reserve Command
6 at Fort McPherson, Georgia; and
7 WHEREAS, her exemplary service earned her numerous military awards and decorations,
8 including the Legion of Merit, the Meritorious Service Medal with two Oak Leaf Clusters,
9 the Army Commendation Medal with three Oak Leaf Clusters, and many more; and
10 WHEREAS, following her military career, Colonel (Ret.) Staten became a licensed Georgia
11 Real Estate Broker and established Promise Realty Inc. and HLS & Associates LLC; and
12 WHEREAS, she has remained actively involved in various professional and community
13 organizations, including serving as President Emeritus of the Atlanta Metro Chapter of the
14 National Association of Black Military Women; and
S. R. 339
- 1 -
25 LC 112 3250
15 WHEREAS, she further serves as a lifetime member of the Military Officers Association of
16 America, the Association of the United States Army, the National Association of Black
17 Military Women, and Disabled American Veterans; and
18 WHEREAS, Colonel (Ret.) Staten is a person of magnanimous strengths with an
19 unimpeachable reputation for integrity, intelligence, fairness, and kindness; and
20 WHEREAS, her selfless and heroic actions stand as a shining tribute to the strength of
21 human spirit and willpower, and it is abundantly fitting and proper that the outstanding
22 accomplishments of this remarkable and distinguished American be appropriately honored.
23 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
24 recognize and commend Colonel (Ret.) Harriet Staten for her effective, unselfish, and
25 dedicated public service to the State of Georgia and this nation and extend the most sincere
26 best wishes for continued health and happiness.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
28 to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to Colonel (Ret.)
29 Harriet Staten.
