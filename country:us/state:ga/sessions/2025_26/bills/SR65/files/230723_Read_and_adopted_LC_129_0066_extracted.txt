25 LC 129 0066
Senate Resolution 65
Goodman of the 8th and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Honoring the life and memory of Allison Adassa Foderingham; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, the State of Georgia mourns the loss of one of its most distinguished citizens
3 with the passing of Allison Adassa Foderingham on January 10, 2025; and
4 WHEREAS, Allison Adassa Foderingham was born on March 1, 1925, in James Hill,
5 Clarendon, Jamaica, a beloved daughter of Alfred and Louise Richards; and
6 WHEREAS, she attended James Hill Primary School, became part of the first graduating
7 class of Clarendon College in Jamaica, and later earned a master's degree in education with
8 a certificate in guidance and counseling from Western Carolina University in 1977; and
9 WHEREAS, she dedicated 51 years of superlative service to her exemplary teaching career,
10 which culminated in her final role as the director of the G.E.D./H.S. Program at The Fulton
11 Atlanta Community Action Authority in Atlanta, Georgia; and
12 WHEREAS, a woman of deep and abiding faith, she was an active member of The Episcopal
13 Church of the Holy Cross, where she was a dedicated member of several groups including
14 the Episcopal Church Women and Daughters of the King; and
S. R. 65
- 1 -
25 LC 129 0066
15 WHEREAS, she was united in love and marriage to her husband, Noel, and was blessed with
16 five remarkable children, Monica, Winsome, Ann Marie, Joan, and Andrew; six wonderful
17 grandchildren, Camille, Monique, Jheanelle, Jacqui, Jordan, and Janayah; and two
18 outstanding great-grandchildren, Dylan and Elijah; and
19 WHEREAS, she gave inspiration to many through her high ideals, morals, and deep concern
20 for her fellow citizens, and the devotion, patience, and understanding she demonstrated to
21 her family and friends were admired by others; and
22 WHEREAS, she was a person of magnanimous strengths with an unimpeachable reputation
23 for integrity, intelligence, fairness, and kindness, and by the example she made of her life,
24 she made this world a better place in which to live; and
25 WHEREAS, a compassionate and generous woman, Allison Adassa Foderingham will long
26 be remembered for her love of family, friendship, and community and will be missed by all
27 who had the great fortune of knowing her.
28 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
29 join in honoring the life and memory of Allison Adassa Foderingham and express their
30 deepest and most sincere regret at her passing.
31 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
32 to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the family of
33 Allison Adassa Foderingham.
