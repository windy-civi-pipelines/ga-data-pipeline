25 LC 52 0636
By: Representatives Park of the 107th, Griffin of the 149th, Jackson of the 128th, and Herring
of the 145th
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 7 of Chapter 4 of Title 49 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to medical assistance generally, so as to provide for the expansion of Medicaid; to
3 provide for definitions; to authorize the Department of Community Health to utilize
4 appropriations for the purposes of funding Medicaid expansion; to provide for the submission
5 of requests for waivers, amendments to waivers, and state plan amendments; to authorize the
6 office of the Commissioner of Insurance to certify qualified health plans; to provide for
7 reporting; to provide for rules and regulations ; to provide for related matters; to provide for
8 legislative notification and approval; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
10 SECTION 1.
11 Article 7 of Chapter 4 of Title 49 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
12 medical assistance generally, is amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
13 "49-4-142.6.
14 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
15 (1) 'Eligible individual' means an individual who:
H. B. 97
- 1 -
25 LC 52 0636
16 (A) Is an adult between 19 and 65 years of age with an annual income that is equal to
17 or less than 138 percent of the federal poverty level;
18 (B) Has been authenticated to be a United States citizen or a documented qualified
19 alien as defined in Section 431 of the Personal Responsibility and Work Opportunity
20 Reconciliation Act of 1996, (P.L. 104-193), as amended; and
21 (C) Is not determined to be more effectively covered through the standard Medicaid
22 program, such as an individual who is medically frail or other individual with
23 exceptional medical needs for whom coverage as provided under this Code section is
24 determined by the department to be impractical, overly complex, or would undermine
25 continuity or effectiveness of care.
26 (2) 'Healthcare coverage' means healthcare benefits as defined by rules promulgated by
27 the Commissioner of Insurance for the certification of qualified health plans.
28 (3) 'Medicaid expansion program' or 'program' means an expansion of the Medicaid
29 program in accordance with this Code section.
30 (4) 'Qualified health plan' means a health insurance plan certified by the office of the
31 Commissioner of Insurance for the Medicaid expansion program.
32 (b) On or before October 31, 2025, in consultation with the office of the Commissioner of
33 Insurance, the department shall submit to the United States Department of Health and
34 Human Services Centers for Medicare and Medicaid services pursuant to Section 1115 of
35 the federal Social Security Act a waiver request along with other necessary amendments
36 for the Medicaid expansion program under the federal Patient Protection and Affordable
37 Care Act (P.L. 111-148), as amended by the federal Health Care and Education
38 Reconciliation Act of 2010 (P.L. 111-152) and the American Rescue Plan of 2021
39 (P.L. 117-2), and any regulations or guidelines issued under such acts in accordance with
40 this Code section. The waiver request shall provide for:
41 (1) The enrollment of certain low-income individuals making less than 138 percent of
42 the federal poverty level in the eligibility category created by
H. B. 97
- 2 -
25 LC 52 0636
43 Section 1902(a)(10)(A)(i)(VIII) of the federal Social Security Act, 42 U.S.C. Section
44 1396a, into qualified healthcare plans offered on the state marketplace established under
45 Section 1332 of the federal Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act (P.L. 111-148);
46 and
47 (2) The full payment of any premiums or other direct costs associated with enrolling in
48 qualified healthcare plans for eligible individuals making less than 138 percent of the
49 federal poverty level who are enrolled in the program.
50 (c) The department is specifically authorized to pay premiums and supplemental
51 cost-sharing subsidies directly to the qualified health plans for eligible individuals enrolled
52 in the program.
53 (d) The office of the Commissioner of Insurance shall certify qualified health plans
54 providing minimum requirements for healthcare coverage.
55 (e) If the Medicaid expansion program provided for in this Code section is approved, the
56 department shall immediately report to the General Assembly that such expansion is
57 operational and annually on or before November 1 of each year shall report to the General
58 Assembly:
59 (1) The number of individuals enrolled in the Medicaid expansion program;
60 (2) Costs incurred by the state for the Medicaid expansion program;
61 (3) Estimated costs for the implementation of the Medicaid expansion program for the
62 current and following state fiscal year; and
63 (4) Recommendations to control costs of the Medicaid expansion program.
64 (f) The department and the office of the Commissioner of Insurance shall be authorized
65 to promulgate rules and regulations consistent with and necessary to carry out the
66 provisions of this Code section."

67 SECTION 2.
68 This Act shall be considered legislative notification for purposes of Code Section 49-4-142.1
69 and legislative approval for purposes of Code Section 49-4-142.2.
70 SECTION 3.
71 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
