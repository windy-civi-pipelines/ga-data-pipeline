Senate Resolution 272
By: Senator James of the 28th
A RESOLUTION
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, Douglas County, Georgia, the state's 133rd county, was created on October 17,
4 1870, and named in honor of Stephen A. Douglas, the Illinois statesman who ran against
5 Abraham Lincoln in the 1860 presidential election; and
6 WHEREAS, carved out of Campbell and Carroll counties, Douglas County encompasses 199
7 square miles and has grown into one of the 50 fastest-growing counties in the United States
8 due to its proximity to Atlanta and access to the famous Interstate 20 corridor; and
9 WHEREAS, Douglasville, the county seat, was officially established on February 25, 1875,
10 and was originally known as "Skinned" or "Skint" Chestnut in reference to a prominent
11 chestnut tree used as a Native American marker; and
12 WHEREAS, Douglas County has a rich indigenous history, once inhabited by the Creek and
13 Cherokee peoples, with numerous Mississippian burial mounds and Native American
14 artifacts found throughout the area; and

15 WHEREAS, in 1881, the Georgia Western Railroad, later acquired by the Pacific Railway
16 Company, began construction of a rail line that would connect Atlanta to Birmingham,
17 Alabama, establishing Douglas County as a key transportation hub; and
18 WHEREAS, Douglas County is home to Sweetwater Creek State Park, a beloved natural
19 resource offering fishing, boating, and hiking, as well as the historic ruins of the New
20 Manchester Manufacturing Company, which was destroyed by Union troops during
21 Sherman's March to the Sea in 1864; and
22 WHEREAS, the county prioritizes education, hosting a satellite campus of West Georgia
23 Technical College and a branch of Mercer University, providing residents with valuable
24 higher education opportunities; and
25 WHEREAS, Douglas County fosters a strong sense of community through annual events
26 such as A Taste of Douglasville, the Hunter Harvest Arts and Crafts Festival, Pioneer Days,
27 the Kris Kringle Market and Tour of Homes, and New Manchester Days; and
28 WHEREAS, the citizens of Douglas County take great pride in their community's rich
29 history, continued growth, and contributions to the State of Georgia, making it abundantly
30 fitting and proper that it be appropriately honored during this special time.
31 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
32 recognize February 27, 2025, as Douglas County Day at the state capitol.
33 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
34 to make appropriate copies of this resolution available for distribution to the public and the
35 press.
