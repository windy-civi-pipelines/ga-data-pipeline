Section 1:
25 LC 52 0779
By: Representatives Corbett of the 174th, Burchett of the 176th, Ridley of the 6th, and Rhodes
of the 124th
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Titles 36 and 50 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to local
2 government and state government, respectively, so as to acknowledge that certain
3 international organizations have no jurisdiction in this state; to prohibit the implementation
4 or enforcement of any rule, regulation, fee, tax, policy, or mandate of certain international
5 organizations; to provide for related matters; to provide for an effective date; to repeal
6 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
8 SECTION 1.
9 Title 36 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to local government, is amended
10 in Chapter 80, relating to general provisions applicable to counties, municipal corporations,
11 and other governmental entities, by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
12 "36-80-32.
13 (a) The World Health Organization and United Nations shall have no jurisdiction or power
14 within this state.
15 (b) No rule, regulation, fee, tax, policy, or mandate of any kind of the World Health
16 Organization or United Nations shall be enforced or implemented by any local board of
H. B. 570
- 1 -
25 LC 52 0779
17 education, local authority, county, municipality, consolidated government, or any political
18 subdivision of this state."
19 SECTION 2.
20 Title 50 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to state government, is amended
21 in Chapter 1, relating to general provisions, by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
22 "50-1-14.
23 (a) The World Health Organization and United Nations shall have no jurisdiction or power
24 within this state.
25 (b) No rule, regulation, fee, tax, policy, or mandate of any kind of the World Health
26 Organization or United Nations shall be enforced or implemented by this state or any
27 department, agency, instrumentality, or political subdivision of this state."
28 SECTION 3.
29 This Act shall become effective upon its approval by the Governor or upon its becoming law
30 without such approval.
31 SECTION 4.
32 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
