24 SECTION 1.
25 (a) The General Assembly finds that:
26 (1) There are inherent physical differences between males and females;
27 (2) Decisions regarding the regulation of sports should be based on promoting and
28 preserving competitive fairness and protecting student safety;
29 (3) Protecting student athletes from harm and promoting and preserving the competitive
30 fairness of sports are important state interests; and
31 (4) Requiring the designation of separate, sex-specific athletic teams and sports is
32 necessary to protect student athletes from harm and to promote and preserve the
33 competitive fairness of sports.
34 (b) It is the intent of the General Assembly that:
35 (1) Student athletes have competitively fair and safe opportunities to participate and
36 succeed in sports; and
37 (2) Female student athletes have fair opportunities to demonstrate their strength, skills, and
38 athletic abilities and to obtain recognition, accolades, college scholarships, and the
39 numerous other long-term benefits that result from participating and competing in sports.
