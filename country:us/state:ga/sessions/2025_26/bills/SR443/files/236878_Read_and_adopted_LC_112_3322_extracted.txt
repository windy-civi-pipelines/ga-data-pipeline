25 LC 112 3322
Senate Resolution 443
Anderson of the 24th and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Commending Camp Glisson upon its 100th anniversary; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, in 1925, in an effort to find a new location for a summer camp, Rev. E.D. Hale
3 and Rev. H.L. Byrd arranged to use four dirt-floor cabins and an open picnic shelter on two
4 acres owned by the Dahlonega Chamber of Commerce located north of town near Cane
5 Creek Falls; and
6 WHEREAS, the first summer camp's attendance of 26 campers has grown to over 3,200
7 summer campers and nearly 12,000 annual guests; the four original cabins have expanded
8 to over 420 year-round beds; and the two acres of leased property have grown to 380 acres
9 owned by Glisson; and
10 WHEREAS, the 50-foot-high Cane Creek Falls, which attracted Indigenous peoples for
11 centuries, powered the City of Dahlonega through Georgia Power with what was understood
12 to be the tallest overcast cast-iron water wheel east of the Mississippi; provided recreation
13 and refreshment to generations of Lumpkin County residents and students at the University
14 of North Georgia, the largest military college in the state; served as an inspiration of Creation
15 to thousands of campers and retreat guests; and continues to be open to inspire and refresh

16 campers when camps are in session and visitors to Glisson when campers and registered
17 guests are not on site; and
18 WHEREAS, the North Georgia Conference of the United Methodist Church has been
19 committed to the impact of decades of Glisson's campers who have practiced loving God and
20 loving their neighbor, becoming better and more capable citizens; campers from all walks
21 of life who have formed lifelong friendships, partnerships in marriage, and bonds as brothers
22 and sisters in the ecumenical family of faith; and campers encouraged to "Depart to Serve"
23 as leaders in churches, schools and special education, the social sector and business, the
24 armed services, public service, and as elected officials.
25 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
26 commend Camp Glisson upon its 100th anniversary for its contributions to both church and
27 society through the strengthening of the Christian character and the servant leadership
28 capacity of countless children, youth, and young adults.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
30 to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to Camp Glisson.
