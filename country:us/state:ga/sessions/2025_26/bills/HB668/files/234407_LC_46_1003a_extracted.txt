25 LC 46 1003
By: Representatives Thomas of the 21st, Seabaugh of the 34th, Washburn of the 144th,
McCollum of the 30th, Dempsey of the 13th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Title 16, Chapter 4 of Title 30, and Chapter 6 of Title 40 of the Official Code of
2 Georgia Annotated, relating to crimes and offenses, rights of persons with disabilities, and
3 uniform rules of the road, respectively, so as to change references to assistance and guide
4 dogs to service dogs; to provide for definitions and conforming changes; to provide for a
5 criminal penalty for a person who causes death or physical harm to a service dog; to provide
6 for exceptions; to revise provisions relating to the use and training of service dogs; to provide
7 for a penalty for persons who misrepresent the need for a service dog; to provide for related
8 matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
10 SECTION 1.
11 Title 16 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to crimes and offenses, is
12 amended in Chapter 11, relating to offenses against public order and safety, by revising Code
13 Section 16-11-107.1, relating to harassment of assistance dog by humans or other dogs and
14 penalty, as follows:
15 "16-11-107.1.
16 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
H. B. 668
- 1 -
25 LC 46 1003
17 (1) 'Assistance dog' means a dog that is or has been trained by a licensed or certified
18 person, organization, or agency to perform physical tasks for a physically challenged
19 person. Assistance dogs include guide or leader dogs that guide individuals who are
20 legally blind; hearing dogs that alert individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing to
21 specific sounds; and service dogs for individuals with disabilities other than blindness or
22 deafness, which are trained to perform a variety of physical tasks, including, but not
23 limited to, pulling a wheelchair, lending balance support, picking up dropped objects, or
24 providing assistance in a medical crisis.
25 (2) 'Harass' means to engage in any conduct directed toward an assistance a service dog
26 that is knowingly likely to impede or interfere with the assistance service dog's
27 performance of its duties or that places the blind, deaf, or physically limited physically
28 or mentally impaired person being served or assisted by the service dog in danger of
29 injury.
30 (3)(2) 'Notice' means an oral or otherwise communicated warning proscribing the
31 behavior of another person and a request that the person stop the particular behavior.
32 (3) 'Service dog' shall have the same meaning as set forth in Code Section 30-4-1.
33 (b) Any person who knowingly and intentionally with criminal negligence harasses or
34 attempts to harass an assistance a service dog, knowing when he or she knows or should
35 know the dog to be an assistance a service dog, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon
36 conviction thereof, shall be punished by imprisonment confinement for not less than 90
37 days or a fine not to exceed $500.00, or both.
38 (c) Any person who has received notice that his or her behavior is interfering with the use
39 of an assistance a service dog and who continues to knowingly and intentionally harass an
40 assistance the service dog, knowing the dog to be an assistance dog, shall be guilty of a
41 misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished by imprisonment
42 confinement for not less than 90 days or a fine not to exceed $500.00, or both, provided
H. B. 668
- 2 -
25 LC 46 1003
43 that any person who is convicted of a second or subsequent violation of this subsection
44 shall be punished as for a misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature.
45 (d) Any person who knowingly and intentionally with criminal negligence allows his or
46 her dog to harass an assistance a service dog, knowing when he or she knows or should
47 know the dog to be an assistance a service dog, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon
48 conviction thereof, shall be punished by imprisonment confinement for not less than 90
49 days or a fine not to exceed $500.00, or both, provided that any person who is convicted
50 of a second or subsequent violation of this subsection shall be punished as for a
51 misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature.
52 (e) Any person who knowingly and intentionally with criminal negligence allows his or
53 her dog to cause death or physical harm to an assistance a service dog by rendering a part
54 of the assistance service dog's body useless or by seriously disfiguring the assistance
55 service dog, knowing when he or she knows or should know the dog to be an assistance a
56 service dog, shall be punished as for a misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature.
57 (f) Any person who causes death or physical harm to a service dog by rendering a part of
58 the service dog's body useless or by seriously disfiguring the service dog, when he or she
59 knows or should know the dog to be a service dog, shall be guilty of a felony and, upon
60 conviction thereof, shall be punished by imprisonment for not less than one nor more than
61 five years and by a fine of not more than $10,000.00. This subsection shall not apply to
62 the euthanasia of a service dog for humane purposes in a manner consistent with the
63 provisions of Code Section 4-11-5.1.
64 (g) The provisions of subsections (e) and (f) of this Code section shall not apply in
65 instances where the service dog is the aggressor toward a person or a person's dog or other
66 animal. In such instances, the person shall have the right to defend his or her person or
67 property in a manner consistent with Code Section 4-8-5."

79 SECTION 3.
80 Chapter 4 of Title 30 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to rights of persons
81 with disabilities, is amended by revising Code Section 30-4-1, relating to definitions, as
82 follows:
83 "30-4-1.
84 As used in this chapter, the term:
85 (1) 'Activities of daily living' means tasks that are essential, routine, and provide for an
86 individual's basic physical needs, including but not limited to eating, toileting, grooming,
87 dressing, shaving, walking, and transferring.
88 (2) 'Housing accommodations' means any real property or portion thereof which is used
89 or occupied, or intended, arranged, or designed to be used or occupied, as the home,
90 residence, or sleeping place of one or more human beings but shall not include any
91 single-family residence the occupants of which rent, lease, or furnish for compensation
92 not more than one room therein.

93 (2) 'Physically disabled person' means any person, regardless of age, who is subject to
94 a physiological defect or deficiency regardless of its cause, nature, or extent that renders
95 the person unable to move about without the aid of crutches, a wheelchair, or any other
96 form of support, or that limits the person's functional ability to ambulate, climb, descend,
97 sit, rise, or to perform any related function.
98 (3) 'Owner training' means training of a dog to become a service dog by an owner who
99 is a physically or mentally impaired person by teaching the dog to perform the specific
100 tasks that directly assist such owner and directly relate to such owner's specific disability
101 and to take a specific action when needed to assist the person.
102 (4) 'Physically or mentally impaired person' means any person, regardless of age, who,
103 regardless of the cause, nature, or extent, is rendered substantially limited or unable to
104 perform one or more activities of daily living as a result of developmental deficiencies,
105 the aging process, learning disabilities, physical injury or disease, cognitive impairments,
106 or psychological or social disabilities.
107 (5) 'Service dog' means any domestic canine that has been individually trained to do
108 work or perform tasks that directly assist a physically or mentally impaired person and
109 directly relate to such person's specific disability and to take a specific action when
110 needed to assist the person, or if still in training, that is operating under the guidance of
111 a working service dog. Such term shall not include a dog that has not been trained to do
112 a specific job or task or that solely provides comfort, companionship, or emotional
113 support to a person, regardless of such person's disability status."
