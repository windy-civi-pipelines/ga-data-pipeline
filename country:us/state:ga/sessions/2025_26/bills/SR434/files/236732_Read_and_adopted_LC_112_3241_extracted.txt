25 LC 112 3241
Senate Resolution 434
A RESOLUTION
1 Commending Myron Fountain, the "Prison Dr."; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, known as the "Prison Dr.," Myron Fountain has dedicated his life to mentoring
3 at-risk youth and steering them toward positive behavioral change through comprehensive
4 mentorship programs; and
5 WHEREAS, having served over 20 years in prison due to a conviction at the age of 19,
6 Myron Fountain took the opportunity to reform himself through self-help programs, martial
7 arts training, and education, emerging with a deep commitment to guiding young individuals
8 away from incarceration; and
9 WHEREAS, originally from Detroit, Michigan, Myron Fountain first established his
10 mentorship work in his home town, incorporating a nonprofit in 2018 before relocating to
11 Georgia in 2020 to start fresh in Atlanta; and
12 WHEREAS, through his nonprofit, The Prison Dr., Myron Fountain provides direct
13 intervention services, including house calls, customized mentorship plans, and follow-up
14 support, to ensure lasting personal transformation among troubled youth; and
S. R. 434
- 1 -

15 WHEREAS, his mentorship programs have positively influenced the lives of more than 100
16 young individuals in metro Atlanta, building on his prior work mentoring around 300 youths
17 in Detroit; and
18 WHEREAS, The Prison Dr.'s corrective mentorship programs address the root causes of
19 rebellious and aggressive behavior, boasting an 80 percent success rate and leading to
20 significant improvements in self-esteem, increased academic retention, and reduced
21 involvement with the justice system; and
22 WHEREAS, Myron Fountain's work is grounded in a philosophy of personal responsibility,
23 parental engagement, and spiritual accountability, emphasizing the importance of strong
24 guidance and community support in shaping the future of young people; and
25 WHEREAS, his unwavering dedication to youth mentorship and intervention continues to
26 make a profound impact in metro Atlanta, offering guidance, discipline, and hope to those
27 at risk of being lost to the criminal justice system.
28 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
29 commend Myron Fountain, the "Prison Dr.," for his exceptional efforts in mentoring and
30 transforming the lives of at-risk youth and extend best wishes for his continued success.
31 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
32 to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to Myron Fountain.
