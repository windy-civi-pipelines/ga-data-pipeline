25 LC 61 0188S
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to education, so as to
2 require the possession of opioid antagonists by institutions within the University System of
3 Georgia and units within the Technical College System of Georgia; to provide for
4 definitions; to provide for possession of opioid antagonists by certain individuals; to require
5 the maintenance of a stock supply of opioid antagonists; to provide for the use and location
6 of opioid antagonist storage compartments; to authorize certain personnel to administer
7 opioid antagonists and carry opioid antagonists; to provide for immunity; to provide that
8 neither civil liability nor professional discipline shall accrue to personnel, the systems, or
9 institutions or units thereof; to provide for an exception; to amend Code Section 31-2A-20
10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to accessibility to opioid antagonists in
11 government buildings and courthouses, guidelines and training, and limitations on liability,
12 so as to make conforming changes; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws;
13 and for other purposes.
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
H. B. 419 (SUB)
- 1 -
25 LC 61 0188S
15 PART I.
16 SECTION 1-1.
17 Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to education, is amended in Part
18 2 of Article 2 of Chapter 3, relating to university system, by adding a new Code section to
19 read as follows:
20 "20-3-88.
21 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
22 (1) 'Automated external defibrillator' means a defibrillator which:
23 (A) Is capable of cardiac rhythm analysis;
24 (B) Will charge and be capable of being activated to deliver a countershock after
25 electrically detecting the presence of certain cardiac dysrhythmias; and
26 (C) Is capable of continuous recording of the cardiac dysrhythmia at the scene with a
27 mechanism for transfer and storage or for printing for review subsequent to use.
28 (2) 'Community funding source' means local sources of capital provided by local banking
29 institutions, including credit unions, community foundations, county or municipal
30 governments, businesses, or residents. Such funding sources may include grants, gifts,
31 contracts, monetary donations, or loans.
32 (3) 'Institution' means any college, school, academy, university, or experiment station at
33 any particular location which forms a part of the university system.
34 (4) 'Opioid antagonist' shall have the same meaning as set forth in Code Section
35 26-4-116.2.
36 (5) 'Opioid antagonist storage compartment' means the structure in which opioid
37 antagonists may be stored.
38 (6) 'University system' means the University System of Georgia.

39 (b) No student, visitor, or university system employee shall be prohibited from possessing
40 an opioid antagonist on university system property or at a university system sponsored
41 activity.
42 (c) Subject to available funding from community funding sources, each institution shall
43 acquire and maintain a supply of opioid antagonists in any secure location in such a
44 quantity to be used for multiple opioid overdose events; provided, however, that, if there
45 occurs a shortage of opioid antagonists, the institution shall make a reasonable effort to
46 maintain its supply of opioid antagonists. Any such supply shall be maintained in
47 accordance with the manufacturer's instructions.
48 (d) Subject to available funding from community funding sources, each institution
49 maintaining one or more automated external defibrillators shall maintain opioid antagonists
50 in an opioid antagonist storage compartment which shall be located within three feet of
51 such defibrillators or as close as space reasonably allows.
52 (e) Any university system personnel may administer an opioid antagonist to any person
53 who the university system personnel believes in good faith to be experiencing an opioid
54 overdose:
55 (1) While at an institution;
56 (2) While at a university system sponsored activity;
57 (3) While under the supervision of university system personnel; or
58 (4) Before or after normal institution activities.
59 (f) Any university system personnel who in good faith administers or chooses not to
60 administer an opioid antagonist shall be immune from civil liability or professional
61 discipline for any act or omission to act related to the administration of an opioid
62 antagonist, except that such immunity shall not apply to an act of willful or wanton
63 misconduct.
64 (g) Neither civil liability nor professional discipline shall accrue to university system
65 personnel and no civil liability shall accrue to the university system or to any institution of

67 however, that immunity shall not apply to an act of willful or wanton misconduct."
