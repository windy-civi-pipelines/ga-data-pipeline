25 LC 49 2115
By: Representatives Crawford of the 89th, Cox of the 28th, McClain of the 109th, Willis of the
55th, and Douglas of the 78th
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 6 of Chapter 2 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, the
2 "Quality Basic Education Act," so as to address mental health risks for student athletes; to
3 provide for guidelines and other relevant materials to inform and educate public school and
4 participating private school student athletes, their parents or guardians, school personnel, and
5 coaches about mental health risks for student athletes and raising awareness of mental health
6 resources available to student athletes; to provide for definitions; to require annual review
7 by coaches; to provide for limited liability; to encourage additional implementation; to
8 provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
10 SECTION 1.
11 Article 6 of Chapter 2 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, the "Quality
12 Basic Education Act," is amended in Part 15, relating to miscellaneous provisions, by adding
13 a new Code section to read as follows:
14 "20-2-324.8.
15 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
H. B. 335
- 1 -
25 LC 49 2115
16 (1) 'Athletic association' means any association of schools or any other similar
17 organization which acts as an organizing, sanctioning, scheduling, or rule-making body
18 for interscholastic athletic activities in which public schools and participating private
19 schools in this state participate.
20 (2) 'Healthcare provider' means a licensed physician, another licensed individual under
21 the supervision of a licensed physician, licensed psychiatrist, licensed psychologist,
22 licensed clinical social worker, licensed professional counselor, clinical nurse specialist
23 in psychiatric/mental health, or other licensed mental or behavioral health clinician or
24 specialist who has received training on screening school age children for mental health
25 risks.
26 (3) 'Interscholastic athletic activity' means interscholastic athletics and practices and
27 scrimmages related to interscholastic athletics.
28 (4) 'Participating private school' means a private school in this state whose students or
29 teams compete against students or teams from a public school or local school system in
30 this state.
31 (5) 'Student athlete' means a public school or participating private school student in
32 grades six through 12 participating in or desiring to participate in an interscholastic
33 athletic activity.
34 (b) Each athletic association shall, in consultation with the Department of Behavioral
35 Health and Developmental Disabilities, develop and post on its public website guidelines
36 and other relevant materials, including, but not limited to, approved videos or links thereto,
37 to inform and educate student athletes, their parents or guardians, school personnel, and
38 coaches about mental health risks for student athletes and raising awareness of mental
39 health resources available to student athletes. In developing such guidelines and materials,
40 an athletic association may utilize educational videos available at no cost to the state for
41 the purpose of educating school personnel and coaches.
H. B. 335
- 2 -

42 (c) Each public school and participating private school which includes one or more grades
43 from grades six through 12 shall provide information to each student athlete's parent or
44 guardian at least once each school year regarding mental health risks for student athletes
45 and to raise awareness of mental health resources available to student athletes.
46 (d)(1) Once each school year, each coach of an interscholastic athletic activity shall
47 review the guidelines and other relevant materials, including, but not limited to, videos,
48 approved by each athletic association of which the coach's school is a member pursuant
49 to subsection (b) of this Code section.
50 (2) A coach shall not be eligible to coach an interscholastic athletic activity until he or
51 she completes the requirements contained in this subsection.
52 (e) Nothing in this Code section shall be construed to create any liability for, or create a
53 cause of action against, a local board of education or other governing body of a public
54 school or participating private school, or an athletic association, or the officers, employees,
55 or volunteers of any such entities for any act or omission related to the removal or
56 nonremoval of a student athlete participating in an interscholastic athletic activity in
57 conjunction with an actual or perceived risk to the mental health of such student athlete or
58 another student athlete; provided, however, that this subsection shall not include healthcare
59 providers unless they are acting in a volunteer capacity.
60 (f) The sponsors of youth athletic activities, as defined in subsection (a) of Code Section
61 20-2-324.1, are encouraged to implement the provisions of this Code section."
62 SECTION 2.
63 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
