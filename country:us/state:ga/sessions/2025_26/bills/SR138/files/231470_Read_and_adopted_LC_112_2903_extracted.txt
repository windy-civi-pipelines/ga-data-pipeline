25 LC 112 2903
Senate Resolution 138
and Harbin of the 16th
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing and commending Georgia Reads Coach Malcolm Mitchell; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, literacy skills are necessary for life and work; improving literacy in all age
4 groups is a moral imperative and necessary for Georgia's workforce and thriving economy;
5 and
6 WHEREAS, in 2023, the General Assembly recognized the importance of literacy with the
7 passage of two bills: SB 211, which created the Georgia Council on Literacy, and HB 538,
8 which required comprehensive education reforms for reading instruction in the science of
9 reading; and
10 WHEREAS, the Georgia Council on Literacy and its 30 members began work on July 1,
11 2023, monitoring the implementation of the Early Literacy Act, reviewing literacy data
12 among various populations, and creating Georgia's 2030 State Literacy Plan with the goal
13 of becoming the state with the greatest literacy gains in the country and ensuring that every
14 child is a reader, every teacher is prepared, and every community is supported; and
S. R. 138
- 1 -
25 LC 112 2903
15 WHEREAS, Malcolm Mitchell, Super Bowl champion, author, and speaker, was named the
16 Georgia Reads Coach by the Georgia Council on Literacy and was tasked by the council to
17 help Georgia reach its goal of all citizens possessing the literacy skills essential for both
18 workforce and daily life; and
19 WHEREAS, founder of the Share the Magic Foundation and player for both the University
20 of Georgia Bulldogs and New England Patriots, Malcolm has demonstrated his energy,
21 dedication, and commitment to inspiring kids to read; and
22 WHEREAS, through in-school programs, virtual challenges, and community partner
23 programs, the Share the Magic Foundation has reached students not only in Atlanta and
24 across Georgia but also in all 50 states and in 17 countries; and
25 WHEREAS, Malcolm's ability to connect with young students makes his Read with Malcolm
26 programs effective in motivating children to read, parents to serve as role models, and
27 communities to join in turning around the state's literacy challenges and turning the page to
28 a brighter future for all Georgians.
29 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
30 recognize and commend Georgia Reads Coach Malcolm Mitchell for his important work in
31 using literacy to empower individuals to find successful careers, keep healthy, and contribute
32 to their family and community.
33 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
34 to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to Georgia Reads
35 Coach Malcolm Mitchell.
S. R. 138
