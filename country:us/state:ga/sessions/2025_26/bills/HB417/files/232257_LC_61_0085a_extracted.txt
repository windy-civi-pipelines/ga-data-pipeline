Section 1:
25 LC 61 0085
By: Representatives Gaines of the 120th, Hitchens of the 161st, Dickey of the 134th, and
Greene of the 154th
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Code Section 20-3-411 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 definitions regarding tuition equalization grants at private colleges and universities, so as to
3 revise the definition of approved school; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting
4 laws; and for other purposes.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
6 SECTION 1.
7 Code Section 20-3-411 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to definitions
8 regarding tuition equalization grants at private colleges and universities, is amended by
9 revising division (2)(B)(i) as follows:
10 "(B)(i) A qualified proprietary institution of higher education located in this state
11 which is a baccalaureate degree-granting institution of higher education; which is
12 accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on
13 Colleges as a Level VI institution; which is not a Bible school or college (or, at the
14 graduate level, a school or college of theology or divinity); which admits as regular
15 students only persons who have a high school diploma, a state approved high school
16 equivalency (HSE) diploma, or a degree from an accredited postsecondary institution;

17 whose students are eligible to participate in the federal Pell Grant program; which has
18 been reviewed and approved for operation and for receipt of tuition equalization grant
19 funds by the Georgia Nonpublic Postsecondary Education Commission; which is
20 domiciled and incorporated in the State of Georgia; which has been in existence in the
21 State of Georgia for at least ten years located in this state since on or before January
22 1, 2021; and which met all of the requirements of this subparagraph by January 1,
23 2011 2021; provided, however, that the criteria for approval for receipt of tuition
24 equalization grant funds shall include but not be limited to areas of course study,;
25 quality of academic instruction,; student placement rate,; research and library sources,
26 resources; faculty,; support staff,; financial resources,; physical plant facilities
27 resources, instructional campuses, facilities, or other physical sites; and support and
28 equipment resources."
29 SECTION 2.
30 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
