Section 1:
25 LC 48 1615-ECS
substitute to HB 105:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 1 of Chapter 9 of Title 34 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to general provisions of workers' compensation, so as to require the State Board of
3 Workers' Compensation to notify the Department of Administrative Services when the board
4 has reasonable cause to believe that an injured worker is an eligible peace officer; to provide
5 for a definition; to amend Chapter 1 of Title 35 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
6 relating to general provisions of law enforcement officers and agencies, so as to require a
7 certain notification to certain peace officers who become disabled in the line of duty under
8 certain circumstances; to provide for a definition; to amend Code Section 45-9-78 of the
9 Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to payment of indemnification for death or
10 disability generally, designation of method of payment, and procedure for making payments,
11 so as to increase the indemnification award of certain state public school personnel in the
12 event of death; to provide for a short title; to provide for related matters; to provide for an
13 effective date; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
15 SECTION 1.
16 This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Daniel D. Podsiadly, Jr. Act."
- 1 -
25 LC 48 1615-ECS
17 SECTION 2.
18 Article 1 of Chapter 9 of Title 34 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
19 general provisions of workers' compensation, is amended by adding a new Code section to
20 read as follows:
21 "34-9-26.
22 (a) As used in this Code section, the term 'eligible peace officer' means any individual who
23 is employed on a full-time or part-time basis by the state or any municipality, county, or
24 other political subdivision thereof; who is required by the terms of such employment,
25 whether by election or appointment, to maintain public order or to make arrests for all
26 criminal offenses; who is required by the terms of such employment to comply with the
27 requirements of the 'Georgia Peace Officer Standards and Training Act' contained in
28 Chapter 8 of Title 35; and who suffers permanent or temporary disability in the line of duty
29 due to a direct interaction with a criminal suspect, arrestee, or inmate.
30 (b) In the event that the State Board of Workers' Compensation has reasonable cause to
31 believe that an injured employee is an eligible peace officer, the board shall notify the
32 Department of Administrative Services. Additionally, the board shall notify the injured
33 employee of such belief and such notification shall include such appropriate contact
34 information of the Georgia State Indemnification Fund and the temporary disability
35 compensation program of Parts 1 and 2, respectively, of Article 5 of Chapter 9 of Title 45
36 and other information as shall be determined necessary by the Department of
37 Administrative Services through the promulgation of rules and regulations."
38 SECTION 3.
39 Chapter 1 of Title 35 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to general
40 provisions of law enforcement officers and agencies, is amended by adding a new Code
41 section to read as follows:
- 2 -
25 LC 48 1615-ECS
42 "35-1-24.
43 (a) As used in this Code section, the term 'eligible peace officer' means any individual who
44 is employed on a full-time or part-time basis by the state or any municipality, county, or
45 other political subdivision thereof; who is required by the terms of such employment,
46 whether by election or appointment, to maintain public order or to make arrests for all
47 criminal offenses; who is required by the terms of such employment to comply with the
48 requirements of the 'Georgia Peace Officer Standards and Training Act' contained in
49 Chapter 8 of this title; and who suffers permanent or temporary disability in the line of duty
50 due to a direct interaction with a criminal suspect, arrestee, or inmate.
51 (b) Each law enforcement agency of this state shall notify every employee whom such
52 agency has reasonable cause to believe may be an eligible peace officer of the existence
53 of the Georgia State Indemnification Fund and the temporary disability compensation
54 program of Parts 1 and 2, respectively, of Article 5 of Chapter 9 of Title 45. Such
55 notification shall be given within ten days of such employee suffering an injury which may
56 reasonably cause such employee to suffer a permanent or temporary disability.
57 Additionally, such notification shall include such appropriate contact and other information
58 as shall be determined necessary by the Department of Administrative Services through the
59 promulgation of rules and regulations."
60 SECTION 4.
61 Code Section 45-9-78 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to payment of
62 indemnification for death or disability generally, designation of method of payment, and
63 procedure for making payments, is amended by revising subsection (b) as follows:
64 "(b) For any compensable claim filed under this article, payment shall be made as follows:
65 (1) In the case of permanent disability, the eligible disabled person pursuant to this
66 article may elect payment of $75,000.00 in equal installments over a period of five years
- 3 -
25 LC 48 1615-ECS
67 or a payment in lump sum which shall consist of $75,000.00 reduced to its present value
68 upon the basis of interest calculated at the rate of 6 percent per annum; and
69 (2) In the case of death, payment shall be made to the estate of a person who is eligible
70 for indemnification under this article as follows: the executor or administrator may elect
71 payment of $75,000.00 $150,000.00 in equal installments over a period of five years or
72 a payment in lump sum which shall consist of $75,000.00 $150,000.00 reduced to its
73 present value upon the basis of interest calculated at the rate of 6 percent per annum."
74 SECTION 5.
75 This Act shall become effective upon its approval by the Governor or upon its becoming law
76 without such approval.
77 SECTION 6.
78 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
