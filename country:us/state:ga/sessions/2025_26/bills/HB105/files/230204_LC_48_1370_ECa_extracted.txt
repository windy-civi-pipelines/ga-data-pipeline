Section 1:
25 LC 48 1370-EC
By: Representatives Wade of the 9th, Gambill of the 15th, Hong of the 103rd, McDonald III
of the 26th, Erwin of the 32nd, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Code Section 45-9-78 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 payment of indemnification for death or disability generally, designation of method of
3 payment, and procedure for making payments, so as to increase the indemnification award
4 of certain state public school personnel in the event of death; to provide for related matters;
5 to provide for an effective date; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
7 SECTION 1.
8 Code Section 45-9-78 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to payment of
9 indemnification for death or disability generally, designation of method of payment, and
10 procedure for making payments, is amended by revising subsection (b) as follows:
11 "(b) For any compensable claim filed under this article, payment shall be made as follows:
12 (1) In the case of permanent disability, the eligible disabled person pursuant to this
13 article may elect payment of $75,000.00 in equal installments over a period of five years
14 or a payment in lump sum which shall consist of $75,000.00 reduced to its present value
15 upon the basis of interest calculated at the rate of 6 percent per annum; and

16 (2) In the case of death, payment shall be made to the estate of a person who is eligible
17 for indemnification under this article as follows: the executor or administrator may elect
18 payment of $75,000.00 $150,000.00 in equal installments over a period of five years or
19 a payment in lump sum which shall consist of $75,000.00 $150,000.00 reduced to its
20 present value upon the basis of interest calculated at the rate of 6 percent per annum."
21 SECTION 2.
22 This Act shall become effective upon its approval by the Governor or upon its becoming law
23 without such approval.
24 SECTION 3.
25 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
