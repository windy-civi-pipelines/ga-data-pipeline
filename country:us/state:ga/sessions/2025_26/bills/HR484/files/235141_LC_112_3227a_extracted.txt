25 LC 112 3227
House Resolution 484
58th, and Frye of the 122nd
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing and commending Georgia State University; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, Georgia State University, founded in 1913, is a premier urban research
3 university focused on research, service, and advanced educational opportunities for more
4 than 52,000 students; and
5 WHEREAS, Georgia State University is an enterprising public research university in
6 downtown Atlanta with satellite campuses in DeKalb, Fulton, and Newton counties; and
7 WHEREAS, Georgia State University has been ranked the nation's number one public
8 university for excellence in undergraduate teaching by U.S. News and World Report for five
9 consecutive years; and
10 WHEREAS, Georgia State University, a national leader in graduating students from widely
11 diverse backgrounds, has been named among the top five "Most Innovative" universities in
12 the country by U.S. News and World Report for a decade and counting; and
H. R. 484
- 1 -
25 LC 112 3227
13 WHEREAS, Georgia State University is recognized as a national leader in helping students
14 reach their academic goals and, since 2003, has raised its six-year graduation rate by 74
15 percent; and
16 WHEREAS, Georgia State University has received an $80 million gift from the Robert W.
17 Woodruff Foundation, the largest gift in university history, to embark on an ambitious
18 campus transformation to integrate its downtown footprint and create vibrant,
19 people-centered spaces for students and the city; and
20 WHEREAS, Georgia State University continues its important role of producing new
21 knowledge that transforms lives and communities thanks to a research portfolio that has
22 invested more than $1.2 billion in research expenditures over the past six years; and
23 WHEREAS, Georgia State University has broken ground on a new, nine-story, $100 million
24 Research Tower on its Atlanta Campus that will expand its capacity for discovery and
25 learning; and
26 WHEREAS, Georgia State University's National Institute for Student Success (NISS)
27 received a $2 million grant from the Ascendium Education Group that will enable the
28 institute to provide its services to six Georgia colleges and universities, helping them identify
29 and resolve institutional barriers to college completion; and
30 WHEREAS, the United States Department of Education (DOE) has awarded the National
31 Institute for Student Success (NISS) its first-ever Trailblazer Award, recognizing its success
32 in increasing graduation rates through data-driven, evidence-based interventions and its
33 impact on the field of student success nationally; and
H. R. 484
- 2 -
25 LC 112 3227
34 WHEREAS, more than 200 Georgia State University student-athletes achieved Sun Belt
35 Conference academic honors for the 2023-24 academic year, including 130 student-athletes
36 who earned a place on the Commissioner's List with a grade point average of 3.5 or higher;
37 and
38 WHEREAS, Georgia State University's student-athletes collectively finished the year with
39 a 3.39 grade point average, higher than the overall Sun Belt GPA; and
40 WHEREAS, Georgia State University continues its important role in addressing critical
41 quality-of-life issues through a highly active research community dedicated to uncovering
42 new knowledge in cancer, heart disease, mental health, public health, and more, earning
43 nearly $186 million in research funding in Fiscal Year 2024; and
44 WHEREAS, Georgia State University has a $3.2 billion economic impact on metro Atlanta
45 each year; and
46 WHEREAS, Georgia State University has adopted a ten-year strategic plan focused on four
47 initiatives: Student Success 2.0; Identity, Placemaking and Belonging; Innovating Research,
48 Scholarship and Creative Activity; and Beyond College to Career; and
49 WHEREAS, nearly two dozen initiatives have been launched under the BluePrint to 2033
50 strategic plan and are being implemented under each pillar.
51 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
52 the members of this body recognize and commend Georgia State University for its all-around
53 success in 2024 and the pride and prestige it brought to the students, staff, and alumni of
54 Georgia State University.
H. R. 484

55 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
56 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to
57 Georgia State University.
