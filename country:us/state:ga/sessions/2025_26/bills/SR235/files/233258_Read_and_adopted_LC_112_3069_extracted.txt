25 LC 112 3069
Senate Resolution 235
Echols of the 49th and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing and commending the Georgia Beverage Association; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, Georgia has a strong and growing infrastructure for recycling; and
3 WHEREAS, the issue of litter in our environment is a growing concern, and the improper
4 disposal of litter and recyclables impacts our world-renowned oceans, rivers, and waterways,
5 negatively affecting Georgia's environment, tourism, and economy; and
6 WHEREAS, go it alone strategies will not have a substantial impact or improve recycling
7 rates, making it imperative that we shape the solution with collective action; and
8 WHEREAS, the beverage industry is committed to creating a circular economy and
9 improving the recycling rates of packaging through projects such as the Georgia Beverage
10 Association's Made to be Remade initiative; and
11 WHEREAS, the Made to be Remade initiative is committed to educating consumers about
12 the value of recycling and investing in state-of-the-art infrastructure upgrades to expand
13 access to recycling and support community education efforts; and
S. R. 235
- 1 -
25 LC 112 3069
14 WHEREAS, The Coca-Cola Company, Keurig, Dr. Pepper, and PepsiCo have launched
15 several initiatives to improve the recycling of beverage containers and reduce the industry's
16 use of new materials by partnering with environment and sustainability leaders such as the
17 World Wildlife Fund, Closed Loop Partners, and The Recycling Partnership; and
18 WHEREAS, the industry's Every Bottle Back initiative is directing the equivalent of $400
19 million to The Recycling Partnership and Closed Loop Partners through a new $100 million
20 industry fund that will be matched three-to-one by other grants and investors to improve the
21 collection of plastic bottle for recycling; and
22 WHEREAS, the industry's Every Bottle Back initiative is working with the World Wildlife
23 Fund to measure and track progress toward reducing the industry's plastic footprint and
24 improving the quality and availability of recycled plastic in key regions of the county,
25 including Georgia; and
26 WHEREAS, the Georgia Beverage Association's Made to be Remade initiative is committed
27 to producing 100 percent recyclable cans, bottles, and caps.
28 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
29 recognize and commend the Georgia Beverage Association for the increased recycling efforts
30 it has undertaken through its Made to be Remade initiative as well as extend best wishes for
31 continued success.
32 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
33 to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the Georgia
34 Beverage Association.
S. R. 235
