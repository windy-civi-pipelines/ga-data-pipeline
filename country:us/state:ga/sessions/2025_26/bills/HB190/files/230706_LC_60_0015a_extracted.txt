25 LC 60 0015
By: Representatives Lim of the 98th, Davis of the 87th, and Lupton of the 83rd
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 3 of Title 44 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 regulation of specialized land transactions, so as to prohibit condominium associations and
3 property owners' associations from retaliating against property owners for taking certain
4 actions; to provide for circumstances that are not considered retaliation; to provide
5 definitions; to provide for remedies; to provide for a defense; to provide for related matters;
6 to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
8 SECTION 1.
9 Chapter 3 of Title 44 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to regulation of
10 specialized land transactions, is amended in Article 3, relating to condominiums, by adding
11 a new Code section to read as follows:
12 "44-3-118.
13 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
14 (1) 'Permissible action' means any of the following actions taken by a unit owner in
15 relation to his or her association:
H. B. 190
- 1 -
25 LC 60 0015
16 (A) In good faith exercising against the association any legal right or remedy granted
17 to such unit owner by contract or law;
18 (B) In good faith filing a complaint or report or participating in the investigation of a
19 complaint or report with a court or governmental entity regarding the association;
20 (C) Providing feedback or input on or otherwise communicating about an association
21 matter; or
22 (D) Establishing, attempting to establish, or participating in an organization or a group,
23 however organized, to address problems related to the association.
24 (2) 'Retaliatory action' means any of the following actions taken against a unit owner by
25 an association, its board, or any person acting on behalf of the association:
26 (A) Filing an action to recover sums due, for damages, or for injunctive relief under
27 Code Section 44-3-76 or 44-3-106, or for foreclosure on a lien for assessments under
28 Code Section 44-3-109; in either case, under circumstances where an enforcement
29 action could have, but had not, been pursued previously;
30 (B) Depriving the unit owner of the use of his or her unit or any common elements,
31 except for reasons authorized by contract or law;
32 (C) Reducing or suspending services to the unit owner, except as provided in paragraph
33 (1) of subsection (c) of this Code section;
34 (D) Increasing the unit owner's assessment fees, except as provided in paragraph (1)
35 of subsection (c) of this Code section; or
36 (E) Materially interfering with the unit owner's rights under any condominium
37 instruments or any rules and regulations adopted by the association pursuant to the
38 condominium instruments.
39 (b) A unit owner establishes a prima-facie case of retaliation by demonstrating that:
40 (1) He or she took a permissible action relating to a life, health, safety, or habitability
41 concern; and
H. B. 190
- 2 -
25 LC 60 0015
42 (2) The association took a retaliatory action within three months of such permissible
43 action.
44 (c) An association shall not be liable under this Code section:
45 (1) For lawfully increasing assessment fees or reducing or suspending services:
46 (A) Under any written agreement between the association and the unit owner; or
47 (B) As part of a pattern of increases in assessment fees or a pattern of service
48 reductions or suspensions for the entire condominium; or
49 (2) For lawfully taking an action described in subparagraph (a)(2)(A) of this Code
50 section under circumstances where an enforcement action could have, but had not, been
51 pursued previously.
52 (d) In addition to any other remedies provided for by law, if an association engages in
53 retaliatory action against a unit owner in accordance with this Code section, such
54 retaliatory action shall be a defense for the unit owner to an action for foreclosure on a lien
55 for assessments filed by the association or its board. In such cases, the unit owner may
56 recover from the association a civil penalty in the amount of one month's assessment fees
57 plus $500.00, court costs, and reasonable attorney's fees, less any delinquent assessment
58 fees or other sums for which the unit owner is liable to the association, and declaratory
59 relief."
