Section 1:
25 LC 50 1141S
By: Representatives Gaines of the 120th, Jones of the 47th, Williamson of the 112th, Efstration
of the 104th, Kelley of the 16th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Code Section 48-5-34 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to ad
2 valorem property tax bill form, so as to require the property tax bill for certain school
3 districts that opt out of the state-wide base year homestead exemption to state the total
4 amount of the school district's reserve funds and the amount of such funds per enrolled
5 student; to require a statement on the property tax bill regarding certain political subdivisions
6 that do not have in effect certain homestead exemptions; to provide for related matters; to
7 provide for an effective date and applicability; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other
8 purposes.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
10 SECTION 1.
11 Code Section 48-5-34 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to ad valorem
12 property tax bill form, is amended by adding new subsections to read as follows:
13 "(c) With respect to each school district that elects to opt out of the state-wide base year
14 homestead exemption as provided for in Code Section 48-5-44.2 and that does not have in
15 effect a base year value or adjusted base year value homestead exemption that is generally
16 applicable to all residents or a homestead exemption that is generally applicable to all
H. B. 370 (SUB)
- 1 -
25 LC 50 1141S
17 seniors in the amount of the full assessed value of the homestead, the ad valorem property
18 tax bill shall state in bold print the total amount of the school district's reserve funds and the
19 amount of the school district's reserve funds per enrolled student as of January 1 of the tax
20 year.
21 (d) With respect to each governing authority of a county, consolidated government,
22 municipality, or school district that elects to opt out of the homestead exemption provided
23 for in Code Section 48-5-44.2 and that does not have in effect a base year value or adjusted
24 base year value homestead exemption that is generally applicable to all residents or a
25 homestead exemption that is generally applicable to all seniors in the amount of the full
26 assessed value of the homestead, the ad valorem property tax bill for the tax year for which
27 such opt out was effective and the following four years shall state in bold print:
28 '[Name of the political subdivision] chose to opt out of property tax relief for
29 homeowners by a vote of [name of the governing authority of the political subdivision]
30 on [the date of opt out vote]. If you have concerns about that decision, please call [the
31 main telephone number for the levying or recommending authority of the political
32 subdivision].'"
33 SECTION 2.
34 This Act shall become effective upon its approval by the Governor or upon its becoming law
35 without such approval, and shall be applicable to taxable years beginning on or after
36 January 1, 2025.
37 SECTION 3.
38 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
