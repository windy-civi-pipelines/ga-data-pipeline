25 LC 61 0192
By: Representative Carpenter of the 4th
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Code Section 20-3-411 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 definitions relative to tuition equalization grants at private colleges and universities, so as to
3 expand the definition of "approved school"; to provide for related matters; to repeal
4 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
6 SECTION 1.
7 Code Section 20-3-411 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to definitions
8 relative to tuition equalization grants at private colleges and universities, is amended by
9 revising division (2)(B)(i) as follows:
10 "(B)(i) A qualified proprietary institution of higher education located in this state
11 which is a baccalaureate degree-granting institution of higher education; which is
12 accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools; which is not a Bible
13 school or college (or, at the graduate level, a school or college of theology or
14 divinity); which admits as regular students only persons who have a high school
15 diploma, a state approved high school equivalency (HSE) diploma, or a degree from
16 an accredited postsecondary institution; whose students are eligible to participate in
H. B. 693

17 the federal Pell Grant program; which has been reviewed and approved for operation
18 and for receipt of tuition equalization grant funds by the Georgia Nonpublic
19 Postsecondary Education Commission; which is domiciled and incorporated in the
20 State of Georgia; which has been in existence in the State of Georgia for at least ten
21 years located in this state since on or before January 1, 2015; and which met all of the
22 requirements of this subparagraph by January 1, 2011 2015; provided, however, that
23 the criteria for approval for receipt of tuition equalization grant funds shall include but
24 not be limited to areas of course study,; quality of academic instruction,; student
25 placement rate,; research and library sources, resources; faculty,; support staff,;
26 financial resources,; physical plant facilities resources, instructional campuses,
27 facilities, or other physical sites; and support and equipment resources."
28 SECTION 2.
29 Said Code section is further amended by redesignating divisions (2)(B)(ii) and (2)(B)(iii) as
30 (2)(B)(iii) and (2)(B)(iv), respectively, and by adding a new division to subparagraph (B) of
31 paragraph (2) to read as follows:
32 "(ii) A qualified nonproprietary institution of higher education located in this state
33 which is a baccalaureate degree-granting institution of higher education offering a
34 baccalaureate degree program or programs in nursing; which is accredited by the
35 Higher Learning Commission; which is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate
36 Nursing Education; which is not a Bible school or college (or, at the graduate level,
37 a school or college of theology or divinity); which admits as regular students only
38 persons who have a high school diploma, a state approved high school equivalency
39 (HSE) diploma, or a degree from an accredited postsecondary institution; whose
40 students are eligible to participate in the federal Pell Grant program; which has been
41 reviewed and approved for operation and for receipt of tuition equalization grant
42 funds by the Georgia Nonpublic Postsecondary Education Commission; and which
H. B. 693

43 has at least one instructional campus that is physically located in the State of Georgia
44 for at least ten years as of January 1, 2025; provided, however, that only such
45 institution's baccalaureate degree program or programs in nursing shall be deemed an
46 approved school for the purposes of this subpart; and provided, further, that an
47 institution or program that is deemed an approved school pursuant to this division
48 shall not be deemed an eligible postsecondary institution as defined in Code Section
49 20-3-519 or for any purposes provided for in Part 7 of this article, relating to HOPE
50 scholarships and grants."
51 SECTION 3.
52 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
