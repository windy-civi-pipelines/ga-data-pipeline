25 LC 49 2009
By: Representatives Cox of the 28th, Rhodes of the 124th, Cannon of the 172nd, McClain of
the 109th, and Erwin of the 32nd
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Titles 20 and 39 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to education
2 and minors, respectively, so as to provide for high school student athletes and prospective
3 student athletes to receive name, image, or likeness compensation; to provide for the
4 automatic expiration of contracts that provide name, image, and likeness compensation to
5 student athletes after the student athlete graduates or is no longer enrolled; to provide for
6 application to certain athletic associations; to allow for professional representation of student
7 athletes and prospective student athletes; to provide for required written notice to high school
8 student athletes; to provide for definitions; to provide for construction; to provide a short
9 title; to provide for related matters; to provide for an effective date and applicability; to
10 repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
12 SECTION 1.
13 This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Georgia High School NIL Protection Act."

14 SECTION 2.
15 Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to education, is amended in
16 Chapter 2, relating to elementary and secondary education, by adding a new article to read
17 as follows:
18 "ARTICLE 32A
19 20-2-2105.
20 As used in this article, the term:
21 (1) 'Athletic association' means any association of schools or any other similar
22 organization which acts as an organizing, sanctioning, scheduling, or rule-making body
23 for interscholastic athletic activities in which public high schools in this state participate.
24 (2) 'Compensation' means anything of value and shall include, but shall not be limited
25 to, a promise, performance, or forbearance provided by a promisor in exchange for his,
26 her, or its promise.
27 (3) 'High school' means a public or private school in this state that enrolls students in any
28 of grades nine through 12.
29 (4) 'Interscholastic athletic activity' means interscholastic athletic events, competitions,
30 scrimmages, exhibitions, practices, and training sessions.
31 (5) 'NIL compensation' means compensation received for the use of a person's name,
32 image, or likeness.
33 (6) 'Parent' means a person who has legal authority to act on behalf of a minor child as
34 a natural or adoptive parent or as a legal guardian.
35 (7) 'Participating school' means a public or private high school in this state that is a
36 member of an athletic association or participates in interscholastic athletic activities
37 which are conducted under the authority or rules of an athletic association.

- 2 -

38 (8) 'Student athlete' means a student enrolled in a participating school who participates
39 in or is eligible to participate in any interscholastic athletic activity which is conducted
40 under the authority or rules of an athletic association. Such term shall include home
41 study students who participate in such interscholastic athletic activities as provided in
42 Code Section 20-2-319.6.
43 20-2-2105.1.
44 Nothing in this article shall be construed to abrogate, impair, or limit the authority of the
45 courts of this state or any department or agency of this state to enforce any provision of
46 federal or state law.
47 20-2-2105.2.
48 For purposes of this article, a scholarship from the participating school in which a student
49 athlete is enrolled that provides such student athlete with all or part of the cost of
50 enrollment and attendance at such participating school is not NIL compensation.
51 20-2-2105.3.
52 (a) Subject to the provisions of Code Sections 39-2-18 through 39-2-21, and except as
53 prohibited in this article, a student athlete shall be permitted to receive NIL compensation.
54 (b) No current or prospective student athlete shall be provided or shall receive NIL
55 compensation in exchange, in whole or in part, for attending, participating, or performing
56 at a particular participating school or a postsecondary educational institution as such term
57 is defined in Code Section 20-3-680.
58 (c) No participating school or entity whose purpose includes supporting or benefiting such
59 participating school or its interscholastic athletic activities or related programs or any
60 officer, director, employee, or agent of such participating school or entity shall provide a
61 current or prospective student athlete with NIL compensation.

- 3 -

62 (d) No student athlete who receives NIL compensation pursuant to this article shall:
63 (1) Wear a school based team jersey or otherwise display a school's name, mascot, logo,
64 or any other school identifying marks when engaging in any conduct or activity in
65 exchange for receiving NIL compensation;
66 (2) Make any reference to a participating school or an athletic association when engaging
67 in any conduct or activity in exchange for receiving NIL compensation; or
68 (3)(A) Except as provided in subparagraph (B) of this paragraph, endorse or promote
69 any good or service of any party that provides NIL compensation to such student athlete
70 or of any third-party partner of a party that provides NIL compensation to such student
71 athlete during any interscholastic athletic activity, including, but not limited to:
72 (i) Wearing apparel of such party or third-party partner;
73 (ii) Displaying a logo or brand of such party or third-party partner; and
74 (iii) Displaying an insignia or identifying mark of such party or third-party partner.
75 (B) The prohibitions provided for in subparagraph (A) of this paragraph shall not apply
76 to tryouts, practices, or training sessions.
77 (e) No student athlete shall receive NIL compensation for any conduct or activity that
78 reasonably can be perceived as endorsing or promoting:
79 (1) Alcohol products;
80 (2) Tobacco products, including, but not limited to, smokeless tobacco products;
81 (3) Vapor products;
82 (4) Cannabis products;
83 (5) Controlled substances;
84 (6) Prescription pharmaceuticals;
85 (7) Casinos and gambling, including sports betting, the lottery, and betting in connection
86 with video games, online games, and mobile devices;
87 (8) Adult entertainment or pornographic products and services;

88 (9) Any product or service, including, but not limited to, online services and products,
89 that are restricted for people under the age of 18 years; or
90 (10) Weapons, firearms, and ammunition.
91 20-2-2105.4.
92 Each participating school in this state shall advise each student athlete in writing at the
93 beginning of the season of each interscholastic athletic activity of the provisions of this
94 article, Article 13 of Chapter 3 of this title, and Code Sections 39-2-18 through 39-2-21 and
95 shall provide each student athlete with information concerning the effect of receiving
96 money or other things of value on the student athlete's future eligibility to participate in
97 intercollegiate athletics.
98 20-2-2105.5.
99 The provisions of this article shall not apply to intramural athletic programs or events at
100 such participating schools.
101 20-2-2105.6.
102 (a) No contract that provides for NIL compensation to a student athlete shall survive after
103 the earlier of:
104 (1) Ten calendar days after the date upon which such student athlete graduates from a
105 participating school; or
106 (2) Thirty calendar days after the date upon which such student athlete's enrollment in
107 a participating school terminates for any reason provided for by Georgia law; the policies,
108 rules, or regulations of the State Board of Education; or the policies, rules, or regulations
109 of a nonpublic participating school, including, but not limited to, withdrawal,
110 nonattendance, dropping out, nonresidency in the participating school's attendance zone,

111 nonresidency in this state, enrollment in a school that is not a participating school, and
112 expulsion.
113 (b) No contract that provides for NIL compensation to a student athlete shall include any
114 provision that does not comply with the provisions of subsection (a) of this Code section,
115 and any such provision shall be void and unenforceable.
116 20-2-2105.7.
117 (a) No athletic association shall prevent a student athlete from or penalize a student athlete
118 for receiving NIL compensation as provided for in this article.
119 (b) No athletic association shall prevent a participating school from participating in
120 interscholastic athletic activities, or otherwise penalize a participating school as a result of
121 a student athlete of such participating school receiving NIL compensation as provided for
122 in this article.
123 (c) No public school which receives funding under Article 6 of this chapter shall
124 participate in, sponsor, or provide coaching staff for interscholastic athletic activities which
125 are conducted under the authority of, conducted under the rules of, or scheduled by any
126 athletic association unless the charter, bylaws, or other governing documents of such
127 athletic association comply with this article.
128 20-2-2105.8.
129 (a) No participating school or athletic association shall prevent a student athlete from
130 obtaining professional representation in relation to contracts or legal matters, including, but
131 not limited to, representation provided by athlete agents who are certified as provided for
132 under Chapter 4A of Title 43 and legal representation provided by attorneys who are
133 licensed to practice law in this state.

135 Responsibility and Trust Act, established in 15 U.S.C. Section 7801, et seq., in their
