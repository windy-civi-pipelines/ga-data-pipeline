25 LC 52 0600
By: Representatives Barnes of the 86th, Hugley of the 141st, Oliver of the 84th, Au of the 50th,
Buckner of the 137th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 2A of Title 31 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to the
2 Department of Public Health, so as to provide for a three-year pilot program for a mobile
3 health clinic to provide postpartum care in certain counties; to provide for definitions; to
4 provide for reports; to provide for contingent effectiveness and automatic repeal; to provide
5 for related matters; to provide for a short title; to provide for an effective date; to repeal
6 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
8 SECTION 1.
9 This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Healthy Mothers Now Act."
10 SECTION 2.
11 Chapter 2A of Title 31 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to the Department
12 of Public Health, is amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
13 "31-2A-21.
14 (a) For purposes of this Code section, the term:
H. B. 149
- 1 -
25 LC 52 0600
15 (1) 'Limited maternity care county' means a county in this state that has fewer than two
16 hospitals or birth centers offering obstetric care or fewer than 60 obstetric providers per
17 10,000 births.
18 (2) 'Maternity care desert' means a county in this state that does not have a hospital or
19 birth center offering obstetric care or an obstetric provider.
20 (3) 'Obstetric provider' means a licensed physician or licensed advanced practice
21 registered nurse who practices in obstetrics and gynecology.
22 (4) 'Postpartum care' means healthcare for a woman for a period of one year following
23 a birth, miscarriage, stillbirth, or neonatal death. Such term includes physiological
24 assessments, mental health evaluations, nutritional evaluations, and guidance on personal
25 and newborn care. Such term includes at least four visits with an obstetric provider as
26 follows:
27 (A) An initial visit within 24 hours of a birth, miscarriage, stillbirth, or neonatal death;
28 (B) A follow-up visit within the first three weeks postpartum;
29 (C) A follow-up visit within the first eight weeks postpartum;
30 (D) A comprehensive visit no later than 12 weeks postpartum; and
31 (E) Intermediary and ongoing care as needed.
32 (b) The department shall conduct a three-year pilot program for the purpose of providing
33 postpartum care through mobile health clinics in limited maternity care counties and
34 maternity care deserts.
35 (c) No later than June 30, 2026, and annually for the duration of the pilot program, the
36 department shall submit a detailed written report on the implementation and effectiveness
37 of the pilot program to the Governor, the Speaker of the House of Representatives, the
38 President of the Senate, and the chairpersons of the House Committee on Public Health and
39 the Senate Health and Human Services Committee. The final report shall include
40 recommendations as to expansion of the pilot program state wide and changes to state law
41 or policy relative to such recommendations.
H. B. 149

42 (d) This Code section shall be contingent upon appropriations made by the General
43 Assembly specifically for the department for such purposes and shall stand repealed by
44 operation of law on June 30, 2029."
45 SECTION 3.
46 This Act shall become effective upon its approval by the Governor or upon its becoming law
47 without such approval.
48 SECTION 4.
49 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
