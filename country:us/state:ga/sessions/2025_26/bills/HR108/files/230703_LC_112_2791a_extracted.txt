25 LC 112 2791
House Resolution 108
the 160th, and LaHood of the 175th
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing February 4, 2025, as Dyslexia Day at the state capitol; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, dyslexia is a specific learning disability that is neurological in origin; and
3 WHEREAS, dyslexia is the most common learning disability, affecting approximately one
4 in five individuals and approximately 80 percent of all individuals with a learning disability;
5 and
6 WHEREAS, the condition is often unexpected because those it affects can have a very high
7 level of intelligence but read at a much lower level; and
8 WHEREAS, dyslexia causes difficulty with phoneme segmentation, the ability to break
9 words down into individual sounds; and
10 WHEREAS, an individual with dyslexia may have weaknesses in decoding and reading
11 fluency, but they are often surrounded by a sea of strengths in reasoning, problem solving,
12 concept formation, critical thinking, and vocabulary; and
H. R. 108

13 WHEREAS, early diagnosis is critical for early remediation and evidence-based intervention
14 that leads to self-awareness, self-empowerment, self-esteem, and necessary accommodations
15 to ensure student, school, and life success; and
16 WHEREAS, it is abundantly fitting and proper to draw attention to the profound educational
17 impact of dyslexia on the education and lives of countless children, adults, and students in
18 the State of Georgia.
19 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
20 the members of this body recognize February 4, 2025, as Dyslexia Day at the state capitol.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
22 and directed to make appropriate copies of this resolution available for distribution to the
