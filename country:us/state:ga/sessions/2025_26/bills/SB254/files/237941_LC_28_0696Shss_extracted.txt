25 LC 28 0696S
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 23 of Title 2 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, the "Georgia
2 Hemp Farming Act," so as to authorize licensed retail dealers in distilled spirits to obtain
3 retail consumable hemp establishment licenses; to authorize licensed retail dealers in distilled
4 spirits to sell or offer for sale consumable hemp products to consumers; to revise certain
5 definitions; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
7 SECTION 1.
8 Chapter 23 of Title 2 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, the "Georgia Hemp
9 Farming Act," is amended by revising Code Section 2-23-9.2, relating to consumable hemp
10 products, packaging, advertising, and distribution, as follows:
11 "2-23-9.2.
12 (a) No consumable hemp product shall be sold or otherwise distributed in this state unless
13 such product is packaged in a container that:
14 (1) Is not attractive to children;
15 (2) Does not bear any reasonable resemblance to any existing candy, snack, or other food
16 product that is widely distributed and familiar to the public;
S. B. 254 (SUB)
- 1 -

17 (3) Does not infringe on any trade dress, trademarks, branding, or other related materials
18 as described in Code Section 10-1-450 or in Chapter 22 of Title 15 of the United States
19 Code; and
20 (4) Is tamper evident and child resistant.
21 (b) No consumable hemp product shall be advertised in this state in any manner that:
22 (1) Is attractive to children;
23 (2) Bears a reasonable resemblance to any existing candy, snack, or other food product
24 that is widely distributed and familiar to the public;
25 (3) Infringes on any trade dress, trademarks, branding, or other related materials as
26 described in Code Section 10-1-450 or in Chapter 22 of Title 15 of the United States
27 Code; or
28 (4) Suggests that such product constitutes or contains low THC oil, as such term is
29 defined in Code Section 16-12-190, or otherwise constitutes or contains medical
30 marijuana or medical cannabis.
31 (c)(1) As used in this subsection Code section, the term:
32 (A) 'Food product' means any product intended to be consumed by humans for physical
33 subsistence; provided, however, that such term shall not include gummies, consumable
34 base oils, or products that constitute drinks or beverages.
35 (B) 'Gummy' means a gelatinous substance in the form of a cube, sphere, prismatoid,
36 ovoid, or other shape that is designed for human ingestion.
37 (2) No consumable hemp product shall be sold or otherwise distributed in this state if
38 such product constitutes or is a component of:
39 (A) A food product; or
40 (B) A drink or beverage that contains alcohol or constitutes an alcoholic beverage
41 under Title 3.

42 (3) Nothing in this subsection is intended to prohibit the sale or distribution of hemp that
43 is contained within gummies or consumable base oils, provided that such gummies or
44 consumable base oils are not a component of a food product.
45 (d) Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter, any provision of Title 3, or any rules
46 and regulations promulgated by the Commissioner of the Department of Revenue pursuant
47 to Chapter 4 of Title 3, a licensed retail dealer in distilled spirits may obtain a retail
48 consumable hemp establishment license and, upon obtaining such license, such licensed
49 retail dealer may sell or offer for sale consumable hemp products to consumers in
50 accordance with the provisions of this chapter and the rules and regulations promulgated
51 by the Commissioner pursuant to this chapter."
52 SECTION 2.
53 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
