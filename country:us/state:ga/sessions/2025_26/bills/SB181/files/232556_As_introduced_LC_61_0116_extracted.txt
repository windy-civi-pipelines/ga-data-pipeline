25 LC 61 0116
By: Senators Anavitarte of the 31st, Burns of the 23rd, Cowsert of the 46th, Gooch of the
51st, Hickman of the 4th and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 1 of Chapter 1 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to general provisions relative to education, so as to provide that public schools and
3 public postsecondary institutions shall not accept funding from or enter into contracts with
4 foreign adversaries; to provide definitions; to provide for related matters; to repeal
5 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
7 SECTION 1.
8 Article 1 of Chapter 1 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
9 general provisions relative to education, is amended by adding a new Code section to read
10 as follows:
11 "20-1-12.
12 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
13 (1) 'Foreign adversary' means:
14 (A) The People's Republic of China, including the Hong Kong Special Administrative
15 Region and the Macau Special Administrative Region (China);
16 (B) Republic of Cuba (Cuba);

17 (C) Islamic Republic of Iran (Iran);
18 (D) Democratic People's Republic of Korea (North Korea);
19 (E) Russian Federation (Russia);
20 (F) Venezuelan politician Nicolás Maduro (Maduro Regime);
21 (G) Republic of Turkey (Turkey); and
22 (H) Republic of Lebanon (Lebanon).
23 (2) 'Funding' means any gift, grant, donation, payment in exchange for services, capital
24 investment, salary, wage, compensation, fee, or other monetary exchange.
25 (3) 'Public postsecondary institution' means a school which is an institution of the
26 University System of Georgia or a unit of the Technical College System of Georgia.
27 (4) 'Public school' means any school under the control and management of a county,
28 independent, or area board of education supported by public funds; any school subject to
29 the provisions of Article 31 or 31A of Chapter 2 of this title supported by public funds;
30 or any school under the control and management of the State Board of Education or a
31 department or agency thereof supported by public funds.
32 (b) No public school, local education agency, or public postsecondary institution shall,
33 directly or indirectly, accept funding from or enter into a contract with a foreign adversary
34 or individuals or entities working on behalf of such foreign adversary."
35 SECTION 2.
36 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
S. B. 181
