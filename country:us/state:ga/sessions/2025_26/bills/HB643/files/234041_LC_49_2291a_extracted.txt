25 LC 49 2291
By: Representatives Townsend of the 179th, Hilton of the 48th, Ballard of the 147th, Rice of
the 139th, Dubnik of the 29th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 27 of Chapter 2 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to loitering at or disrupting schools, so as to provide for the local board of education
3 or other governing body of each public and private elementary and secondary school in this
4 state to install key boxes at each school building in locations determined in collaboration
5 with local law enforcement and emergency management agencies; to provide for the purpose,
6 content, and minimum standards of such key boxes; to provide for policies and regulation;
7 to provide for the creation and maintenance of records; to provide for immunity from civil
8 liability; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
10 SECTION 1.
11 Article 27 of Chapter 2 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
12 loitering at or disrupting schools, is amended by adding a new Code section to read as
13 follows:
14 "20-2-1186.
15 (a) The local board of education or other governing body of each public and private
16 elementary and secondary school in this state shall be authorized to provide for the
H. B. 643

17 installation at each school building of at least one exterior key box that meets or exceeds
18 Underwriters Laboratories Standard 1037.
19 (b) Each key box required by subsection (a) of this Code section shall:
20 (1) Be installed at an appropriate location for permitting local law enforcement officers
21 and emergency management personnel emergency access to such building, the precise
22 location of which shall be determined by such local board of education or other governing
23 body in collaboration with local law enforcement agencies and local emergency
24 management agencies of the jurisdiction in which such school building is located; and
25 (2) Contain:
26 (A) Such keys and other access control devices as are necessary to access each building
27 and room located on the entire school grounds; and
28 (B) Accurate maps of the entire school grounds that:
29 (i) Identify and label access points to each building's interior, including, but not
30 limited to, rooms, doors, stairwells, and hallways, each of which shall include any
31 identifiers or names utilized by staff and students;
32 (ii) Identify and label locations of critical utilities, key boxes, automated external
33 defibrillators, and trauma kits or other emergency response aids; and
34 (iii) Identify and label areas at or near each building, including parking areas, athletic
35 fields, surrounding roads, outbuildings, and neighboring properties.
36 (c)(1) The local board of education or other governing body of each public and private
37 elementary and secondary school in this state shall adopt such policies and procedures
38 as are necessary to implement the provisions of this Code section.
39 (2) Each local board of education or other governing body of each public and private
40 school that elects to provide for the installation of a key box as provided for in this Code
41 section shall establish key audit mechanisms and processes in collaboration with local
42 law enforcement officers and emergency management personnel to ensure the creation
43 and maintenance of a record of each instance such key box is accessed.
H. B. 643
- 2 -
25 LC 49 2291
44 (d) Information to be included with any key box required by subsection (a) of this Code
45 section shall be exempt from public disclosure to the extent provided in Code Section
46 50-18-72.
47 (e)(1) Local boards of education, other public and private school governing bodies, local
48 school systems, other public and private schools, and local governments and agencies
49 shall be immune from civil liability for any damages arising out of the installation and
50 use of key boxes required by subsection (a) of this Code section.
51 (2) Employees of local boards of education, other public and private school governing
52 bodies, local school systems, other public and private schools, and local governments and
53 agencies shall be immune from civil liability for any damages arising out of the
54 installation and use of key boxes required by subsection (a) of this Code section unless
55 it is shown that such employee acted with gross negligence or bad faith."
56 SECTION 2.
57 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
