Section 1:
25 LC 44 2863
By: Senators Summers of the 13th, Brass of the 6th, Goodman of the 8th, Watson of the 11th,
Anderson of the 24th and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Code Section 48-5-7.4 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 preferential assessment for bona fide conservation use property and bona fide residential
3 transitional property, so as to increase the maximum acreage to qualify for assessment and
4 taxation as a bona fide conservation use property; to provide for related matters; to provide
5 for a contingent effective date and automatic repeal; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other
6 purposes.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
8 SECTION 1.
9 Code Section 48-5-7.4 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to preferential
10 assessment for bona fide conservation use property and bona fide residential transitional
11 property, is amended by revising the introductory language of subsection (a), the introductory
12 language of paragraph (1) of subsection (a), subparagraphs (a)(1)(A) and (a)(1)(A.1), the
13 introductory language of paragraph (2) of subsection (a), paragraph (3) of subsection (b), and
14 subsections (e) and (r) as follows:
S. B. 43
- 1 -
25 LC 44 2863
15 "(a) For purposes of this article, the term 'bona fide conservation use property' means
16 property described in and meeting the requirements of paragraph (1) or (2) of this
17 subsection, as follows:
18 (1) Not more than 2,000 acres 6,000 acres of tangible real property of a single person,
19 the primary purpose of which is any good faith production, including but not limited to
20 subsistence farming or commercial production, from or on the land of agricultural
21 products or timber, subject to the following qualifications:
22 (A) Such property includes the value of tangible property permanently affixed to the
23 real property which is directly connected to such owner's production of agricultural
24 products or timber and which is devoted to the storage and processing of such
25 agricultural products or timber from or on such real property;
26 (A.1) In the application of the limitation contained in the introductory language of this
27 paragraph, the following rules shall apply to determine beneficial interests in bona fide
28 conservation use property held in a family owned farm entity as described in
29 division (1)(C)(iv) of this subsection:
30 (i) A person who owns an interest in a family owned farm entity as described in
31 division (1)(C)(iv) of this subsection shall be considered to own only the percent of
32 the bona fide conservation use property held by such family owned farm entity that
33 is equal to the percent interest owned by such person in such family owned farm
34 entity; and
35 (ii) A person who owns an interest in a family owned farm entity as described in
36 division (1)(C)(iv) of this subsection may elect to allocate the lesser of any unused
37 portion of such person's 2,000 acre 6,000 acre limitation or the product of such
38 person's percent interest in the family owned farm entity times the total number of
39 acres owned by the family owned farm entity subject to such bona fide conservation
40 use assessment, with the result that the family owned farm entity may receive bona
41 fide conservation use assessment on more than 2,000 acres 6,000 acres;"
S. B. 43
- 2 -
25 LC 44 2863
42 "(2) Not more than 2,000 acres 6,000 acres of tangible real property, excluding the value
43 of any improvements thereon, of a single owner of the types of environmentally sensitive
44 property specified in this paragraph and certified as such by the Department of Natural
45 Resources, if the primary use of such property is its maintenance in its natural condition
46 or controlling or abating pollution of surface or ground waters of this state by storm-water
47 runoff or otherwise enhancing the water quality of surface or ground waters of this state
48 and if such owner meets the qualifications of subparagraph (C) of paragraph (1) of this
49 subsection, subject to the following qualifications:"
50 "(3) No property shall qualify as bona fide conservation use property if such current use
51 assessment would result in any person who has a beneficial interest in such property,
52 including any interest in the nature of stock ownership, receiving in any tax year any
53 benefit of current use assessment as to more than 2,000 acres 6,000 acres. If any taxpayer
54 has any beneficial interest in more than 2,000 acres 6,000 acres of tangible real property
55 which is devoted to bona fide conservation uses, such taxpayer shall apply for current use
56 assessment only as to 2,000 acres 6,000 acres of such land;"
57 "(e) A single owner shall be authorized to enter into more than one covenant under this
58 Code section for bona fide conservation use property, provided that the aggregate number
59 of acres of qualified property of such owner to be entered into such covenants does not
60 exceed 2,000 acres 6,000 acres. Any such qualified property may include a tract or tracts
61 of land which are located in more than one county. A single owner shall be authorized to
62 enter qualified property in a covenant for bona fide conservation use purposes and to enter
63 simultaneously the residence located on such property in a covenant for bona fide
64 residential transitional use if the qualifications for each such covenant are met. A single
65 owner shall be authorized to enter qualified property in a covenant for bona fide
66 conservation use purposes and to enter other qualified property of such owner in a covenant
67 for bona fide residential transitional use."
S. B. 43
- 3 -
25 LC 44 2863
68 "(r) Property which is subject to current use assessment under this Code section shall be
69 separately classified from all other property on the tax digest; and such separate
70 classification shall be such as will enable any person examining the tax digest to ascertain
71 readily that the property is subject to current use assessment under this Code section.
72 Covenants shall be public records and shall be indexed and maintained in such manner as
73 will allow members of the public to locate readily the covenant affecting any particular
74 property subject to current use assessment under this Code section. Based on information
75 submitted by the county boards of tax assessors, the commissioner shall maintain a central
76 registry of conservation use property, indexed by owners, so as to ensure that the 2,000
77 acre 6,000 acre limitations of this Code section are complied with on a state-wide basis."
78 SECTION 2.
79 This Act shall become effective on January 1, 2027, only if an amendment to the
80 Constitution increasing the maximum acreage to qualify for assessment and taxation as a
81 bona fide conservation use property is ratified by the voters at the November, 2026,
82 state-wide general election. If such an amendment is not so ratified, then this Act shall not
83 become effective and shall stand repealed on January 1, 2027.
84 SECTION 3.
85 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
