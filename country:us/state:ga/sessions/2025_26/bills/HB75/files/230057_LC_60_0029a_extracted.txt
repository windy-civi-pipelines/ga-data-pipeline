25 LC 60 0029
By: Representatives Ridley of the 22nd, Ridley of the 6th, Horner of the 3rd, Tarvin of the 2nd,
Chastain of the 7th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 1 of Chapter 11 of Title 4 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to general provisions relative to animal protection, so as to revise certain provisions
3 relating to the impounding of domesticated animals by a government agency and the care of
4 such impounded domesticated animals; to require a court order prior to the euthanasia of any
5 impounded domesticated animal; to provide for state or local liability in cases of the death
6 of, loss of, or injury to an impounded domesticated animal under certain circumstances; to
7 provide for notice and hearings; to provide a definition; to provide for a short title; to provide
8 for an effective date and applicability; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting
9 laws; and for other purposes.
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
11 SECTION 1.
12 This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Justice for Peanut and Fred Act."
H. B. 75
- 1 -
25 LC 60 0029
13 SECTION 2.
14 Article 1 of Chapter 11 of Title 4 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
15 general provisions relative to animal protection, is amended by adding a new paragraph to
16 Code Section 4-11-2, relating to definitions, to read as follows:
17 "(2.1) 'Domesticated animal' means a dog, cat, bird, fish, reptile, or other animal
18 customarily kept as a pet in this state and maintained in or near to the household of its
19 owner or an animal lawfully taken or possessed after procuring all licenses, stamps, tags,
20 or permits required under Title 27 and maintained in or near to the household of its
21 owner. Such term does not include livestock as defined in Code Section 4-3-2."
22 SECTION 3.
23 Said article is further amended by revising Code Section 4-11-9.3, relating to caring for an
24 impounded animal, as follows:
25 "4-11-9.3.
26 (a) It shall be the duty of any person impounding an animal under this article or Code
27 Section 4-8-24, 4-11-9.2, 16-12-4, or 16-12-37 to make reasonable and proper
28 arrangements to provide the impounded animal with humane care and adequate and
29 necessary veterinary services. Such arrangements may include, but shall not be limited to,
30 providing shelter and care for the animal at any state, federal, county, municipal, or
31 governmental facility or shelter; contracting with a private individual, partnership,
32 corporation, association, or other entity to provide humane care and adequate and necessary
33 veterinary services for a reasonable fee; or allowing a private individual, partnership,
34 corporation, association, or other entity to provide humane care and adequate and necessary
35 veterinary services as a volunteer and at no cost.
36 (b) Any person impounding an animal under this article or Code Section 4-8-24, 16-12-4,
37 or 16-12-37 or providing care for an impounded animal shall have a lien on such animal
38 for the reasonable costs of caring for such animal. Such lien may be foreclosed in any

39 court of competent jurisdiction to hear civil cases. Liens shall be foreclosed in magistrate
40 courts only when the amount of the lien does not exceed the jurisdictional limits
41 established by law for such courts.
42 (c) Any person impounding an animal under this article shall be authorized to return such
43 animal to its owner, upon payment by the owner of all costs of impoundment and care and
44 upon the entry of a consent order, unless such owner has been convicted of, pled guilty to,
45 or pled nolo contendere to animal cruelty or dog fighting under any local, state, or federal
46 law, regulation, or ordinance, or in a prior administrative or legal action in this state or any
47 other state, was found to have failed to provide humane care to an animal, committed
48 cruelty to animals, or committed an act prohibited under Code Section 16-12-37 in
49 violation of the laws of this state or of the United States or any of the several states. Such
50 consent order shall provide conditions relating to the care and treatment of such animal,
51 including, but not limited to, the following, that:
52 (1) Such animal shall be given humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary
53 services;
54 (2) Such animal shall not be subjected to cruelty; and
55 (3) The owner shall comply with this article.
56 (d)(1) The provisions of subsection (c) of this Code section shall not apply to an animal
57 that was an object or instrumentality of a crime. Any government agency impounding
58 an animal as a result of a violation of this article or Code Section 4-11-9.2, 16-12-4,
59 or 16-12-37 shall not return such animal to its owner.
60 (2) Any government agency having custody of an animal that was seized as an object or
61 instrumentality of a crime may, with the consent of the prosecuting attorney, apply to the
62 court having jurisdiction over the offense for an order authorizing such agency to dispose
63 of the animal prior to trial of the criminal case as provided by law.
64 (e) Any government agency in custody of a domesticated animal impounded pursuant to
65 this article or Code Section 4-8-24 shall be subject to liability for up to $5,000.00 for any

66 failure of its officers or employees to exercise due care in the execution of their official
67 duties under subsection (a) of this Code section that results in the death of, loss of, or any
68 injury to such domesticated animal. This subsection shall be a limited waiver of the
69 defense of sovereign immunity of a state or local government."
70 SECTION 4.
71 Said article is further amended by revising Code Section 4-11-9.6, relating to disposal of
72 impounded animal, as follows:
73 "4-11-9.6.
74 (a)(1) The government agency having custody of an animal impounded pursuant to this
75 article which is not returned to the owner as provided in Code Sections 4-11-9.3
76 and 4-11-9.5, or when ownership of the animal is relinquished by the owner for which
77 the owner has relinquished ownership, may dispose of the animal through sale by any
78 commercially feasible means, at a public auction or by sealed bids, adoption, or, if in the
79 opinion of a licensed accredited veterinarian or a veterinarian employed by a state or
80 federal government and approved by the Commissioner such animal has a temperament
81 or condition such that euthanasia is the only reasonable course of action, by humanely
82 disposing of euthanizing the animal, subject to paragraph (2) of this subsection.
83 (2) Except as otherwise provided in Code Sections 4-8-25, 4-8-26, and 4-8-30, in cases
84 involving an impounded domesticated animal for which the owner has not relinquished
85 ownership, the government agency may humanely euthanize such domesticated animal
86 only if a judge of any court of competent jurisdiction within this state finds, after notice
87 and opportunity for hearing as provided by subsection (b) of this Code section, that such
88 domesticated animal has a temperament or condition such that euthanasia is the only
89 reasonable course of action and enters an order for the euthanasia of such domesticated
90 animal.

- 4 -

91 (b)(1) It shall be the duty of the government agency to immediately notify the owner of
92 any veterinarian opinion to euthanize a domesticated animal as provided in paragraph (1)
93 of subsection (a) of this Code section. Such notice shall include a summary of the
94 veterinarian's findings and a statement of the time limits for the owner to respond and
95 request a hearing as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection. Such notice shall be
96 provided by personal service or by registered mail, certified mail, or statutory overnight
97 delivery sent to the last known address of the owner. If the owner of the domesticated
98 animal is unknown or cannot be found, service of the notice of euthanasia on such owner
99 shall be made by posting the notice in a conspicuous place at the location where the
100 domesticated animal was impounded and by publishing a notice once in a newspaper of
101 general circulation in the county where the domesticated animal was impounded.
102 (2) An owner may request, in writing, a hearing within ten business days of the date the
103 notice of euthanasia was served, or, if the owner could not be served, such owner may
104 request, in writing, a hearing within 30 days of the date of publication of the notice of
105 euthanasia. Such request for hearing shall be served upon the government agency having
106 custody of the domesticated animal. If no hearing is requested within the time limits
107 specified in this paragraph, such domesticated animal may be humanely euthanized in
108 accordance with paragraph (2) of subsection (a) of this Code section.
109 (3) The scope of the hearing shall be limited to whether euthanasia of the domesticated
110 animal is the only reasonable course of action.
111 (4) The hearing shall be conducted in accordance with the hearing procedures set forth
112 in subsection (b) of Code Section 4-11-9.5.
113 (5) Within five business days after such hearing, the hearing officer shall forward a copy
114 of the decision to the owner. If such decision is that euthanasia of the domesticated
115 animal is the only reasonable course of action, the hearing officer shall include a notice
116 of the date by which the euthanasia shall occur with a copy of the decision.

117 (6) If the hearing officer finds that euthanasia of the domesticated animal is not the only
118 reasonable course of action, the hearing officer shall recommend that the government
119 agency having custody of the domesticated animal dispose of such domesticated animal
120 in any manner provided for in paragraph (1) of subsection (a) of this Code section except
121 euthanasia.
122 (c)(1) Any proceeds from the sale of such an animal pursuant to paragraph (1) of
123 subsection (a) of this Code section shall be used first to pay the costs associated with the
124 impoundment, including, but not limited to, removal of the animal from the premises,
125 shelter and care of the animal, notice, hearing, and disposition of the animal. Any funds
126 remaining shall:
127 (1)(A) If the owner is unknown or cannot be found, be paid into the state treasury if the
128 animal was impounded by the Commissioner or his or her designated agent or into the
129 treasury of the local government if the animal was impounded by the sheriff, a deputy
130 sheriff, another law enforcement officer, or an animal control officer; or
131 (2)(B) If the owner is known, be paid to the owner.
132 (c)(2) The government agency responsible for conducting the sale shall keep a record of
133 all sales, disbursements, and distributions made under this article."
134 SECTION 5.
135 (a) This Act shall become effective upon its approval by the Governor or upon its becoming
136 law without such approval.
137 (b) This Act shall apply to any impounding of animals and related causes of action occurring
138 on or after the effective date of this Act.
139 SECTION 6.
140 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
