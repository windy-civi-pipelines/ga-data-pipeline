25 LC 112 2710
House Resolution 45
79th, Kelley of the 16th, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing the 51st anniversary of the Consulate-General of Japan in Atlanta; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, the Consulate-General of Japan in Atlanta has strengthened the cultural,
4 political, and economic ties between the Southeast United States and Japan and has worked
5 to help and support Japanese citizens and companies in the Southeast; and
6 WHEREAS, a long and steady friendship has grown between Georgia and Japan, as shown
7 by the establishment of the Office of the State of Georgia in Japan 51 years ago; and
8 WHEREAS, Georgia has become a model for welcoming Japanese companies to the
9 Southeast, and the region has grown to become an indispensable part of the United
10 States-Japan relationship; and
11 WHEREAS, the Japanese community has thrived together with all other communities to
12 make Georgia greater; and
H. R. 45
- 1 -
25 LC 112 2710
13 WHEREAS, current trade between Georgia and Japan is $7.65 billion in import and export
14 trade; and
15 WHEREAS, there are 514 Japan affiliated offices (not including retail locations) now located
16 in Georgia, employing 38,290 people; and
17 WHEREAS, the consulate-general created Japan Fest in 1986 and helped grow it into the
18 largest Japanese cultural festival in the Southeast; and
19 WHEREAS, the 2020 Summer Olympics were held in Tokyo, Japan, in 2021 due to
20 COVID-19; and
21 WHEREAS, Americus, Atlanta, Augusta, Columbus, Dublin, Gainesville, Macon, and Rome
22 have sister-city relationships with cities in Japan, making Georgia the state with the highest
23 number of Japanese sister cities in the Southeast; the State of Georgia has a sister-state
24 relationship with the Kagoshima Prefecture; and the Port of Savannah has a sister-port
25 relationship with the Port of Shimizu; and
26 WHEREAS, nearly 1,000 Georgia public school students are studying the Japanese language,
27 13 Georgia institutes of higher education have Japanese language programs, and since 1987,
28 573 Georgians have participated in Japan's Japanese Exchange and Teaching (JET) program;
29 and
30 WHEREAS, more than 25 members of the Georgia General Assembly and other Georgia
31 officials were hosted in the autumn of 2019 on trips to Japan to meet with Japanese
H. R. 45
- 2 -
25 LC 112 2710
32 government, business, and cultural officials in order to promote a deeper understanding and
33 to cement ties between Japan and Georgia.
34 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
35 the members of this body recognize the 51st anniversary of the Consulate-General of Japan
36 in Atlanta and the long and steady friendship between this state and Japan and commend the
37 efforts of the Consulate-General to strengthen cultural, educational, political, and economic
38 ties between Georgia and Japan.
39 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
40 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the
41 Consulate-General of Japan in Atlanta.
