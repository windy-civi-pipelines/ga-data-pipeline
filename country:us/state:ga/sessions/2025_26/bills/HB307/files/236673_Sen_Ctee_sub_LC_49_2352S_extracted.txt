A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to education, so as to
2 include provisions for students significantly at risk of not achieving grade level reading
3 proficiency or with characteristics of dyslexia; to provide for support plans for such students;
4 to provide for notice to parents and guardians of such students; to require the Department of
5 Education to publish and distribute information relevant to dyslexia and students with
6 characteristics of dyslexia; to require local school systems and public schools to distribute
7 information relevant to dyslexia and students with characteristics of dyslexia; to provide for
8 reporting; to prohibit the three-cueing systems model from being featured or promoted in
9 high-quality instructional materials or structured literacy for students in kindergarten through
10 third grade; to prohibit public schools from employing curricula, instructional materials,
11 instructional practices, and other interventions that utilize the three-cueing systems model;
12 to repeal a provision relating to reading recovery programs; to provide for written
13 notification to parents and guardians; to repeal certain provisions setting deadlines applicable
14 to the State Board of Education and the Department of Education that have expired; to
15 provide for ongoing review and updating of required and authorized actions; to require
16 additional reports by the Department of Education; to revise a short title; to repeal Code
17 Section 20-2-159.6, relating to screening for dyslexia and related disorders, training and
18 professional development, pilot program evaluating early intervention, and data reporting;

20 Student Achievement; to provide for duties and responsibilities for such council; to expand
21 the purposes of the Georgia Council on Literacy to improving literacy outcomes for Georgia
22 adults as well as students; to revise provisions for compensation of members of the Georgia
23 Council on Literacy; to extend the automatic repealer on the Georgia Council on Literacy
24 from 2026 to 2030; to provide for and revise definitions; to make conforming changes; to

47 Said title is further amended in said part by revising Code Section 20-2-153.1, relating to

51 "20-2-153.1.
52 (a) This Code section shall be known and may be cited as the 'Georgia Early Literacy and
53 Dyslexia Act.'
54 (b) As used in this Code section, the term:
55 (1) 'Board' means the State Board of Education.
56 (1.1)(2) 'Center' means the Sandra Dunagan Deal Center for Early Language and
57 Literacy.
58 (3) 'Characteristics of dyslexia' means persistent weaknesses in one or more areas of
59 foundational reading skills and inadequate response to targeted intervention that indicates
60 a need for more intensive intervention.
61 (1.2)(4) 'Council' means the Georgia Council on Literacy.
62 (2)(5) 'Department' means the Department of Education.
63 (3)(6) 'Dyslexia' means a specific learning disability that is neurological in origin.
64 Dyslexia is characterized by difficulties with accurate or fluent word recognition and by
65 poor spelling and decoding abilities. These difficulties typically result from a deficit in
66 the phonological component of language that is often unexpected in relation to other
67 cognitive abilities and the provision of effective classroom instruction. Secondary
- 3 -
25 LC 49 2352S
68 consequences may include problems in reading comprehension and reduced reading
69 experience that can impede the growth of vocabulary and background knowledge.
70 (7) 'Foundational literacy skills' means phonological awareness, phonemic awareness,
71 alphabet knowledge, phonics, oral reading fluency, spelling, vocabulary, reading
72 comprehension, spelling, oral language, and the intersection of reading and writing rapid
73 automatized naming.
74 (4)(8) 'High-quality instructional materials' means instructional materials aligned to the
75 science of reading that instruct students in foundational literacy skills and
76 grade-appropriate English language arts and reading standards approved by the State
77 Board of Education board. Instructional materials that feature or promote the use of the
78 three-cueing system shall not constitute high-quality instructional materials.
79 (9) 'Parent' or 'guardian' means an individual who has legal authority to act on behalf of
80 a child as a natural or adoptive parent, a legal guardian, or a legal agent. Such term shall
81 also include a kinship caregiver, as such term is defined in Code Section 20-1-15.
82 (5)(10) 'Reading intervention' means evidence based strategies frequently used to
83 remediate reading deficiencies and includes, but is not limited to, individual and
84 small-group instruction, multisensory approaches, tutoring, mentoring, or the use of
85 technology that targets specific reading skills and abilities.
86 (6)(11) 'Science of reading' means the body of research that identifies evidence based
87 approaches of explicitly and systematically teaching students to read, including
88 foundational literacy skills that enable students to develop reading skills required to meet
89 state standards in literacy.
90 (7)(12) 'Significantly at risk of not attaining grade level reading proficiency' 'Significant
91 reading deficiency' means for students in kindergarten through third grade that such
92 student's score on a universal reading screener is within the range of scores determined
93 by the department to demonstrate a lack of proficiency in foundational literacy skills
94 foundational literacy skills, as measured by scores on a universal reading screener and

95 other quantitative and qualitative data, indicate that the student is at high risk of not
96 attaining grade level reading proficiency.
97 (8)(13) 'Structured literacy' means an evidence based approach to teaching oral and
98 written language aligned to the science of reading founded on the science of how children
99 learn to read and characterized by explicit, systematic, cumulative, and diagnostic
100 instruction in phonology, sound-symbol association, syllable instruction, morphology,
101 syntax, and semantics. An approach to teaching oral and written language that features
102 or promotes the three-cueing system shall not constitute structured literacy.
103 (14) 'Three-cueing system' means any model for teaching students word reading based
104 on meaning, structure and syntax, and visual cues. Such models are also referred to as
105 'MSV.'
106 (9)(15) 'Tiered reading intervention plan' means a plan that describes the evidence based
107 reading intervention services a student will receive to remediate such student's reading
108 deficit and to ensure that such student becomes proficient support the student in reaching
109 grade level proficiency in foundational literacy skills.
110 (10)(16) 'Universal reading screener' means a uniform tool that screens and monitors a
111 student's progress in foundational literacy skills that is administered to students multiple
112 times during the school year.
