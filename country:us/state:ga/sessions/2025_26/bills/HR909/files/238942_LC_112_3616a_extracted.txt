25 LC 112 3616
House Resolution 909
By: Representative Gilliard of the 162nd
A RESOLUTION
2 WHEREAS, the State of Georgia mourns the loss of one of its most distinguished citizens
3 with the passing of Reverend Franklin D. Blackshear; and
4 WHEREAS, Reverend Franklin D. Blackshear was born to Reverend William and Mrs.
5 Bessie Blackshear as the youngest of ten siblings and was reared in the Cuyler-Brownsville
6 neighborhood of Savannah, Georgia, where service to others was instilled in him at an early
7 age; and
8 WHEREAS, Reverend Blackshear began his educational career as a mathematics teacher,
9 and in 1965, he became a guidance counselor at A. E. Beach High School, where he served
10 for 24 years until his retirement in 1989; and
11 WHEREAS, he was baptized, licensed, and ordained at First Jerusalem Baptist Church,
12 where he remained a lifelong member, and he was installed as pastor of Mt. Tabor Baptist
13 Church in 1981, where he served faithfully for 43 years alongside his wife, Lady Ella
14 Blackshear, and retired as pastor emeritus in May 2024; and
H. R. 909
- 1 -
25 LC 112 3616
15 WHEREAS, he was a member of the Savannah Baptist Ministers Union, a recipient of the
16 2024 Community Service Award of Appreciation from Second African Baptist Church, and
17 a highly respected mentor, educator, and spiritual leader throughout the Savannah
18 community; and
19 WHEREAS, he gave inspiration to many through his high ideals, morals, and deep concern
20 for his fellow citizens, and the devotion, patience, and understanding he demonstrated to his
21 family and friends were admired by others; and
22 WHEREAS, a compassionate and generous man who demonstrated his commitment to
23 witnessing Christ through word and deed, Reverend Franklin D. Blackshear will long be
24 remembered for his love of family, friendship, and community and will be missed by all who
25 had the great fortune of knowing him.
26 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
27 the members of this body join in honoring the life and memory of Reverend Franklin D.
28 Blackshear and express their deepest and most sincere regret at his passing.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
30 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the
31 family of Reverend Franklin D. Blackshear.
H. R. 909
