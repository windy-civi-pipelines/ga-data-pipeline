A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 7 of Title 48 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 income taxes, so as to exempt from taxation certain income received as retirement benefits
3 derived from service in the armed forces of the United States or the reserve components
4 thereof; to revise a tax credit for contributions to law enforcement foundations; to expand the
5 number of foundations that qualify; to expand the qualified expenditures; to increase the
6 annual amount of contributions allowed; to reduce a cap for certain entities; to extend the
7 sunset date; to remove the carry forward authorization; to provide for procedures; to provide
8 for related matters; to provide for effective dates and applicability; to repeal conflicting laws;
9 and for other purposes.
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
11 SECTION 1.
12 Chapter 7 of Title 48 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to income taxes,
13 is amended in Code Section 48-7-27, relating to computation of taxable net income, by
14 revising paragraph (5.1) of subsection (a) as follows:
15 "(5.1)(A) Up to $17,500.00 of income Income received by an individual who is less than
16 62 65 years of age paid to such individual as retirement benefits from military service in

17 the armed forces of the United States or the reserve components thereof and an additional
18 amount of up to $17,500.00 of such income, provided that he or she has Georgia earned
19 income otherwise included in his or her Georgia taxable net income in an amount that
20 exceeds $17,500.00 in an amount that shall not exceed $65,000.00.
21 (B) In the case of a married couple filing jointly, each spouse who is otherwise
22 qualified for an exclusion allowed by this paragraph shall be individually entitled to
23 exclude retirement income received by that spouse up to the exclusion amount.
24 (C) The exclusions provided for in this paragraph shall not apply to or affect and shall
25 be in addition to those adjustments to net income provided for under any other
26 paragraph of this subsection; provided, however, that no individual shall be allowed an

28 paragraph (5) of this subsection;"

33 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
34 (1) 'Law enforcement foundation' means any domestic nonprofit corporation with the
35 sole function of supporting one local law enforcement unit through a formal relationship
36 recognized by such local law enforcement unit with one or more local law enforcement
37 units or which provides support to law enforcement state wide and which maintains
38 nonprofit status under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code and tax exempt
39 status under Code Section 48-7-25.

46 state law enforcement division or department, railroad police, or any unit appointed under
47 the authority of Chapter 9 of Title 35 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated.
48 (3) 'Qualified contributions' means the preapproved contribution of funds by a taxpayer
49 to a qualified law enforcement foundation under the terms and conditions of this Code
50 section.
51 (4) 'Qualified expenditures' means expenditures made by a qualified law enforcement
52 foundation:
53 (A)(i) For salary supplements paid no more than twice annually or training provided
54 directly to law enforcement officers and other employees employed by the any local
55 law enforcement unit affiliated with such qualified law enforcement foundation; or
56 (ii) For the purchase, lease, maintenance, or improvement of equipment to be used
57 by such officers or employees;
58 (iii) For the purchase or lease of supplies and materials for technology updates,
59 including computer hardware and software; or
60 (iv) For the lease of facilities or purchase of goods or services to be used for the
61 promotion of community engagement; or
62 (B) To cover any costs incurred by the any local law enforcement unit affiliated with
63 such law enforcement foundation for the operation of an emergency response team that
64 combines law enforcement officers and behavioral health specialists, provided that such
65 costs shall not include salaries or other regular compensation.

67 has been designated as the sole local law enforcement foundation for a single local law
