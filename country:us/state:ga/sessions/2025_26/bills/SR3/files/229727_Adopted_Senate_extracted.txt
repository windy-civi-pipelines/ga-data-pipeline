Senate Resolution 3
ADOPTED SENATE
A RESOLUTION
1 Adopting the Rules of the Senate; and for other purposes.
2 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the Rules of the Senate in effect at the
3 adjournment of the 2024 Regular Session of the General Assembly are hereby adopted as the
4 Rules of the Senate for the 2025-2026 biennium of the General Assembly unless and until
5 otherwise provided by resolution of the Senate, with the following amendments:
6 SECTION 1.
7 The Rules of the Senate are amended by revising subsection (b) of Rule 1-3.2, relating to
8 travel and reimbursement for employees of the Senate, as follows:
9 "(b) Prior to any disbursement by the Legislative Fiscal Officer, there shall be on file with
10 said fiscal officer documentation of the approval by the Committee on Administrative
11 Affairs of all out-of-state travel reimbursement requests, submitted by the Secretary of the
12 Senate, the Senator to which such staff member is assigned, or the directors of the Senate
13 Research Office of Policy and Legislative Analysis, Press Office, or Budget and Evaluation
14 offices Office, as applicable, and the person requesting reimbursement shall submit an
15 itemized listing of all expenses claimed hereunder."
S. R. 3
- 1 -
25 LC 33 9754
16 SECTION 2.
17 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by revising Rule 2-1.2 as follows:
18 "2-1.2 Committee on Administrative Affairs
19 There shall be a Committee on Administrative Affairs composed of the President of the
20 Senate, the President Pro Tempore, the Majority Leader, the Minority Leader, the Secretary
21 of the Senate, and three members appointed by the President of the Senate. This committee
22 shall have the responsibility of employing, supervising, disciplining, and setting the
23 compensation of all aides, secretaries, and other personnel for the Senate, including the
24 Senate Budget and Evaluation Office, Senate Press Office, and Senate Research offices
25 Office of Policy and Legislative Analysis. The committee shall supervise the purchase and
26 allotment of supplies for the Senate. This committee shall also supervise and approve all
27 out-of-state travel of members of the Senate and staff. The committee shall be chaired by
28 the President Pro Tempore or his or her designee. The committee is authorized to meet and
29 take official action after adjournment sine die of a session and prior to the convening of the
30 next session."
31 SECTION 3.
32 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by adding a new rule as follows:
33 "2-1.14 Interim Special Committees
34 (a) Any interim special committee created by the Senate shall be subject to a resolution
35 creating such committee. The Senate may also authorize the President of the Senate to
36 create such committees.
37 (b) Without the approval of the President of the Senate or subject to a resolution adopted
38 pursuant to paragraph (a), no interim special committee may hold more than five (5)
39 meetings. The members of any such committee shall receive the allowances provided for
40 in Code Section 28-1-8 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, provided that such
41 allowances shall not be received by any member of the committee for more than five (5)
S. R. 3
- 2 -
25 LC 33 9754
42 days of service unless additional days of service are authorized by the President of the
43 Senate or resolution.
44 (c) The chairperson shall call all meetings of any interim special committee. The
45 chairperson shall provide notice at least seventy-two (72) hours prior to a meeting of the
46 committee. Such notice shall be directed to the committee members; the Secretary of the
47 Senate, who shall post notice of the meeting on the General Assembly's website as soon
48 as practicable following receipt; and additional individuals at the discretion of the chair.
49 The committee may conduct such meetings at such places and at such times as it may deem
50 necessary or convenient to enable it to exercise fully and effectively its powers, perform
51 its duties, and accomplish the objectives and purposes set forth in the resolution creating
52 the committee; provided that should a chairperson desire to hold a meeting of the
53 committee in a location other than the State Capitol or the Coverdell Legislative Office
54 Building, he or she must obtain prior written approval from the Committee on
55 Administrative Affairs.
56 (d) Each interim special committee at its first organizational meeting shall set the number
57 of members required for a quorum; however, the quorum shall not be set at less than a
58 majority of the membership of the committee. An interim special committee cannot
59 circumvent the provisions of the Senate Rules governing such committees by suspending
60 any Rule or part thereof. Interim special committees may establish rules of operation that
61 are not in conflict with Senate Rules.
62 (e) In the event an interim special committee adopts any specific findings or
63 recommendations that include suggestions for proposed legislation, the chairperson shall
64 file a report of the same prior to the date of abolishment set forth in the resolution creating
65 the committee. In the event the committee adopts a report that does not include
66 suggestions for proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file the report. No report shall
67 be filed unless the same has been adopted prior to the date of the committee's abolishment
68 by majority vote of a quorum of the committee. A report so approved shall be signed by
S. R. 3

25 LC 33 9754
69 the chairperson of the committee and filed with the Secretary of the Senate. In the absence
70 of an approved report, the chairperson may file with the Secretary of the Senate a copy of
71 the minutes of the meetings of the committee in lieu thereof."
72 SECTION 4.
73 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by revising Rule 2-5.1 as follows:
74 "2-5.1 Motions; How Made
75 All motions in standing committees shall receive a second before being put by the
76 presiding officer."
77 SECTION 5.
78 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by revising Rule 4-2.10 as follows:
79 "4-2.10 Bills Placed on Desk; Calendar Changes
80 No general bill or resolution shall be put for final passage unless the same has been put on
81 a calendar and placed on each Senator's desk no less than one hour prior to the time of
82 convening on the date of passage. The Secretary of the Senate shall put all bills which
83 appear on the prepared calendar and which may be considered that day in numerical order
84 on the Senators' desks. The calendar can be changed by a vote of two-thirds of the
85 Senators voting, provided such two-thirds constitutes a majority of the members elected
86 to the Senate."
87 SECTION 6.
88 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by revising Rule 9-1.1 as follows:
89 "9-1.1 Senatorial Conduct
90 When the Senate is in session, Senators shall conduct themselves at all times with dignity
91 and in a manner to insure ensure decorum in the deliberations of the body and shall be

92 called to order by the President for activities to the contrary, including unnecessary
93 conversation which is disruptive to the business of the session and inappropriate dress."
94 SECTION 7.
95 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by revising paragraph (7) of subsection (a) of
96 Rule 9-1.4, relating to persons entitled to admission to the Senate during session, as follows:
97 "(7) Staff members of the office of the Lieutenant Governor, the office of the President
98 Pro Tempore, the office of the Majority Leader, the office of the Minority Leader, the
99 Senate Budget and Evaluation Office, the Senate Research Office of Policy and
100 Legislative Analysis, and the Senate Press Office,"
101 SECTION 8.
102 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by revising subsection (b) of Rule 9-1.6,
103 relating to secretaries, interns, and aides, as follows:
104 "(b) Each Senator may select an aide (not paid by the Senate) by designating in writing his
105 or her name to the Secretary of the Senate; this aide shall not be changed during the session
106 except by permission of the Committee on Administrative Affairs. No intern may be
107 designated as an aide by a Senator. The aide provided for in this paragraph shall be issued
108 an identifying badge, but shall not come on the floor of the Senate while the Senate is in
109 session. No Senator may designate, and there shall be no identifying badge issued to, an
110 individual as an aide who is a registered lobbyist or is required to register as a lobbyist
111 pursuant to Article 4 of Chapter 5 of Title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated."

114 "9-1.13 Smoking, Eating, Use of Cellular Phones Prohibited
115 There shall be no smoking, or eating, use of cellular telephones, or reading of newspapers
116 and other materials not pertinent to legislation in the Senate Chamber while the Senate is
117 in session."
118 SECTION 10.
119 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by revising Rule 9-1.14 as follows:
120 "9-1.14 Use of Computers, Cellular Phones, Reading Materials on the Senate Floor
121 A Senator shall use his or her computer and cellular phone in the Senate Chamber only for
122 legislative business, provided that this shall not prohibit the Senator from using the
123 computer at such times such devices for purposes of ordinary and necessary
124 communications with his or her home and business. Such devices shall not make audible
125 noises nor distract other Senators from the business before the Senate. Maintenance and
126 repair of Senators' computers or cellular phones shall not be conducted on the floor of the
127 Senate while the Senate is in session. There shall be no use of reading materials in the
128 Senate Chamber while the Senate is in session except that which is pertinent to legislation
129 or current events."
130 SECTION 11.
131 Said Rules of the Senate are further amended by revising subsection (a) of Rule 10-1.3,
132 relating to changes in rules, as follows:
133 "(a) Senate Rules shall be adopted at the beginning of any two-year term of the General
134 Assembly and at the beginning of any special session. Adoption of the Senate
135 Organizational Rules Resolution or Senate Special Session Organizational Rules
136 Resolution shall require an affirmative vote of a majority of the members to which the
137 Senate is entitled. All subsequent resolutions introduced to change or amend the Senate

138 Rules shall be assigned to the Committee on Rules and would require a two-thirds (2/3)
139 affirmative vote of the members to which the Senate is entitled for adoption."
