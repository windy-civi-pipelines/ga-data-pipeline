25 LC 59 0173
House Resolution 815
28th, Corbett of the 174th, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Creating the House Study Committee on Exploring the Potential for Humanoids and
2 Robotics in Georgia Workforce Development; and for other purposes.
3 WHEREAS, artificial intelligence (AI), the development of computer systems to perform
4 tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as learning and decision making, has
5 the potential to spur innovation and transform industry and government; and
6 WHEREAS, the science and technology of AI continue to develop, and more products and
7 services are utilizing AI in fields such as healthcare, financial services, education, housing,
8 and transportation; and
9 WHEREAS, Georgia and other states are embracing new technology and the opportunity to
10 improve lives through the responsible deployment of AI; and
11 WHEREAS, the technology associated with humanoids and robotics has improved
12 tremendously in recent years; and
13 WHEREAS, it is in the best interest of this state and its citizens to examine whether and how
14 humanoids and robotics can be used to enhance economic development.
H. R. 815
- 1 -
25 LC 59 0173
15 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES:
16 (1) Creation of House study committee. There is created the House Study Committee
17 on Exploring the Potential for Humanoids and Robotics in Georgia Workforce
18 Development.
19 (2) Members and officers. The committee shall be composed of five members of the
20 House of Representatives to be appointed by the Speaker of the House of
21 Representatives. The Speaker shall designate a member of the committee as chairperson
22 of the committee.
23 (3) Powers and duties. The committee shall undertake a study of the conditions, needs,
24 issues, and problems mentioned above or related thereto and recommend any action or
25 legislation which the committee deems necessary or appropriate.
26 (4) Meetings. The chairperson shall call all meetings of the committee. The committee
27 may conduct such meetings at such places and at such times as it may deem necessary or
28 convenient to enable it to exercise fully and effectively its powers, perform its duties, and
29 accomplish the objectives and purposes of this resolution.
30 (5) Allowances and funding. The legislative members of the committee shall receive
31 the allowances provided for in Code Section 28-1-8 of the Official Code of Georgia
32 Annotated. The allowances authorized by this resolution shall not be received by any
33 member of the committee for more than five days unless additional days are authorized.
34 Funds necessary to carry out the provisions of this resolution shall come from funds
35 appropriated to the House of Representatives.
36 (6) Report.
37 (A) In the event the committee adopts any specific findings or recommendations that
38 include suggestions for proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file a report of the
39 same prior to the date of abolishment specified in this resolution, subject to
40 subparagraph (C) of this paragraph.
H. R. 815
- 2 -
25 LC 59 0173
41 (B) In the event the committee adopts a report that does not include suggestions for
42 proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file the report, subject to subparagraph (C)
43 of this paragraph.
44 (C) No report shall be filed unless the same has been approved prior to the date of
45 abolishment specified in this resolution by majority vote of a quorum of the committee.
46 A report so approved shall be signed by the chairperson of the committee and filed with
47 the Clerk of the House of Representatives.
48 (D) In the absence of an approved report, the chairperson may file with the Clerk of the
49 House of Representatives a copy of the minutes of the meetings of the committee in lieu
50 thereof.
51 (7) Abolishment. The committee shall stand abolished on December 1, 2025.
