25 LC 112 3268
House Resolution 568
156th, Cummings of the 39th, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Commending the Atlanta Jewish Film Festival for 25 years of artistic and cultural excellence;
2 and for other purposes.
3 WHEREAS, the Atlanta Jewish Film Festival (AJFF) is the largest film festival in Atlanta
4 and the State of Georgia and is one of the most prestigious Jewish film festivals in the world,
5 attracting tens of thousands of attendees annually; and
6 WHEREAS, since its founding in 2000 by the Atlanta Regional Office of the American
7 Jewish Committee (AJC), AJFF has entertained, educated, and engaged diverse audiences
8 through Jewish storytelling, inspiring greater social and cultural understanding across
9 communities; and
10 WHEREAS, AJFF continues to partner with religious, ethnic, cultural, and educational
11 organizations—including public and private schools, colleges, and universities—to foster
12 deeper dialogue and strengthen bonds between communities; and
13 WHEREAS, AJFF is recognized as one of Georgia's most significant cultural events, ranking
14 among the highest-attended film festivals worldwide, growing from its inaugural audience
15 of 1,900 filmgoers to a record-breaking annual attendance of more than 40,000; and
H. R. 568
- 1 -

16 WHEREAS, the 25th Anniversary Atlanta Jewish Film Festival, taking place February 19
17 to March 16, 2025, features 88 theatrical screenings of 64 films from 22 countries across
18 multiple metro Atlanta venues, along with a 10-day streaming window offering 32 titles for
19 statewide streaming throughout Georgia; and
20 WHEREAS, during its 10th anniversary, AJFF expanded its cultural footprint by presenting
21 a landmark concert of iconic Jewish film scores in partnership with Turner Classic Movies
22 and the Atlanta Symphony Orchestra; and
23 WHEREAS, in 2015, AJFF made history by becoming the largest Jewish film festival in the
24 world, reinforcing Georgia's reputation as a vibrant hub for film and the arts; and
25 WHEREAS, as part of its 25th anniversary milestone, AJFF hosted a Field Trip Screening
26 event of Elie Wiesel: Soul on Fire, welcoming more than 500 students from public and
27 private schools across metro Atlanta, with a post-film Q&A featuring the filmmaker to
28 engage young audiences in the lessons of Holocaust history and human rights; and
29 WHEREAS, in celebration of its 25th anniversary, AJFF has launched the Kenny Blank
30 Vision Initiative, a $2.5 million capital campaign aimed at transforming the organization into
31 a year-round Jewish cultural institution by expanding programs in community relations,
32 education, direct filmmaker support, accessibility, and innovation; and
33 WHEREAS, the 25th anniversary of AJFF marks a pivotal moment in its evolution,
34 transitioning from a once-a-year festival into a global leader in Jewish arts and culture
35 dedicated to fostering creative expression and connecting diverse audiences through the
36 power of film.
H. R. 568
- 2 -
25 LC 112 3268
37 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
38 the members of this body commend the Atlanta Jewish Film Festival for 25 years of artistic
39 excellence, cultural impact, and commitment to strengthening the Atlanta community
40 through film and dialogue.
41 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
42 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the
43 Atlanta Jewish Film Festival.
