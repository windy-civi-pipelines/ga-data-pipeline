Section 1:
25 LC 61 0074
By: Representatives Berry of the 56th, Carpenter of the 4th, Frye of the 122nd, Park of the
107th, Sanchez of the 42nd, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Part 3 of Article 7 of Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia
2 Annotated, relating to the Georgia Student Finance Authority, so as to establish a needs
3 based grant program to combat food insecurity among postsecondary students; to provide
4 definitions; to provide for a maximum award amount; to provide for applications; to provide
5 for rules, regulations, and procedures; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting
6 laws; and for other purposes.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
8 SECTION 1.
9 Part 3 of Article 7 of Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
10 relating to the Georgia Student Finance Authority, is amended by adding a new subpart to
11 read as follows:
12 "Subpart 2B
13 20-3-367.
14 As used in this subpart, the term:
H. B. 716
- 1 -
25 LC 61 0074
15 (1) 'Commission' means the Georgia Student Finance Commission created by Code
16 Section 20-3-233.
17 (2) 'Eligible postsecondary institution' shall have the same meaning as set forth in Code
18 Section 20-3-519.
19 (3) 'Eligible student' means a person:
20 (A) Whose family income is considered economically disadvantaged, based on criteria
21 established by the commission, or who has a financial aid gap;
22 (B) Who meets any academic or other standards established by the commission;
23 (C) Who is eligible for a scholarship or grant pursuant to Code Section 20-3-519.1; and
24 (D) Who has completed the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).
25 (4) 'Financial aid gap' means the monetary amount remaining after other funding, as
26 determined by the commission, for the cost of attendance certified by an eligible
27 postsecondary institution.
28
29 20-3-368.
30 (a) Subject to appropriations, the commission shall establish a needs based financial aid
31 program to provide grants to eligible students enrolled at an eligible postsecondary
32 institution for the purpose of combating food insecurity among postsecondary students.
33 The maximum award per eligible student shall not exceed an amount equal to the highest
34 cost meal plan of the postsecondary institution attended by such eligible student. The
35 commission may provide for individualized eligibility criteria and grant amounts as
36 determined to be the most appropriate for the particular eligible postsecondary institution
37 and its student population in accordance with this subpart. The commission shall also
38 establish criteria necessary for eligible students to retain and continue to receive such
39 grants in accordance with this subpart.
40 (b) Each eligible student wishing to receive a grant pursuant to this subpart shall submit
41 a grant application in accordance with the rules, regulations, and procedures prescribed by
H. B. 716
- 2 -
25 LC 61 0074
42 the commission. The commission is authorized to define such terms and prescribe such
43 rules, regulations, and procedures as may be reasonable and necessary to carry out the
44 purposes of this subpart. In the event a student on whose behalf a grant is paid does not
45 enroll as a full-time or part-time student for the academic semester for which the grant is
46 paid, the qualified institution shall make a refund to the commission in accordance with the
47 rules and regulations of the commission."
48 SECTION 2.
49 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
