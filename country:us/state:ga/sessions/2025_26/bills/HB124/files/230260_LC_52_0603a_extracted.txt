Section 1:
25 LC 52 0603
By: Representatives Scoggins of the 14th, Hawkins of the 27th, Hatchett of the 155th, Clark
of the 100th, Newton of the 127th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 1 of Chapter 24 of Title 33 and Article 1 of Chapter 18 of Title 45 of the
2 Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to general provisions relative to insurance and
3 the state employees' health insurance plan and post-employment health benefit fund,
4 respectively, so as to require coverage for healthcare services for pediatric autoimmune
5 neuropsychiatric disorders associated with streptococcal infection (PANDAS) and pediatric
6 acute onset neuropsychiatric syndrome (PANS) in accordance with nationally recognized
7 clinical practice guidelines; to provide for definitions; to allow for deductibles; to prohibit
8 special deductibles; to provide for rules and regulations; to provide for related matters; to
9 repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
11 SECTION 1.
12 Article 1 of Chapter 24 of Title 33 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
13 general provisions relative to insurance, is amended by adding a new Code section to read
14 as follows:
15 "33-24-59.34.
16 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
H. B. 124
- 1 -
25 LC 52 0603
17 (1) 'Health benefit policy' means any individual or group plan, policy, or contract for
18 healthcare services issued, delivered, issued for delivery, or renewed in this state which
19 provides major medical benefits, including those contracts executed by the State of
20 Georgia on behalf of state employees under Article 1 of Chapter 18 of Title 45, by a
21 health care corporation, health maintenance organization, preferred provider organization,
22 accident and sickness insurer, fraternal benefit society, hospital service corporation,
23 medical service corporation, or other insurer or similar entity. Such term shall not
24 include any self-insured health benefit plan subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the
25 federal Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974, 29 U.S.C. Section 1001,
26 et seq.
27 (2) 'Nationally recognized clinical practice guidelines' means evidence based clinical
28 practice guidelines developed by independent organizations or medical professional
29 societies utilizing a transparent methodology and reporting structure and with a
30 conflict-of-interest policy. Such guidelines establish standards of care informed by a
31 systematic review of evidence and an assessment of the benefits and risks of alternative
32 care options and include recommendations intended to optimize patient care.
33 (3) 'Pediatric acute onset neuropsychiatric syndrome' or 'PANS' means a class of acute
34 onset obsessive compulsive or tic disorders or other behavioral changes presenting in
35 children and adolescents that are not otherwise explained by another known neurologic
36 or medical disorder.
37 (4) 'Pediatric autoimmune neuropsychiatric disorders associated with streptococcal
38 infections' or 'PANDAS' means a condition in which a streptococcal infection in a child
39 or adolescent causes the abrupt onset of clinically significant obsessions, compulsions,
40 tics, or other neuropsychiatric symptoms or behavioral changes, or a relapsing and
41 remitting course of symptom severity.
H. B. 124
- 2 -
25 LC 52 0603
42 (b) All health benefit policies renewed or issued on or after July 1, 2025, shall include
43 coverage for healthcare services for PANDAS or PANS for an individual covered under
44 such policy as provided in this Code section.
45 (c) Healthcare services for PANDAS or PANS for an individual covered under a health
46 benefit policy shall be covered for the purposes of diagnosis, treatment, appropriate
47 management, or ongoing monitoring of a covered person's disorder when such services are
48 supported by nationally recognized clinical practice guidelines.
49 (d) The benefits in a health benefit policy as provided for in this Code section shall be
50 subject to the same deductibles, coinsurance, copayment provisions, and other limitations
51 established for all covered benefits within such health benefit policy. Special deductibles,
52 coinsurance, copayment, and other limitations that are not generally applicable to other
53 healthcare services covered by a health benefit policy shall not be imposed on coverage for
54 healthcare services for PANDAS or PANS.
55 (e) The Commissioner shall promulgate rules and regulations necessary to implement the
56 provisions of this Code section."
57 SECTION 2.
58 Article 1 of Chapter 18 of Title 45 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to the
59 state employees' insurance health insurance plan and post-employment health benefit fund,
60 is amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
61 "45-18-4.2.
62 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
63 (1) 'Nationally recognized clinical practice guidelines' means evidence based clinical
64 practice guidelines developed by independent organizations or medical professional
65 societies utilizing a transparent methodology and reporting structure and with a
66 conflict-of-interest policy. Such guidelines establish standards of care informed by a

68 care options and include recommendations intended to optimize patient care.
69 (2) 'Pediatric acute onset neuropsychiatric syndrome' or 'PANS' means a class of acute
70 onset obsessive compulsive or tic disorders or other behavioral changes presenting in
71 children and adolescents that are not otherwise explained by another known neurologic
72 or medical disorder.
73 (3) 'Pediatric autoimmune neuropsychiatric disorders associated with streptococcal
74 infections' or 'PANDAS' means a condition in which a streptococcal infection in a child

80 public employees, dependents, and retirees.
81 (b) Beginning January 1, 2026, the state health benefit plan shall include coverage for

83 provided in this Code section.
84 (c) Healthcare services for PANDAS or PANS for an individual covered under the state

87 supported by nationally recognized clinical practice guidelines.
88 (d) The benefits in the state health benefit plan as provided for in this Code section shall

90 limitations established for all covered benefits within such plan. Special deductibles,
91 coinsurance, copayment, and other limitations that are not generally applicable to
92 healthcare services covered by such plan shall not be imposed on coverage for healthcare
93 services for PANDAS or PANS."
