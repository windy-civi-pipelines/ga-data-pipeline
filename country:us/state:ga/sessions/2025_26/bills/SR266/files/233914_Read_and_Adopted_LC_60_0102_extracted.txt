Senate Resolution 266
By: Senator James of the 28th
A RESOLUTION
2 Furquan R. Stafford and other community activists; and for other purposes.
3 WHEREAS, for many years, countless individuals have suffered from medical deficiencies
4 which could be treated or controlled by therapies derived from human plasma; and
5 WHEREAS, the United States has established a substantial network of federally licensed
6 plasma collection centers, and reports have indicated that over 493 million plasma donations
7 were recorded across such centers between 2007 and 2021; and
8 WHEREAS, reports have further indicated that 33 million liters of plasma are used
9 worldwide every year to manufacture essential plasma protein therapies critical for treating
10 a wide array of medical conditions, including, but not limited to, blood clotting disorders,
11 sickle cell anemia, immune deficiencies, neurological disorders, hemophilia, and Alzheimer's
12 disease; and
13 WHEREAS, demand for these plasma protein therapies has escalated dramatically over the
14 past two decades, driven by advances in the diagnosis of rare diseases, elevated standards of
15 care, and enhanced patient access to innovative treatments; and

16 WHEREAS, the global market for human plasma sales reached a remarkable estimate of
17 approximately $30 billion in 2022, marking significant growth from just over $5 billion in
18 2000; and
19 WHEREAS, statistics have shown that over 86 percent of plasma donations in the United
20 States originate from African American donors; and
21 WHEREAS, no plasma collection centers in the United States are owned by African
22 Americans; and
23 WHEREAS, several community activists have diligently and conscientiously advocated to
24 expand development of minority owned plasma collection centers, including, but not limited
25 to, Furquan R. Stafford who has been advocating on this issue for over ten years.
26 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
27 encourage the United States plasma collection industry to open up its markets and allow
28 equal opportunity for minorities to participate in the industry by executing plasma collection
29 agreements with them.
30 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of this body recognize and commend the
31 significant contributions made by Furquan R. Stafford and other community activists in
32 expanding racial and ethnic diversity in the ownership and operation of plasma collection
33 centers.
34 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
35 to make appropriate copies of this resolution available for distribution to members of the
36 public and press.
