14 WHEREAS, January 27, the anniversary of the liberation of Auschwitz-Birkenau, is
15 observed as International Holocaust Remembrance Day to memorialize the six million
16 Jewish victims of the Holocaust and the millions of other victims of Nazi persecution; and
17 WHEREAS, January 27, 2025, marks the 80th anniversary of the liberation of Auschwitz,
18 a critical time to honor the victims, survivors, and liberators while recognizing the lessons
19 of Holocaust history amidst an alarming global rise in antisemitism; and
20 WHEREAS, located in German-occupied Poland, Auschwitz consisted of three camps,
21 including Birkenau, a killing center where over a million victims were systematically
22 murdered by individuals and professionals involved in its operation; and
23 WHEREAS, trains carrying Jews from across Nazi-occupied and allied Europe regularly
24 arrived at Auschwitz, where its leadership and workers conducted these systematic killings
25 over nearly five years; and
26 WHEREAS, in mid-January 1945, as Soviet forces approached Auschwitz, SS units forcibly
27 evacuated nearly 60,000 prisoners on death marches under brutal winter conditions, resulting
28 in thousands of deaths from starvation, exposure, and violence; and
29 WHEREAS, on January 27, 1945, the Soviet army liberated Auschwitz, freeing the
30 remaining 7,000 prisoners, many of whom were sick and dying, and ending the camp's
31 operation; and

32 WHEREAS, we, the people of Georgia, join communities throughout the world in
33 commemorating International Holocaust Remembrance Day and honoring the victims,
34 survivors, and liberators of the Holocaust.

36 the members of this body recognize January 27, 2025, as International Holocaust
