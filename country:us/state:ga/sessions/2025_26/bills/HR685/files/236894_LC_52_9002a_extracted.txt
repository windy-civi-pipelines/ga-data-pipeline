25 LC 52 9002
House Resolution 685
84th, and Ballard of the 147th
A RESOLUTION
1 Creating the House Study Committee on Mental Health Workforce Development; and for
2 other purposes.
3 WHEREAS, according to the Health Resources and Services Administration, the United
4 States is experiencing a mental health crisis, and more than half of the U.S. population lives
5 in a Mental Health Professional Shortage Area; and
6 WHEREAS, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of substance use,
7 behavioral disorder, and mental health counselors is projected to grow 19 percent from 2023
8 to 2033, which is much faster than the average for all occupations; and
9 WHEREAS, the need for qualified clinical mental health professionals is at a critical level,
10 particularly in rural Georgia; and
11 WHEREAS, although undergraduate degrees in behavioral science, social work, sociology,
12 or psychology can prepare students for graduate school, many students would prefer degrees
13 that prepare them for immediate careers upon graduation; and
H. R. 685

14 WHEREAS, providing qualified baccalaureate mental health professionals a path to licensure
15 or certification would help alleviate the shortage of mental health professionals in the
16 workforce in Georgia; and
17 WHEREAS, an increase in the number of qualified mental health professionals in Georgia
18 will ensure greater accessibility to mental health services; and
19 WHEREAS, it would be beneficial to conduct a study to survey current undergraduate
20 degrees and projected mental health careers available to college graduates, evaluate
21 employment needs and opportunities, and recommend mental health workforce development
22 initiatives.
23 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES:
24 (1) Creation of House study committee. There is created the House Study Committee
25 on Mental Health Workforce Development.
26 (2) Members and officers. The committee shall be composed of five members of the
27 House of Representatives to be appointed by the Speaker of the House of
28 Representatives. The Speaker shall designate a member of the committee as chairperson
29 of the committee.
30 (3) Powers and duties. The committee shall undertake a study of the conditions, needs,
31 issues, and problems mentioned above or related thereto and recommend any action or
32 legislation which the committee deems necessary or appropriate.
33 (4) Meetings. The chairperson shall call all meetings of the committee. The committee
34 may conduct such meetings at such places and at such times as it may deem necessary or
35 convenient to enable it to exercise fully and effectively its powers, perform its duties, and
36 accomplish the objectives and purposes of this resolution.
H. R. 685
- 2 -
25 LC 52 9002
37 (5) Allowances and funding. The legislative members of the committee shall receive
38 the allowances provided for in Code Section 28-1-8 of the Official Code of Georgia
39 Annotated. The allowances authorized by this resolution shall not be received by any
40 member of the committee for more than five days unless additional days are authorized.
41 Funds necessary to carry out the provisions of this resolution shall come from funds
42 appropriated to the House of Representatives.
43 (6) Report.
44 (A) In the event the committee adopts any specific findings or recommendations that
45 include suggestions for proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file a report of the
46 same prior to the date of abolishment specified in this resolution, subject to
47 subparagraph (C) of this paragraph.
48 (B) In the event the committee adopts a report that does not include suggestions for
49 proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file the report, subject to subparagraph (C)
50 of this paragraph.
51 (C) No report shall be filed unless the same has been approved prior to the date of
52 abolishment specified in this resolution by majority vote of a quorum of the committee.
53 A report so approved shall be signed by the chairperson of the committee and filed with
54 the Clerk of the House of Representatives.
55 (D) In the absence of an approved report, the chairperson may file with the Clerk of the
56 House of Representatives a copy of the minutes of the meetings of the committee in lieu
57 thereof.
58 (7) Abolishment. The committee shall stand abolished on December 1, 2025.
