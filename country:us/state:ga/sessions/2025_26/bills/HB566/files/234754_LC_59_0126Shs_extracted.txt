to HB 566:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 1 of Title 10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to selling
2 and other trade practices, so as to enact the "NO FAKES Act of 2025"; to protect intellectual
3 property rights in the voice and visual likeness of individuals; to provide for the
4 characteristics of a right to use the voice or visual likeness in a digital replica; to provide for
5 licensing and transferability of a right; to require the Secretary of State to maintain a
6 directory of certain post-mortem digital replication rights and a directory of designated
7 agents; to authorize the Secretary of State to make interpretations and resolve ambiguities
8 in carrying out provisions of this article; to provide for rules and regulations; to provide for
9 civil liability for the unauthorized use of a digital replica; to provide for penalties; to bar
10 liability arising from retroactive application of this Act; to provide for definitions; to provide
11 for an effective date; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
13 SECTION 1.
14 This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Nurture Originals, Foster Art, and Keep
15 Entertainment Safe Act of 2025" or the "NO FAKES Act of 2025."

17 Chapter 1 of Title 10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to selling and other
18 trade practices, is amended by enacting a new article to read as follows:
19 "ARTICLE 37
20 10-1-950.
21 As used in this article, the term:
22 (1) 'Digital replica' means a newly created, computer generated, highly realistic
23 electronic representation that is readily identifiable as the voice or visual likeness of an
24 individual that:
25 (A) Is embodied in a sound recording, image, audiovisual work, including an
26 audiovisual work that does not have any accompanying sounds, or transmission:
27 (i) In which the actual individual did not actually perform or appear; or
28 (ii) That is a version of a sound recording, image, or audiovisual work, in which the
29 actual individual did perform or appear, where the fundamental character of the
30 performance or appearance has been materially altered; and
31 (B) Does not include the electronic reproduction, use of a sample of one sound
32 recording or audiovisual work into another, remixing, mastering, or digital remastering
33 of a sound recording or audiovisual work authorized by the copyright holder.
34 (2) 'Individual' means a human being, living or dead.
35 (3) 'Online service' means:
36 (A)(i) Any public website, online application, mobile application, or virtual reality
37 environment that predominantly provides a community forum for user generated
38 content, such as sharing videos, images, games, audio files, or other material; or
39 (ii) A digital music provider, as defined in 17 U.S.C. Section 115(e); and

40 (B) May include a social media service, social network, or application store; provided,
41 however, that such term does not include a service by wire or radio that provides the
42 capability to transmit data to and receive data from all, or substantially all, internet
43 endpoints, including any capabilities that are incidental to enabling the operation of the
44 communications service of a provider of online services or network access, or the
45 operator of facilities for such service.
46 (4) 'Production' means the creation of a digital replica.
47 (5) 'Right holder' means the individual whose voice or visual likeness is at issue with
48 respect to a digital replica and any other person that has acquired, through a license,
49 inheritance, or otherwise, the right to authorize the use of such voice or visual likeness
50 in a digital replica.
51 (6) 'Sound recording artist' means an individual who creates or performs in sound
52 recordings for economic gain or for the livelihood of the individual.

64 such right is commercially exploited by the individual during the individual's lifetime;
65 (3) Upon the death of the individual:

70 law; and
