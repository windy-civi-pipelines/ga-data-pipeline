Section 1:
25 LC 61 0155
By: Representatives Santos of the 117th, Reese of the 140th, Bell of the 75th, Berry of the 56th,
Flournoy of the 74th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Part 3 of Article 7 of Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia
2 Annotated, relating to the Georgia Student Finance Authority, so as to establish a needs
3 based financial aid program to award grants to eligible students who have not yet received
4 their financial aid award so that such students can obtain the essential materials they need to
5 begin classes; to provide a maximum award amount; to provide definitions; to provide for
6 eligibility; to provide for applications; to provide for rules, regulations, and procedures; to
7 provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
9 SECTION 1.
10 Part 3 of Article 7 of Chapter 3 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
11 relating to the Georgia Student Finance Authority, is amended by adding a new subpart to
12 read as follows:
13 "Subpart 2B
14 20-3-367.
15 As used in this subpart, the term:
H. B. 695
- 1 -
25 LC 61 0155
16 (1) 'Commission' means the Georgia Student Finance Commission created by Code
17 Section 20-3-233.
18 (2) 'Eligible student' means a person:
19 (A) Whose family income is considered economically disadvantaged, based on criteria
20 established by the commission;
21 (B) Who meets any academic or other standards established by the commission;
22 (C) Who is eligible for a scholarship or grant pursuant to Code Section 20-3-519.1;
23 (D) Who has completed the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA);
24 (E) Who is enrolled in a qualified institution; and
25 (F) Who has applied and is eligible for state or federal financial aid but has not
26 received his or her financial aid award within two weeks of the first day of the semester
27 or quarter.
28 (3) 'Essential materials' means books, materials, or other items identified by the course
29 instructor as essential to participating in and completing a required course as part of an
30 eligible student's program of study.
31 (4) 'Qualified institution' shall have the same meaning as the term 'eligible postsecondary
32 institution' as set forth in Code Section 20-3-519.
33 20-3-368.
34 (a) Subject to appropriations, the commission shall establish a needs based financial aid
35 program to provide grants to eligible students for the purpose of obtaining essential
36 materials. The maximum award amount per eligible student shall be $5,000.00 in total.
37 Grant awards shall be disbursed directly to each student who is awarded a grant under this
38 subpart. The commission may provide for individualized eligibility criteria and grant
39 amounts as determined to be the most appropriate for the particular qualified institution and
40 program of study in accordance with this subpart and the rules and regulations of the
41 commission.
H. B. 695
- 2 -
25 LC 61 0155
42 (b) Each eligible student wishing to receive a grant pursuant to this subpart shall submit
43 a grant application in accordance with the rules, regulations, and procedures prescribed by
44 the commission. Such application shall include the essential materials such student wishes
45 to obtain and the estimated cost of such essential materials.
46 20-3-369.
47 The commission is authorized to define such terms and prescribe such rules, regulations,
48 and procedures as may be reasonable and necessary to carry out the purposes of this
49 subpart. Such rules, regulations, and procedures shall provide for the expedited processing
50 of applications and disbursement of grant awards."
51 SECTION 2.
52 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
