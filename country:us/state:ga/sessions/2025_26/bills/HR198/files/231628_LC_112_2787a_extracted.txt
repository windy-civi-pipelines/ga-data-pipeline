25 LC 112 2787
House Resolution 198
57th, Williams of the 37th, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing February 13, 2025, as New Americans Day at the state capitol; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, for the past 12 years, New Americans Day celebrations have brought hundreds
4 of new Americans, friends, and community supporters to the state capitol annually for a day
5 of education and celebration; and
6 WHEREAS, Georgia has a long history of extending welcome to new Americans from
7 around the world, giving them an opportunity to live, work, and thrive in the state; and
8 WHEREAS, today, one in ten Georgians—more than one million people—are foreign-born,
9 bringing valuable skills and talents from countries around the world to the state; and
10 WHEREAS, approximately one in eight Georgia workers is foreign-born, as are one in five
11 doctors and home health aides; one in five science and technology professionals; one in five
12 manufacturing workers; and nearly half of all workers in the construction industry; and
H. R. 198

13 WHEREAS, foreign-born Georgians are 41 percent more likely than others to start new
14 businesses in the state, accounting for more than 20 percent of entrepreneurs in the state and
15 generating more than $2.5 billion in business income annually; and
16 WHEREAS, hardworking foreign-born Georgians across all sectors paid over $11 billion in
17 federal, state, and local taxes and held more than $32 billion in spending power in 2021
18 alone; and
19 WHEREAS, nearly half of all foreign-born Georgians are already naturalized United States
20 citizens eligible to vote and many more are on the path toward citizenship; and
21 WHEREAS, foreign-born Georgians were never more essential to the state than during the
22 COVID-19 pandemic, serving in disproportionate numbers in front line jobs in hospitals and
23 assisted living facilities, farms and food processing plants, warehouses and delivery services,
24 and more; and
25 WHEREAS, since the withdrawal of United States forces from Afghanistan in August 2021
26 and the Russian invasion of Ukraine in 2022, Georgia has provided refuge to thousands and
27 granted humanitarian parole to escape those countries and come to safety in the United
28 States, including many who risked their lives to support the United States' mission overseas;
29 and
30 WHEREAS, Georgia has one of the oldest and most successful refugee programs in the
31 United States, with approximately 90 percent of refugees economically self-sufficient within
32 six months; and
H. R. 198
- 2 -

33 WHEREAS, Georgia is a welcoming home for peoples of all origins and from all walks of
34 life, and it is abundantly fitting and proper that these new Americans be appropriately
35 recognized.
36 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
37 the members of this body recognize and commend the manifold contributions of immigrants
38 to the State of Georgia and recognize February 13, 2025, as New Americans Day at the state
39 capitol.
40 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
41 and directed to make appropriate copies of this resolution available for distribution to the
42 public and the press.
