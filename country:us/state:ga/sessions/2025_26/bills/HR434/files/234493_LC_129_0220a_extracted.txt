25 LC 129 0220
House Resolution 434
McQueen of the 61st
A RESOLUTION
1 Honoring the life and memory of Terrance Lamont Cooper; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, the members of this body wish to express their deepest and most sincere regret
3 at the passing of Terrance Lamont Cooper on February 16, 2025; and
4 WHEREAS, Terrance Lamont Cooper was born on June 3, 1971, in Queens, New York, and
5 later relocated to Georgia in 1985; and
6 WHEREAS, he was educated in the Bibb County School System, attending Florence Bernd
7 Elementary School and Appling B Middle School before graduating from Northeast High
8 School in 1989; and
9 WHEREAS, he pursued higher education at Valdosta State College before beginning his
10 professional career; and
11 WHEREAS, he answered the call to service by enlisting in the United States Army National
12 Guard, where he served honorably as a Material Storage and Handling Specialist with the
13 Detachment 1, Bravo Company, 148th Brigade; and
H. R. 434
- 1 -
25 LC 129 0220
14 WHEREAS, as a Desert Storm Veteran (1990-1991), he demonstrated unwavering courage
15 and commitment to the defense of this nation; and
16 WHEREAS, he embarked on a distinguished career at the Centers for Disease Control and
17 Prevention, beginning as an IT Helpdesk Support Contractor in 1999, and through his hard
18 work and dedication, he became a full-time employee in July 2024 as a Helpdesk Lead; and
19 WHEREAS, Terrance Lamont Cooper was a devoted son, friend, and colleague whose
20 kindness, dedication, and service will long be remembered by those who knew and loved
21 him.
22 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
23 the members of this body join in honoring the life and memory of Terrance Lamont Cooper,
24 express their heartfelt condolences to his family and friends, and recognize his outstanding
25 contributions to both his community and the nation.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
27 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the
28 family of Terrance Lamont Cooper.
H. R. 434
