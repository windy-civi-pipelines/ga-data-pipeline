25 LC 47 3450
House Resolution 367
99th, and Williams of the 168th
A RESOLUTION
1 Proposing an amendment to the Constitution so as to provide that members of the General
2 Assembly elected in 2028 and thereafter shall serve four-year terms of office; to provide that
3 members of the General Assembly elected in the 2026 general election shall serve two-year
4 terms of office; to provide for related matters; to provide for the submission of this
5 amendment for ratification or rejection; and for other purposes.
6 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
7 SECTION 1.
8 Article III, Section II of the Constitution is amended by revising Paragraph V as follows:
9 "Paragraph V. Election and term of members. (a) The members of the General
10 Assembly elected in 2028 and thereafter shall be elected by the qualified electors of their
11 respective districts for a term of two four years and shall serve until the time fixed for the
12 convening of the next General Assembly.
13 (b) The members of the General Assembly in office on June 30, 1983, elected in the
14 2026 general election shall serve out the remainder of the two-year terms to which elected.
15 (c) The first election for members of the General Assembly under this Constitution shall
16 take place on the Tuesday after the first Monday in November, 1984 2026, and subsequent
H. R. 367

17 elections biennially until 2028, and subsequent elections after 2028 quadrennially on that
18 day until the day of election is changed by law."
19 SECTION 2.
20 The above proposed amendment to the Constitution shall be published and submitted as
21 provided in Article X, Section I, Paragraph II of the Constitution. The ballot submitting the
22 above proposed amendment shall have written or printed thereon the following:
23 "( ) YES Shall the Constitution of Georgia be amended so as to provide that members
24 ( ) NO of the General Assembly shall serve four-year terms of office after the 2028
25 general election?"
26 All persons desiring to vote in favor of ratifying the proposed amendment shall vote "Yes."
27 All persons desiring to vote against ratifying the proposed amendment shall vote "No." If
28 such amendment shall be ratified as provided in said Paragraph of the Constitution, it shall
29 become a part of the Constitution of this state.
