25 LC 112 2793
House Resolution 181
the 154th, and Cameron of the 1st
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing February 10, 2025, as Georgia Hearing Day at the state capitol; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, hearing plays a critical role in communication and personal relationships,
4 overall health outcomes, psychological well-being, and safety; and
5 WHEREAS, hearing loss has been tied to cognitive decline, dementia, falls, depression, and
6 isolation. It can be a symptom of an underlying medical condition, including, but not limited
7 to, infections, tumors, otosclerosis, and Meniere's disease and is typically irreversible; and
8 WHEREAS, a study conducted by the Better Hearing Institute concluded that untreated
9 hearing loss has significant income ramifications, including negatively impacting household
10 income by up to $12,000 annually and costing $122 billion in lost earning in the United
11 States; and
12 WHEREAS, noise-induced hearing loss is a preventable and significant, through often
13 unrecognized, health problem among United States adults, and is a growing problem with
14 adolescents due to overamplification of personal listening devices; and

15 WHEREAS, according to the National Institutes of Deafness and Other Communications
16 Disorders (NIDCD), more than 37 million American adults (15 percent) report some trouble
17 hearing, and approximately 28.2 million American adults could benefit from using hearing
18 aids; and
19 WHEREAS, according to the NIDCD, fewer than 30 percent of adults aged 70 and older and
20 16 percent of adults aged 20 to 69 who experience hearing loss and who could benefit from
21 hearing aids have ever used them; and
22 WHEREAS, the Georgia Society of Hearing Professionals, a chapter of the International
23 Hearing Society, is composed of hearing health care professionals, including hearing aid
24 specialists and audiologists, who are trained and specialize in the identification and
25 assessment of hearing loss, rehabilitation through the use of hearing aids and related devices,
26 and counseling; and
27 WHEREAS, Georgia Hearing Day, founded by the Georgia Society of Hearing
28 Professionals, aims to raise awareness of how to prevent deafness and hearing loss and to
29 promote ear and hearing care across the world.
30 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
31 the members of this body recognize February 10, 2025, as Georgia Hearing Day at the state
32 capitol to encourage the citizens of Georgia to protect their hearing and seek the services of
33 a licensed hearing health care professional, such as a hearing aid specialist, audiologist, or
34 otolaryngologist, for regular hearing checks.
