25 LC 60 0087
House Resolution 395
the 69th, and Paris of the 142nd
A RESOLUTION
1 Designating the month of May as Maternal Mental Health Awareness Month; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, over 100,000 babies are born in the State of Georgia each year; and
4 WHEREAS, between 10 and 20 percent of new and expectant mothers are affected by
5 perinatal depression and related mood disorders, such as anxiety and psychosis, and
6 frequently experience symptoms which are overlooked and heavily stigmatized, causing
7 them to feel confused, ashamed, and isolated; and
8 WHEREAS, perinatal depression and related mood disorders affect all women and teenage
9 girls equally, regardless of their age, race, or income level; and
10 WHEREAS, perinatal depression is highly treatable with therapeutic intervention such as
11 medication, professional therapy and counseling, support groups, and community support
12 services, including crisis hotlines, but there is a general lack of knowledge of and use of
13 screening and assessment tools for perinatal depression and related mood disorders; and
H. R. 395
- 1 -
25 LC 60 0087
14 WHEREAS, many women at risk for perinatal depression often do not seek help because
15 they are not informed about perinatal depression and related mood disorders as part of their
16 healthcare; and
17 WHEREAS, if left untreated, perinatal depression and related mood disorders in new and
18 expectant mothers can have a profound impact on families and significantly contribute to
19 adverse developmental and behavioral outcomes and attachment disorders in young children;
20 and
21 WHEREAS, to promote maternal and infant mental health in the State of Georgia, there is
22 a need for heightened awareness and increased education among all citizens in this state
23 regarding the incidence of perinatal depression and related mood disorders and the
24 availability of prenatal screening tools and treatment, prevention, and support services.
25 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
26 the members of this body:
27 (1) Designate May of each year as Maternal Mental Health Awareness Month in the
28 State of Georgia;
29 (2) Encourage officials and departments of state, county, and municipal governments,
30 businesses, and all citizens to participate in appropriate programs and activities that
31 facilitate increased awareness and education about perinatal and related mood disorders
32 throughout each May during Maternal Mental Health Awareness Month; and
33 (3) Respectfully request the Governor to issue annually a proclamation recognizing May
34 in the State of Georgia as Maternal Mental Health Awareness Month and call upon the
35 people in the State of Georgia to observe the month with appropriate programs and
36 activities.

37 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
38 and directed to make appropriate copies of this resolution available for distribution to
39 Governor Brian Kemp and the public and the press.
