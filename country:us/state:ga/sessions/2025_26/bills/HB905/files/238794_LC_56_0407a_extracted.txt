Section 1:
25 LC 56 0407
By: Representatives Richardson of the 125th, Gunter of the 8th, Leverett of the 123rd,
Efstration of the 104th, Burchett of the 176th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Code Section 47-23-64 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 transfer of funds from the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia to the Georgia Judicial
3 Retirement System by superior court judges, district attorneys, or solicitors-general and
4 crediting service, so as to provide that any member of such retirement system shall be
5 eligible to transfer funds; to provide conditions for an effective date and automatic repeal;
6 to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
8 SECTION 1.
9 Code Section 47-23-64 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to transfer of
10 funds from the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia to the Georgia Judicial Retirement
11 System by superior court judges, district attorneys, or solicitors-general and crediting service,
12 is amended by revising paragraph (1) of subsection (a) as follows:
13 "(a)(1) Any superior court judge, state court judge, solicitor general, or district attorney
14 member of this retirement system who was previously an active member of the
15 Employees' Retirement System of Georgia may elect to have all contributions made by
16 or on behalf of such member transferred from such retirement system to this retirement
H. B. 905
- 1 -
25 LC 56 0407
17 system. Any such member shall notify the board of trustees of each retirement system
18 not later than December 31, 2002 2026, or within 90 days after first becoming a member
19 of this retirement system, whichever date is later."
20 SECTION 2.
21 This Act shall become effective on July 1, 2026, only if it is determined to have been
22 concurrently funded as provided in Chapter 20 of Title 47 of the Official Code of Georgia
23 Annotated, the "Public Retirement Systems Standards Law"; otherwise, this Act shall not
24 become effective and shall be automatically repealed in its entirety on July 1, 2026, as
25 required by subsection (a) of Code Section 47-20-50.
26 SECTION 3.
27 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
