Section 1:
25 LC 57 0249S
to HB 500:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 9 of Chapter 4 of Title 49 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to the "Temporary Assistance for Needy Families Act," so as to increase access to
3 and amount of benefits; to revise a definition; to increase the lifetime maximum for benefits;
4 to revise the income and resources of an applicant that may be considered to determine
5 eligibility; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
7 SECTION 1.
8 Article 9 of Chapter 4 of Title 49 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to the
9 "Temporary Assistance for Needy Families Act," is amended in Code Section 49-4-181,
10 relating to definitions, by revising paragraph (4) as follows:
11 "(4) 'Cash assistance' means the money payment component of TANF assistance, which
12 shall be based on a standard of need that is equal to 40 percent of the federal poverty level
13 for the applicable family size and which equates to a maximum monthly amount equal
14 to 75 percent of such amount for each such family size."
H. B. 500 (SUB)
- 1 -
25 LC 57 0249S
15 SECTION 2.
16 Said article is further amended in Code Section 49-4-182, relating to the Temporary
17 Assistance for Needy Families Program created, by revising subsection (b) as follows:
18 "(b) Assistance shall be provided in accordance with the state plan and any future
19 amendments thereto. Cash assistance to a recipient who is not a minor child and who is a
20 head of a household or married to the head of a household shall be limited to a lifetime
21 maximum of 48 60 months, whether or not consecutive, beginning January 1, 1997
22 July 1, 2025."
23 SECTION 3.
24 Said article is further amended in Code Section 49-4-183, relating to administration of article
25 by department, promulgation of rules and regulations by board, and duties of department, by
26 revising paragraph (3) of subsection (b) as follows:
27 "(3) Consideration of the income and resources of an applicant for assistance in
28 determining eligibility; provided, however, that the resources of an applicant for assistance
29 shall not exceed $5,000.00, provided that the equity value of one vehicle per family shall
30 not be counted as a resource of the family if the vehicle is used for work, training, or
31 education preparatory to employment or job search; provided, further, that an up to
32 $4,650.00 exclusion of the equity value of one additional vehicle is allowed;"
33 SECTION 4.
34 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
H. B. 500 (SUB)
