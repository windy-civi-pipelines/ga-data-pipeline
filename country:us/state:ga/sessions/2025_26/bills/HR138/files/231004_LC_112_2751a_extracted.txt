25 LC 112 2751
House Resolution 138
of the 21st, and Clark of the 100th
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing February 6, 2025, as National Guard Day at the state capitol; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, the Georgia Department of Defense provides ready and relevant military forces
4 to combatant commanders to defend our nation and to the governor to support homeland
5 defense and defense support to civil authorities; and
6 WHEREAS, the Georgia Department of Defense is headquartered at the Clay National Guard
7 Center in Marietta, Georgia, and it includes the Georgia Army National Guard, the Georgia
8 Air National Guard, and the Georgia State Defense Force; and
9 WHEREAS, the Georgia Department of Defense consists of more than 11,000 army national
10 guard soldiers; 2,800 air national guard airmen; 450 state defense force volunteers; and 500
11 state employees; and
12 WHEREAS, the Georgia Army National Guard is the sixth largest in the nation, trains in
13 nearly 70 hometown armories and regional facilities in almost 60 counties across the state,
14 and includes combat, combat support, and combat service support units; and

15 WHEREAS, the Georgia Army National Guard is organized into five major subordinate
16 commands, which are the Macon-based 48th Infantry Brigade Combat Team; the 648th
17 Maneuver Enhancement Brigade at Fort Moore; and the 78th Troop Command, 78th
18 Aviation Troop Command, and 201st Regional Support Group at the Clay National Guard
19 Center in Marietta; and
20 WHEREAS, the Georgia Air National Guard is the air power component of the State of
21 Georgia with airmen and officers assigned to the 116th Air Control Wing in Warner Robins,
22 the 165th Airlift Wing in Savannah, and geographically separated units throughout Georgia,
23 playing an active role in supporting combatant commanders and Georgia's homeland defense
24 and emergency response; and
25 WHEREAS, the Georgia State Defense Force is a wholly volunteer unit of the Georgia
26 Department of Defense, serving in support of the national and state constitutions under
27 direction of the governor and the adjutant general, notably during hurricane response
28 operations in 2024; and
29 WHEREAS, the Georgia National Guard has deployed more than 33,000 soldiers and airmen
30 throughout all geographic combatant commands since September 11, 2001; maintained a
31 steady overseas presence in support of contingency operations; maintained state partnerships
32 with the Country of Georgia and Republic of Argentina; and supported homeland response
33 missions within the State of Georgia due to winter storms, hurricanes, COVID-19, and civil
34 unrest; and
35 WHEREAS, in 2024, more than 2,000 Georgia guard soldiers, airmen, and state defense
36 force personnel responded in the wake of hurricanes Debbie, Milton, and Helene, the last of
37 which was the Georgia National Guard's largest domestic storm response in more than 30

38 years, encompassing more than 100 missions in 35 counties ranging from debris clearance
39 to humanitarian support; and
40 WHEREAS, the selfless actions of the Georgia Department of Defense and its components
41 at home and abroad in 2024 stands as an example of putting service before self, and it is
42 abundantly fitting and proper that the outstanding accomplishments of these remarkable and
43 distinguished Georgians be appropriately recognized.
44 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
45 the members of this body recognize February 6, 2025, as National Guard Day at the state
46 capitol and commend the men and women of the Georgia National Guard for their strength,
47 leadership, courage, and selfless service.
48 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
49 and directed to make appropriate copies of this resolution available for distribution to the
50 public and the press.
