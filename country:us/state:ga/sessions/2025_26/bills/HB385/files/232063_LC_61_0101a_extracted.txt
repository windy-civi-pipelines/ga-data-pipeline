25 LC 61 0101
By: Representative Holcomb of the 101st
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Code Section 20-3-519.2 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 eligibility requirements for a HOPE scholarship and award amount, so as to allow
3 academically successful students who are concurrently seeking a baccalaureate degree and
4 a first professional degree to use the full number of hours of HOPE scholarship eligibility;
5 to allow academically successful students who commence a graduate program at an eligible
6 postsecondary institution within 18 months of earning a baccalaureate degree to use the full
7 number of hours of HOPE scholarship eligibility; to provide for exceptions based on
8 previous participation in the dual enrollment program; to provide for related matters; to
9 repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
11 SECTION 1.
12 Code Section 20-3-519.2 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to eligibility
13 requirements for a HOPE scholarship and award amount, is amended by revising
14 subsection (d) as follows:
15 "(d) A student may receive the HOPE scholarship until the first of these events:

17 earned a baccalaureate or first professional degree;.

19 (A) of this paragraph shall not apply to any student:
20 (I) Who continuously meets the achievement standards provided for in this Code

23 (II) Who continuously met the achievement standards provided for in this Code

43 graduation from high school or the equivalent thereof as determined by the Georgia
44 Student Finance Commission in its rules and regulations; provided, however, that, for a
45 student who serves in the military during such ten-year period, any such active duty
46 military service shall not count against the ten-year period nor constitute a failure to be
47 enrolled. Any full-time or part-time student receiving a HOPE scholarship and enrolled
48 in an eligible postsecondary institution after June 30, 2019, shall remain eligible for a
49 HOPE scholarship pursuant to this paragraph, provided that such student meets all other
50 eligibility requirements, including, but not limited to, those set forth in paragraphs (1)
51 and (2) of this subsection. Students with a disability, as defined in the federal Americans
52 with Disabilities Act, 42 U.S.C. Section 12102, which prevents or substantially inhibits
53 full-time academic study, may apply to the Georgia Student Finance Commission for a
54 limited waiver from the applicable year limit set forth in this paragraph and request
55 additional time to complete the first to occur of the events set forth in paragraphs (1)
56 and (2) of this subsection. Such application for a limited waiver shall be considered and
57 determined by the Georgia Student Finance Commission in accordance with its rules and
58 regulations."
59 SECTION 2.
60 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
