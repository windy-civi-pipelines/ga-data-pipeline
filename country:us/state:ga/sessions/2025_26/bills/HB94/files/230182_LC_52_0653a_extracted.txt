25 LC 52 0653
By: Representatives Lumsden of the 12th, Taylor of the 173rd, Hawkins of the 27th, Cooper
of the 45th, and Silcox of the 53rd
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 24 of Title 33 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 insurance generally, so as to require coverage for certain expenses for standard fertility
3 preservation services when a medically necessary treatment for cancer, sickle cell disease,
4 or lupus may directly or indirectly cause an impairment of fertility; to provide for definitions;
5 to provide for exclusions; to allow for certain cost-sharing requirements; to provide for rules
6 and regulations; to provide for related matters; to provide for an effective date; to repeal
7 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
8 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
9 SECTION 1.
10 Chapter 24 of Title 33 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to insurance
11 generally, is amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
12 "33-24-59.34.
13 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
14 (1) 'Health benefit policy' means any individual or group plan, policy, or contract for
15 healthcare services issued, delivered, issued for delivery, or renewed in this state by an
16 insurer that provides major medical benefits, including those plans, policies, or contracts
H. B. 94

17 executed by the state on behalf of state employees under Article 1 of Chapter 18 of
18 Title 45. Such term shall not include self-funded, employer sponsored health insurance
19 plans subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the federal Employee Retirement Income
20 Security Act of 1974, as codified and amended at 29 U.S.C. Section 1001, et seq.
21 (2) 'Iatrogenic infertility' means an impairment of fertility caused directly or indirectly
22 by a medically necessary treatment for cancer, sickle cell disease, or lupus.
23 (3) 'Insurer' means any person, corporation, or other entity authorized to provide health
24 benefit policies under this title, including a healthcare corporation, health maintenance
25 organization, preferred provider organization, accident and sickness insurer, fraternal
26 benefit society, hospital service corporation, medical service corporation, or any similar
27 entity.
28 (4) 'Medically necessary treatment' means a medically necessary treatment for cancer,
29 sickle cell disease, or lupus that has a potential side effect of iatrogenic infertility. Such
30 treatment includes but is not limited to the surgical removal of the primary or secondary
31 reproductive organs, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and bone marrow transplantation.
32 (5) 'Standard fertility preservation services' means procedures to preserve fertility that
33 are consistent with established medical practices or professional guidelines. Such
34 services include but are not limited to egg, sperm, embryo, and ovarian tissue
35 cryopreservation.
36 (b) Every health benefit policy renewed or issued after January 1, 2026, shall include
37 coverage for expenses for standard fertility preservation services when a medically
38 necessary treatment may directly or indirectly cause iatrogenic infertility in any covered
39 person. Such coverage shall include evaluation expenses, laboratory assessments,
40 medications, and treatments associated with standard fertility preservation services,
41 including storage of gametes for up to one year.
42 (c) The coverage provided for in subsection (b) of this Code section may:
43 (1) Exclude costs associated with storage of gametes for more than one year;
H. B. 94
- 2 -
25 LC 52 0653
44 (2) Include age restrictions;
45 (3) Include a lifetime limit per procedure per eligible insured; and
46 (4) Be limited to nonexperimental procedures.
47 (d) The benefits in a health benefit policy as provided in subsection (b) of this Code
48 section shall be subject to the same deductibles, coinsurance, and copayment provisions
49 established for all covered benefits within such health benefit policy. Special deductibles,
50 coinsurance, copayment, or other limitations that are not generally applicable to other
51 hospital, medical, or surgical services covered by a health benefit policy shall not be
52 imposed on coverage for standard fertility preservation services.
53 (e) The Commissioner shall promulgate rules and regulations necessary to implement the
54 provisions of this Code section in accordance with current guidelines established by
55 professional medical organizations such as the American Society of Clinical Oncology or
56 the American Society for Reproductive Medicine."
57 SECTION 2.
58 This Act shall become effective upon its approval by the Governor or upon its becoming law
59 without such approval.
60 SECTION 3.
61 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
