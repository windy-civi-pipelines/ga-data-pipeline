25 LC 61 0261S
substitute to HB 750:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend an Act to create the State Court of Forsyth County, approved April 15, 1996 (Ga.
2 L. 1996, p. 4558), as amended, particularly by an Act approved May 1, 2002 (Ga. L. 2002,
3 p. 5579), so as to add a judge to the court; to provide for the selection of a chief judge; to
4 provide for duties of the chief judge; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting
5 laws; and for other purposes.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
7 SECTION 1.
8 An Act to create the State Court of Forsyth County, approved April 15, 1996 (Ga. L. 1996,
9 p. 4558), as amended, particularly by an Act approved May 1, 2002 (Ga. L. 2002, p. 5579),
10 is amended by revising subsection (a) of and adding new subsections to Section 3 to read as
11 follows:
12 "(a) Effective July 1, 2026, there shall be three judges of the State Court of Forsyth County
13 who shall be selected as provided in this section."
14 "(e) The third judge of the State Court of Forsyth County shall be appointed by the
15 Governor for an initial term of office beginning on July 1, 2026, and continuing through
16 December 31, 2028, and until his or her successor is elected and qualified. All future
- 1 -
25 LC 61 0261S
17 successors to said judge shall be elected by the qualified voters of Forsyth County at a
18 nonpartisan election to be held and conducted jointly with the general primary in 2028 and
19 quadrennially thereafter pursuant to Code Section 21-2-138 of the O.C.G.A. The judge
20 thus elected shall serve a four-year term and until his or her successor is elected and
21 qualified.
22 (f)(1) There is hereby created the position of chief judge of the State Court of Forsyth
23 County. The chief judge shall be the judge who has served the longest continuous period
24 of time in office as a judge of said court.
25 (2) The duties of the chief judge shall include:
26 (A) Regularly meeting with all judges of the court to discuss issues affecting the
27 courts, to collaborate on best practices regarding case management and sentencing, and
28 to improve operational efficiencies;
29 (B) In consultation with the chief judge of the Superior Court of Forsyth County,
30 managing the available courtroom space, including any courtrooms in ancillary
31 facilities, in order to maximize secure courtrooms for criminal trials and other criminal
32 proceedings, including assigning judges to a particular courtroom on a permanent or
33 temporary basis;
34 (C) In consultation with the chief judge of the Superior Court of Forsyth County,
35 annually identifying present and projected courtroom and administrative space and
36 other facility needs to the Forsyth County Board of Commissioners after consultation
37 with all of the judges of the court;
38 (D) Working with the court administrator, the county manager of Forsyth County, the
39 local governing authorities, and the Forsyth County Board of Commissioners to
40 eliminate duplication of functions and achieve greater operational efficiencies;
41 (E) Preparing and presenting the court's budget to state and local authorities after
42 conferring with all of the judges of the court;
- 2 -
25 LC 61 0261S
43 (F) After consulting with all of the judges of the court, establishing criteria and
44 qualifications for the employment, discipline, and dismissal of employees assigned to
45 the courts and each judge;
46 (G) Devising and implementing a personnel system that ensures fair and lawful
47 treatment of all court personnel, including judicial staff, while preserving the authority
48 of each judge to manage and direct his or her selected employees in accordance with
49 applicable guidelines;
50 (H) Managing assignments for visiting state court judges; and
51 (I) Performing all other duties required by law or that are necessary or convenient for
52 performing the duties set forth in this subsection.
53 (3) The chief judge shall be permitted to obtain assistance from the other judges of the
54 court and from judicial staff to perform the duties set forth in this subsection."
55 SECTION 2.
56 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
