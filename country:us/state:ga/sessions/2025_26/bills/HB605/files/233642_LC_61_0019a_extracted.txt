Section 1:
25 LC 61 0019
By: Representatives Lim of the 98th, Roberts of the 52nd, and Lupton of the 83rd
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 1 of Chapter 2A of Title 31 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to general provisions related to the Department of Public Health, so as to require the
3 Department of Public Health to continue operation of the Georgia Tobacco Quit Line, subject
4 to appropriations or the availability of other funds; to provide for definitions; to provide
5 requirements for the Quit Line; to provide that the Quit Line will provide information about,
6 evaluation for, and referrals to lung cancer screening services; to provide for the utilization
7 and security of any data collected by the Quit Line; to provide for the creation of written
8 materials with information about the Quit Line by the Department of Public Health and to
9 require certain sellers of tobacco products to display and distribute such written materials;
10 to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
12 SECTION 1.
13 Article 1 of Chapter 2A of Title 31 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
14 general provisions related to the Department of Public Health, is amended by adding a new
15 Code section to read as follows:
H. B. 605
- 1 -
25 LC 61 0019
16 "31-2A-21.
17 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
18 (1) 'Alternative nicotine product', 'cigarette', 'tobacco product', 'tobacco related object',
19 and 'vapor product' shall have the same meanings as set forth in Code Section 16-12-170.
20 (2) 'Quit Line' means the Georgia Tobacco Quit Line operated by the Department of
21 Public Health pursuant to this Code section.
22 (b) Subject to appropriations or the availability of other funds, the department shall
23 continue to operate, maintain, and make available a public health service to help Georgians
24 quit smoking, vaping, and using all forms of tobacco products, known as the Georgia
25 Tobacco Quit Line. The department may contract for the design, operation, and
26 maintenance of the Quit Line.
27 (c) The Quit Line shall be available 24 hours a day, seven days a week. The Quit Line
28 shall:
29 (1) Provide cessation services that address the use of all tobacco products;
30 (2) Provide telephone, text, and internet based services for users of tobacco products,
31 vapor products, and alternative nicotine products, provided that such users are 13 years
32 of age or older;
33 (3) Provide services and support for pregnant and postpartum women who use tobacco
34 products, vapor products, or alternative nicotine products;
35 (4) Provide support in multiple languages;
36 (5) Provide referrals to community resources, when applicable;
37 (6) Provide free supplies of nicotine replacement therapies, such as gum or patches, for
38 qualified Georgia residents 18 years of age and older;
39 (7) Provide information about lung cancer screening to individuals accessing the Quit
40 Line, evaluate lung cancer screening eligibility for individuals accessing the Quit Line,
41 and provide referrals to lung cancer screening services, when applicable;
42 (8) Follow national guidelines for treating tobacco use and dependence;
H. B. 605
- 2 -
25 LC 61 0019
43 (9) Implement practices to comply with all applicable laws respecting individual and
44 patient privacy;
45 (10) Implement practices to ensure the security of any data collected, if applicable, in
46 line with industry best practices and in compliance with all applicable laws; and
47 (11) Utilize de-identified data to track the success of the Quit Line's operations and
48 identify trends in service needs and outcomes.
49 (d) The department shall design appropriate written materials, including signage and
50 materials for distribution by retailers to individuals who purchase cigarettes, tobacco
51 products, vapor products, or alternative nicotine products, to inform individuals about the
52 Quit Line. Such written materials shall include:
53 (1) Information on the services provided by the Quit Line, including information about
54 services related to lung cancer screening;
55 (2) Information on the effectiveness of and eligibility for lung cancer screening; and
56 (3) Contact information for the Quit Line, such as a telephone number or a public
57 website.
58 (e)(1) Any person owning or operating a place of business in which cigarettes, tobacco
59 products, tobacco related objects, vapor products, or alternative nicotine products are sold
60 or offered for sale shall post in a conspicuous place signage designed by the department
61 pursuant to subsection (d) of this Code section.
62 (2) Any person provided for in paragraph (1) of this subsection shall distribute to
63 individuals purchasing cigarettes, tobacco products, vapor products, or alternative
64 nicotine products the written materials designed by the department pursuant to subsection
65 (d) of this Code section."
66 SECTION 2.
67 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
