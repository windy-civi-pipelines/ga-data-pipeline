25 LC 129 0020
Senate Resolution 12
the 29th, Walker III of the 20th and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Honoring and celebrating the life and legacy of Bruce A. Thompson; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, the State of Georgia celebrates the extraordinary life and enduring legacy of
3 Bruce A. Thompson, a visionary leader, devoted mentor, and dedicated public servant whose
4 entrepreneurial spirit and unwavering commitment to excellence will continue to inspire and
5 shape the future for generations of Georgians to come; and
6 WHEREAS, Bruce Thompson was born in California and raised on a farm in Montana,
7 where he embraced the values of hard work and perseverance from an early age; and
8 WHEREAS, these principals earned him a college wrestling scholarship and laid the
9 foundation for a lifetime of service, beginning with six years in the United States Army
10 National Guard; and
11 WHEREAS, a natural entrepreneur, Bruce launched his first business at the age of 10 and
12 went on to build a remarkable portfolio of ventures, including two automatic pool cover
13 companies, six insurance agencies, two funeral homes, and several software firms; and
S. R. 12
- 1 -
25 LC 129 0020
14 WHEREAS, he moved to Georgia in 1990 and immersed himself in his community, serving
15 his church, mentoring future leaders, and contributing to organizations such as the SBA
16 Advisory Board, Truett McConnell University, and the Etowah Scholarship Foundation; and
17 WHEREAS, Bruce began his legislative career in 2013 as a Georgia State Senator, where he
18 chaired several committees and authored landmark legislation, including the Heartbeat Bill,
19 the Dexter Mosley Act, and the Carnival Safety Act, earning recognition as "Legislator of
20 the Year" many times; and
21 WHEREAS, known as the "turnaround specialist," Bruce's passion for problem solving
22 inspired his successful run as Georgia's tenth Labor Commissioner; and
23 WHEREAS, his tenure at the Georgia Department of Labor was marked by a renewed focus
24 on transparency, responsiveness, and fiscal responsibility; values he championed with his
25 trademark determination and integrity; and
26 WHEREAS, with the spirit of a fighter and the compassion borne of strong faith, Bruce was
27 known as a mentor to many in the business community and political arena; and
28 WHEREAS, Bruce A. Thompson courageously lost his eight-month-long battle with
29 pancreatic cancer on November 24, 2024, leaving behind a powerful legacy of leadership and
30 service; and
31 WHEREAS, Bruce cherished his role as father and often spoke of his gratitude for the
32 privilege of raising his son, Max, and his daughter, Faith, alongside his wife of nearly 28
33 years, Becky; and
S. R. 12
- 2 -
25 LC 129 0020
34 WHEREAS, a devoted believer in Jesus Christ, Bruce lived his life guided by faith,
35 embodying the words of Psalm 138:1 even in the most difficult times.
36 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
37 join in honoring the extraordinary life and leadership of Bruce A. Thompson, celebrating his
38 profound contributions to the State of Georgia, and expressing deepest condolences to his
39 family, friends, and all who were blessed to know him.
40 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
41 to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the family of
42 Bruce A. Thompson.
