Section 1:
25 LC 62 0204S
substitute to HB 200:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 2 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 elementary and secondary education, so as to provide for the use of local school system
3 facilities by certain youth groups; to provide for a definition; to require that each new
4 elementary school constructed on or after July 1, 2027, shall include a playground meeting
5 certain standards; to provide playground design standards and minimum guidelines; to
6 provide for the State Board of Education to establish a three-year pilot immersive writing
7 program for eligible public elementary schools; to provide for eligibility; to provide for grant
8 funds for eligible public elementary schools to develop and implement immersive writing
9 programs for students in grades two through five; to provide for applications and selection
10 to participate in the pilot program; to provide for assessment and reporting on the impacts
11 of the pilot program; to authorize the State Board of Education to promulgate rules and
12 regulations to implement the pilot program; to provide for automatic repeal; to provide for
13 related matters; to provide for an effective date; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other
14 purposes.
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
- 1 -
25 LC 62 0204S
16 SECTION 1.
17 Chapter 2 of Title 20 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to elementary and
18 secondary education, is amended in Code Section 20-2-261, relating to common minimum
19 facility requirements, by revising subsection (a) as follows:
20 "(a) The State Board of Education shall establish common minimum facility requirements
21 which each public school facility must shall meet in order to be certified for use in any
22 component of the educational or recreational program of that such school. Such minimum
23 requirements shall:
24 (1) Include such include those provisions of law or state board policy on matters that
25 relate to:
26 (A) Fire fire and physical safety;
27 (B) Sanitation sanitation and health, including temperature and ventilation;
28 (C) Minimum minimum space, size, and configuration for the various components of
29 the instructional program; and
30 (D) Construction construction stability, quality, and suitability for intended uses;. Such
31 minimum requirements shall not
32 (2) Not prohibit wood construction that is otherwise in compliance with state minimum
33 standard codes as they existed on January 1, 2014. As used in this subsection, the term
34 'state minimum standard codes' shall have the same meaning as in paragraph (9) of Code
35 Section 8-2-20; and
36 (3) Provide that the construction of each new elementary school commenced on or after
37 July 1, 2027, shall include a playground which meets the following design standards:
38 (A) Provides a sensory-diverse environment that enables children of all abilities to
39 develop physically, socially, and emotionally;
40 (B) Provides an engaging play space with age and ability-appropriate levels of
41 challenge and offers opportunities for children of all abilities to succeed, regardless of
42 physical and intellectual attributes;
- 2 -
25 LC 62 0204S
43 (C) Enables all students to benefit from state mandated recess time for elementary
44 students;
45 (D) Capable for use as an outdoor classroom that is open to all students; and
46 (E) Meets the following minimum guidelines:
47 (i) Playground is accessible via a sidewalk or pathway from the school;
48 (ii) Playground is a minimum of 10,000 square feet in area;
49 (iii) Fencing surrounds and encloses the entire playground area;
50 (iv) A minimum of 20 percent of the total playground area shall be shaded; provided,
51 however, that such shaded area may comprise more than one area so long as the total
52 shaded area meets or exceeds 20 percent of the total playground area; and, provided,
53 further, that such shading may be provided by natural means, such as existing mature
54 trees, or man-made means, such as sun canopies;
55 (v) Unitary surfacing shall be used throughout at least 80 percent of the playground
56 area. Examples of unitary surfacing include, but are not limited to, turf, bonded
57 rubber, and poured in place rubber; and
58 (vi) Components that are installed on a portion of the playground with the unitary
59 surfacing and accessible for all users that address the physical, sensory, cognitive,
60 social, emotional, imaginative, and communication needs of those who will visit the
61 playground, including, but not limited to:
62 (I) One communication board;
63 (II) One learning American Sign Language (ASL) alphabet panel or one learning
64 Braille alphabet panel;
65 (III) One roller slide;
66 (IV) A minimum of two pieces of equipment that make music, such as chimes,
67 drums, and bells;
68 (V) A minimum of two adaptive or accessible swings;
- 3 -
25 LC 62 0204S
69 (VI) A minimum of one play piece accessible to a child remaining in a wheelchair
70 that allows such child to play with other children;
71 (VII) A minimum of four sensory panels; and
72 (VIII) A minimum of one wheelchair accessible raised structure or wheelchair
73 accessible slide."
74 SECTION 2.
75 Said chapter is further amended by designating Code Sections 20-2-330 through 20-2-334
76 as Part 1 and by adding a new part to read as follows:
77 "Part 2
78 20-2-335.
79 (a)(1) The State Board of Education shall establish a pilot immersive writing program
80 for students in grades two through five in order to:
81 (A) Provide funding for all public school students in grades two through five to
82 participate in programs that provide for immersive writing experiences that are aligned
83 with writing standards established by the State Board of Education and can be
84 integrated into existing curricula;
85 (B) Improve literacy skills of elementary school students and motivate them to engage
86 in writing projects; and
87 (C) Study the impacts of such pilot writing program.
88 (2) Subject to appropriations by the General Assembly, the pilot program shall include
89 funding for up to $2 million in grants each school year, for up to three school years
90 beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, to participating public elementary schools.
91 (3) To be eligible to participate in the pilot program, a public elementary school shall:
92 (A) Serve students in any grades two through five; and
- 4 -
25 LC 62 0204S
93 (B) By January 1, 2026, submit an application to participate in the pilot program,
94 which application shall include such school's statement of intent for the use of grant
95 funds.
96 (4) The State Board of Education shall establish pilot program requirements and criteria
97 for selecting eligible public schools to participate in the pilot program in accordance with
98 the provisions of this Code section.
99 (5) The State Board of Education shall be authorized to establish reporting requirements
100 for the public schools participating in the pilot program.
101 (6) The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules and regulations necessary to
102 carry out the provisions of this Code section.
103 (b)(1) By October 1, 2025, the Department of Education shall develop an application
104 process and seek applications from eligible public schools for the pilot program to be
105 submitted no later than January 1, 2026.
106 (2)(A) The Department of Education shall monitor and annually assess the status of the
107 pilot program with cooperation and input from the public schools and local school
108 systems participating in the pilot program.
109 (B) Beginning no later than June 1, 2027, and by June 1 of each year thereafter, the
110 Department of Education shall provide to the State Board of Education a report of the
111 annual assessment provided for in subparagraph (A) of this paragraph.
112 (c) This Code section shall stand repealed in its entirety on July 1, 2029."
113 SECTION 3.
114 Said chapter is further amended by revising Code Section 20-2-12, which is reserved, as
115 follows:
116 "20-2-12.
117 (a) As used in this Code section, the term 'patriotic society' means a youth group listed in
118 Title 36 of the United States Code as a patriotic society, including the Boy Scouts of
- 5 -
25 LC 62 0204S
119 America and its affiliated Georgia groups and councils and the Girl Scouts of the United
120 States of America and its affiliated Georgia groups and councils.
121 (b) To encourage schools to facilitate access for students to participate in activities
122 provided by a patriotic society for the purposes of encouraging civic education, each local
123 school system that uses state funds to implement programs pursuant to this chapter is
124 encouraged to permit the use of such local school system's facilities by a patriotic society.
125 When a local school system denies a patriotic society access to a facility of such system,
126 the principal of the school where such facility is located shall provide in writing reasons
127 for the denial to a requesting entity. Reserved."
128 SECTION 4.
129 This Act shall become effective on July 1, 2025.
130 SECTION 5.
131 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
