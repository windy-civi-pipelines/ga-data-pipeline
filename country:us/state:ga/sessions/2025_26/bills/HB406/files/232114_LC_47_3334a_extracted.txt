Section 1:
25 LC 47 3334
By: Representative Leverett of the 123rd
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 23 of Title 47 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to the
2 Georgia Judicial Retirement System, so as to increase the retirement age of superior court
3 judges first taking office on or after July 1, 2026; to provide for an effective date; to provide
4 for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
6 SECTION 1.
7 Chapter 23 of Title 47 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to the Georgia
8 Judicial Retirement System, is amended by revising Code Section 47-23-102, relating to
9 vesting, benefits upon retirement, and compliance with federal income tax laws, as follows:
10 "47-23-102.
11 (a) The right of a member to receive benefits under this chapter shall vest after the member
12 obtains ten years of creditable service; provided, however, that no member shall receive
13 a retirement benefit prior to attaining the age of 60 years. Except as otherwise provided
14 in Article 2 of Chapter 1 of this title, a member's accumulated contributions shall be 100
15 percent vested and nonforfeitable at all times. Any member retiring on or after
16 July 1, 1996, and any member who was retired on July 1, 1996, with 16 years or more of

17 creditable service shall receive a benefit equal to 66.66 percent, plus 1 percent for each year
18 of creditable service over 16 years, of the member's salary; provided, however, that no
19 member shall receive more than 24 years of creditable service. Any member retiring with
20 less than 16 years of creditable service may retire at a reduced benefit pursuant to Code
21 Section 47-23-103. Except as provided in subsection (c) of this Code section, normal
22 Normal retirement age under this retirement system shall be the date the member has
23 reached age 60 years of age, provided that he or she has at least ten years of creditable
24 service. For purposes of Section 402(1) of the federal Internal Revenue Code regarding
25 distributions from governmental plans for health and long-term care insurance for public
26 safety officers, normal retirement age shall be the earliest date when the member has
27 satisfied the requirements for a retirement under this or the predecessor retirement system.
28 Except as otherwise provided in Article 2 of Chapter 1 of this title, a member's right to his
29 or her retirement allowance is nonforfeitable upon attainment of normal retirement age.
30 Any member who was retired on July 1, 1996, with more than 16 years of creditable
31 service shall receive in July, 1998, a one-time benefit payment equal to two times the
32 product of 1 percent of the salary paid to such judge at the time of his or her retirement
33 multiplied by the number of years of creditable service in excess of 16 years.
34 (b) The board is authorized to provide by rule or regulation for the payment of benefits to
35 members or beneficiaries of the retirement system at a time and under circumstances not
36 provided for in this chapter to the extent that such payment is required to maintain the
37 retirement system as a qualified retirement plan for the purposes of federal income tax laws
38 and regulations.
39 (c) For any superior court judge first taking office on or after July 1, 2026, the normal
40 retirement age under this retirement system shall be the date the member has reached 65
41 years of age. All other requirements to receive a retirement benefit under this Code section
42 shall apply to such superior court judge."

43 SECTION 2.
44 Said chapter is further amended by revising Code Section 47-23-103, relating to retirement
45 based on age and application to retire, as follows:
46 "47-23-103.
47 (a) In lieu of retirement at the benefit level provided by Code Section 47-23-102, a
48 member, except for any superior court judge first taking office on or after July 1, 2026,
49 may retire at any time after attaining the age of 60 years and after obtaining a minimum of
50 ten years of creditable service. The monthly retirement benefit for such early retirement
51 shall be a percentage of the benefit under Code Section 47-23-102, and such percentage
52 shall be the proportion which the number of years of creditable service the member has in
53 the retirement system bears to 16.
54 (b) The effective date of retirement shall be the first day of the month in which the
55 application is received by the board of trustees, provided that no retirement shall, in any
56 case, be effective earlier than the first day of the month following the final month of the
57 applicant's employment. Applications for retirement shall not be accepted more than 90
58 days in advance of the effective date of retirement."
59 SECTION 3.
60 This Act shall become effective on July 1, 2026.
61 SECTION 4.
62 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
