House Resolution 797
By: Representative Jackson of the 128th
A RESOLUTION
2 WHEREAS, at the early age of 10 years old, Janice Reed began to produce and promote
3 entertainment such as festivals, carnivals, and other community events in Chicago; and
4 WHEREAS, upon moving to Atlanta in 1975, Ms. Reed began producing entertainment
5 events for the late Reverend Hosea L. Williams of Hosea Feed the Hungry and Homeless,
6 a dedicated musical pursuit she continues to this day; and
7 WHEREAS, additionally, she provides family oriented, specialty acts such as magicians,
8 clowns, face painters, stilt walkers, dance and mime teams as well as choirs, orchestras,
9 comedians, and drum teams throughout the state; and
10 WHEREAS, Ms. Reed is also the co-founder of the Sweet Auburn Festival, which began in
11 1982, and a dedicated community activist; and
12 WHEREAS, she is a person of magnanimous strengths with an unimpeachable reputation for
13 integrity, intelligence, fairness, and kindness; and
