By: Senators Albers of the 56th, Robertson of the 29th, Walker III of the 20th, Esteves of the
35th, Anavitarte of the 31st and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to alcoholic beverages,
2 so as to provide for regulation of the manufacture, distribution, and sale of malt beverages;
3 to provide for definitions; to authorize manufacturers of malt beverages to sell or donate malt
4 beverages they produce, subject to certain limitations and conditions; to provide for direct
5 donation of malt beverages by brewers and brewpubs to certain charitable events; to provide
6 that sales for consumption off the premises are not subject to a daily maximum; to permit
7 small brewers to sell a limited quantity of malt beverages at wholesale; to provide for
8 remittance of excise and use taxes on donated malt beverages; to provide for a short title; to
9 provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
11 SECTION 1.
12 This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Craft Beer and Local Economy
13 Revitalization Act."
S. B. 122
- 1 -
25 LC 55 0461
14 SECTION 2.
15 Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to alcoholic beverages, is
16 amended by adding a new paragraph to Code Section 3-1-2, relating to definitions, to read
17 as follows:
18 "(20.1) 'Small brewer' means a brewer or brewpub whose sales of products to such
19 brewer's or brewpub's wholesaler licensed under this title, if any, do not exceed 15
20 percent of such wholesaler's total sales in the prior calendar year."
21 SECTION 3.
22 Said title is further amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
23 "3-5-6.
24 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
25 (1) 'Charitable event permit holder' means a bona fide nonprofit civic organization that
26 has been issued a permit pursuant to Code Section 3-9-3 for the sale of alcoholic
27 beverages for consumption on the premises.
28 (2) 'Permitted charitable event' means an event for which a permit has been obtained by
29 a bona fide nonprofit civic organization pursuant to Code Section 3-9-3 authorizing such
30 organization to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
31 (b) A brewer or brewpub may directly donate malt beverages it manufactures to a
32 charitable event permit holder for a permitted charitable event, provided that:
33 (1) The amount of such donated malt beverages shall not exceed the amount necessary
34 for the permitted charitable event;
35 (2) Any donated malt beverages that are not used or consumed during the permitted
36 charitable event shall be returned to the brewer or brewpub within 24 hours of the event
37 closure or the expiration of the charitable event permit, whichever is later; and

39 event by the charitable event permit holder or the brewer or brewpub that made the

41 SECTION 4.
42 Said title is further amended by revising subsection (b) of Code Section 3-5-24.1, relating to
43 exception to three-tier system, transfer of liquids, sales at brewer's licensed premises, taxing,
44 regulatory authority, and conditions or limitations, as follows:
45 "(b) A limited exception to the provisions of this title providing a three-tier system for the
46 distribution and sale of malt beverages shall exist to the extent that the license to
47 manufacture malt beverages in this state shall include the right to sell malt beverages to
48 individuals on the brewer's licensed premises for personal use and not for resale, subject
49 to the following terms and conditions:
50 (1) The brewer may only make sales of malt beverages to an individual while the
51 individual is physically on the brewer's licensed premises where the brewer produces malt
52 beverages;
53 (2) The brewer may make sales of malt beverages the brewer produces at the brewer's
54 licensed premises where the individual is purchasing the malt beverages;
55 (3) As long as the brewer and all of the brewer's licensed premises are under common
56 ownership, the brewer may make sales of malt beverages the brewer produces at any
57 licensed premises of the brewer and subsequently transfers in compliance with the
58 limitations and reporting obligations of subsection (c) of this Code section to the brewer's
59 licensed premises for sale where the individual is purchasing the malt beverages;
60 (4) The brewer may only make sales of malt beverages for which the brewer is the sole
61 owner of the brand and brand label;
62 (5) Sales for consumption on the premises are not subject to a daily maximum amount;

64 of malt beverages per individual per day are not subject to a daily maximum amount; and
65 (7) The maximum amount of malt beverages the brewer may sell pursuant to this
66 subsection (b) of this Code section in each calendar year shall be 6,000 barrels in the
67 aggregate among all brewer's licensed premises making such sales."
68 SECTION 5.
69 Said title is further amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
70 "3-5-24.2.
71 (a) Notwithstanding the provisions of Code Section 3-5-24.1 and as a limited exception
72 to the provisions of this title providing a three-tier system for the distribution and sale of
73 malt beverages, the license to manufacture malt beverages in this state shall include for
74 small brewers the right to:
75 (1) Sell per calendar year up to 3,000 barrels of malt beverages produced at the small
76 brewer's licensed premises at wholesale to retailers licensed in this state that are located
77 within a 100 mile radius of the small brewer's licensed premises;
78 (2) Sell, deliver, and ship its malt beverages to other licensed brewers and brewpubs; and
79 (3) Acquire and receive deliveries and shipments of such malt beverages made by other
80 licensed brewers and brewpubs.
81 (b) The commissioner shall promulgate and enforce such rules and regulations as he or she
82 may deem reasonable and necessary to effectuate the provisions of this Code section."

93 SECTION 7.

98 (a) The excise taxes provided for in this part shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by
99 the licensed wholesale dealer in malt beverages; provided, however, that such taxes shall
100 be imposed upon and shall be paid by the licensed brewer or brewpub for malt beverages
101 served or sold by the brewer directly to the public pursuant to Code Section 3-5-24.1 or
102 donated by the brewer or brewpub pursuant to Code Section 3-5-6.
103 (b) The taxes shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar
104 month in which the beverages are sold, donated, or disposed of within the particular
105 municipality or county.
106 (c) Each licensee responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report itemizing
107 for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages, by size and type
108 of container, sold or donated during the month within each municipality or county. The
109 licensee shall file the report with each municipality or county wherein the beverages are
