Section 1:
25 LC 39 4805S
substitute to HB 254:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Part 2 of Article 4 of Chapter 13 of Title 45 and Chapter 3 of Title 50 of the
2 Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to the Capitol Art Standards Commission and
3 state flag, seal, and other symbols, respectively, so as to provide for duties and
4 responsibilities of the commission; to provide for the placement of a monument in honor of
5 the Honorable Clarence Thomas within the Nathan Deal Judicial Center; to provide for
6 placement of a Revolutionary War Patriots marker upon the grounds of the state capitol
7 building and upon the grounds of the state archives building; to provide for the approval,
8 placement, and procurement of such markers; to provide for related matters; to repeal
9 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
11 SECTION 1.
12 Part 2 of Article 4 of Chapter 13 of Title 45 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
13 relating to the Capitol Art Standards Commission, is amended in Code Section 45-13-71,
14 relating to duties and responsibilities of commission, by adding a new paragraph to read as
15 follows:
- 1 -
25 LC 39 4805S
16 "(5.1) To oversee the design, procurement, and placement of a monument honoring the
17 Honorable Clarence Thomas pursuant to Code Section 50-3-130;"
18 SECTION 2.
19 Chapter 3 of Title 50 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to state flag, seal,
20 and other symbols, is amended by adding new articles to read as follows:
21 "ARTICLE 8
22 50-3-130.
23 (a) Subject to the availability of funds, there shall be placed within the Nathan Deal
24 Judicial Center a monument honoring the Honorable Clarence Thomas.
25 (b)(1) Such monument shall be designed, procured, and placed by the Capitol Art
26 Standards Commission, subject to final approval by a monument committee composed
27 of the following members of the General Assembly:
28 (A) Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the
29 House of Representatives;
30 (B) Two members of the Senate appointed by the Lieutenant Governor; and
31 (C) One member from each house of the General Assembly appointed by the
32 Governor.
33 (2) The monument committee established pursuant to this subsection shall stand
34 abolished upon placement of such monument.
35 (c) No public funds shall be expended for the design or procurement of such monument.
36 Gifts and donations from private individuals, organizations, or foundations shall be
37 accepted and expended by the Capitol Art Standards Commission to carry out the
38 requirements of this Code section.
- 2 -
25 LC 39 4805S
39 (d) Such monument shall be procured and placed as soon as practicable but not before the
40 state has been granted any intellectual property license necessary for the purposes of this
41 Code section.
42 ARTICLE 9
43 50-3-140.
44 (a) There shall be placed upon the grounds of the state capitol building and upon the
45 grounds outside the state archives building a marker which commemorates the patriots of
46 the Revolutionary War, honoring the men and women who helped America achieve
47 independence. Each such marker shall honor the two-hundred and fiftieth anniversary of
48 the United States and those who fought for the noble cause of liberty and for a new nation.
49 (b) Placement location for Revolutionary War Patriots markers provided for in this Code
50 section shall be subject to approval by the Capitol Arts Standards Commission for
51 placement upon the grounds of the state capitol building and by the Board of Regents for
52 the placement of the marker upon the grounds outside the state archives building.
53 (c) No public funds shall be expended for the design or procurement of the markers
54 provided for in this Code section. Each such marker shall be placed as soon as practicable."
55 SECTION 3.
56 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
