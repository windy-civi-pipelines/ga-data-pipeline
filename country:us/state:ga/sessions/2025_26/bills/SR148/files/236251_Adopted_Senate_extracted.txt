25 LC 112 3111S (SCS)
Senate Resolution 148
James of the 28th and others
ADOPTED SENATE
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing and commending Taiwan for its relations with the United States and the State
2 of Georgia; and for other purposes.
3 WHEREAS, Taiwan and the United States of America are long-standing allies who both
4 deeply cherish the common values of democracy, peace, and prosperity; and
5 WHEREAS, Taiwan continues to pursue integrated diplomacy and serve the best interests
6 of the Indo-Pacific region by deepening ties with other like-minded nations, safeguarding
7 regional security, and utilizing Taiwan's technological and economic success to promote
8 partnerships on mutual benefits; and
9 WHEREAS, Taiwan is the United States' eighth-largest trading partner, and the United States
10 is Taiwan's second-largest trading partner, with an estimated $128 billion worth of trade;
11 Taiwan was Georgia's 15th largest import and 17th largest export market in 2023; and
12 WHEREAS, the United States and Taiwan's first agreement entered into force under the
13 "US-Taiwan Initiative on 21st-Century Trade," which includes commitments on

14 anti-corruption, good regulatory practices, customs administration and trade facilitation, and
15 small- and medium-sized enterprises; and
16 WHEREAS, the Taiwanese government donated $200,000 for Hurricane Helene relief to
17 Georgia, and Taiwan's National Day was joyously celebrated at the Georgia Aquarium last
18 October with more than 250 distinguished guests, including government officials,
19 entrepreneurs, and leaders from the Taiwanese community; this event emphasized the
20 celebration of the Taiwan Relations Act's 45th anniversary as well as symbolized the strong
21 connection between Taiwan and Georgia.
22 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
23 support constant talks under the "US-Taiwan Initiative on 21st-Century Trade" and a
24 "US-Taiwan Avoid Double Taxation Agreement," as Taiwanese companies are looking for
25 a diverse and free market investment in the United States, as well as encourage the State of
26 Georgia to establish a trade office in Taiwan as 23 other states and Guam have done;
27 organize a trade delegation to Taiwan to attract more investments; and encourage city
28 governments to participate in the annual "Smart City" forum in Taiwan.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that with the celebration of Taiwan's democracy and the
30 reaffirmation of the United States' commitment to Taiwan, the members of this body support
31 the Taiwan Caucus as a means to explore more collaborative opportunities with Taiwan as
32 well as support Taiwan's meaningful participation in international organizations and
33 economic cooperations such as WHO, UNFCCC, ICAO, INTERPOL, CPTPP, and IPEF.
34 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of this body support the signing of an
