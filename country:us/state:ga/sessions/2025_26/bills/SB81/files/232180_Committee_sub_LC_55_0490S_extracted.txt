substitute to SB 81:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 22 of Chapter 1 of Title 10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to motor vehicle franchise practices, so as provide for protection of consumer data
3 in motor vehicle sales or lease transactions by affiliates of franchisors, manufacturers, and
4 distributors; to provide for procedures, conditions, and limitations on the use of motor
5 vehicle dealer data; to provide for standards for access and integration of data collected and
6 maintained by dealers and by third parties on behalf of dealers; to provide for applicability;
7 to provide for requirements of franchisors, manufacturers, and distributors in relation to
8 dealers; to provide for additional unlawful activities by franchisors; to revise legislative
9 findings; to provide for definitions; to amend Code Section 40-2-39 of the Official Code of
10 Georgia Annotated, relating to registration and licensing of new motor vehicle dealers,
11 temporary site permits, administrative fines, and penalty, so as to provide for activities which
12 qualify as activity as a new motor vehicle dealer; to provide for related matters; to repeal
13 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:

25 LC 55 0490S
15 SECTION 1.
16 Article 22 of Chapter 1 of Title 10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
17 motor vehicle franchise practices, is amended by revising Code Section 10-1-621, relating
18 to legislative findings, as follows:
19 "10-1-621.
20 The General Assembly finds and declares that:
21 (1) The distribution and sale of motor vehicles within this state are vital to the general
22 economy of this state and to the public interest and public welfare;
23 (2) The provision for warranty service, recall service, and the repair of predelivery
24 transportation damages to motor vehicles is of substantial concern to the people of this
25 state;
26 (3) Robust competition among dealers benefits consumers through competitive pricing,
27 service, and convenient repair facilities, thus the The maintenance of full and fair
28 inter-brand and intra-brand competition among dealers and others is in the public interest;
29 and
30 (4) The maintenance of strong and sound dealerships is essential to provide continuing
31 and necessary reliable services to the consuming public in this state, to the introduction
32 of new automotive technology and support and maintenance of such after a sale, to
33 protect against the creation or perpetuation of monopolies and other practices that are
34 detrimental to public welfare and local businesses, and to provide stable employment to
35 the citizens of this state."

112 SECTION 3.
113 Said article is further amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
114 "10-1-633.
115 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
116 (1) 'Authorized integrator' means a third party with whom a dealer has entered into a
117 written contract to perform a specific function which requires such third party to access
118 protected dealer data or write data to a dealer data system or both.
- 5 -
25 LC 55 0490S
119 (2) 'Data access overcharge' means any charge to a dealer or person authorized to
120 perform integration that is greater than an amount which reimburses direct costs incurred
121 by a dealer data systems vendor for integration. When a dealer data systems vendor fails
122 to disclose the direct costs of integration and provide documentation of such costs, any
123 direct costs sought to be reimbursed shall be considered a data access overcharge.
124 (3) 'Dealer data system' means any software or hardware provided by a third party and
125 used by a dealer in its business operations to store, process, or maintain protected dealer
126 data.
127 (4) 'Dealer data systems vendor' means any third party that stores protected dealer data
128 pursuant to written contract with a dealer and shall include a dealer management system
129 or customer relations management system provided by a third party. Such term shall not
130 include a manufacturer, distributor, or affiliate subject to Code Section 10-1-632.
131 (5) 'Integration' means access by an authorized integrator to protected dealer data which
132 does not require access to any copyright protected material but allows for access to all
133 protected dealer data and which can be accomplished by any commercially reasonable
134 means not otherwise in violation of this Code section.
135 (6) 'Prior express written consent' means written consent provided by the dealer that is
136 contained in a document separate from any other consent, contract, franchise agreement,
137 or other writing and that specifically outlines a dealer's consent for an authorized
138 integrator to obtain protected dealer data, including the scope and duration of such
139 consent, and may be unilaterally revoked by the dealer upon 30 days' notice without
140 cause and immediately with cause.
141 (7) 'Protected dealer data' means any of the following data that is stored in a dealer data
142 system:
143 (A) Personal, financial, or other data pertaining to a consumer that is provided to a
144 dealer by a consumer;
145 (B) Motor vehicle diagnostic data; or
- 6 -
25 LC 55 0490S
146 (C) Any other data relating to the business operations of a dealer stored or maintained
147 within a dealer data system.
148 (8) 'Secure open application programming interface' or 'secure open API' means an
149 application programming interface that allows an authorized integrator to integrate with
150 a dealer data system remotely and securely.
151 (9) 'STAR standards' means standards for the automotive retail industry established by
152 the Standards for Technology in Automotive Retail organization.
153 (10) 'Third party' includes service providers, vendors, including dealer data systems
154 vendors and authorized integrators, and any other individual or entity other than a dealer.
155 Such term shall not include any manufacturer; distributor; affiliate of a manufacturer or
156 distributor; a third party acting on behalf of or engaged contractually or by other
157 agreement with a manufacturer or distributor or an affiliate thereof; any governmental
158 entity acting pursuant to federal, state, or local law; or any third party acting pursuant to
159 a valid court order.
160 (b) It shall be unlawful for a third party to:
161 (1) Access, share, sell, copy, use, or transmit protected dealer data from a dealer data
162 system without the prior express written consent of a dealer;
163 (2) Take any action, by contract, technical means, or otherwise, that would prohibit or
164 limit a dealer's ability to protect, store, copy, share, or use any protected dealer data,
165 including, but not limited to:
166 (A) Imposing any data access overcharges or other restrictions of any kind on the
167 dealer or any authorized integrator for integration;
168 (B) Prohibiting an authorized integrator or any third party identified by a dealer as an
169 authorized integrator that has satisfied or is compliant with the STAR standards, or a
170 standard compatible with such standards, from performing integration or placing
171 unreasonable restrictions on integration, which shall include, but not be limited to:
- 7 -
25 LC 55 0490S
172 (i) Unreasonable restrictions on the scope or nature of the data shared with an
173 authorized integrator;
174 (ii) Unreasonable restrictions on the ability of an authorized integrator to write data
175 to a dealer data system;
176 (iii) Unreasonable restrictions or conditions on a third party accessing or sharing
177 protected dealer data or writing data to a dealer data system; and
178 (iv) Requiring unreasonable access to sensitive, competitive, or other confidential
179 business information of a third party as a condition for access to protected dealer data
180 or as a condition to share protected dealer data with an authorized integrator;
181 (C) Prohibiting or limiting a dealer's ability to store, copy, securely share, or use
182 protected dealer data outside the dealer data system in any manner and for any reason;
183 or
184 (D) Permitting access to or accessing protected dealer data without prior express
185 written consent by the dealer.
186 (c)(1) A secure open application programming interface shall:
187 (A) Be made available by a dealer data systems vendor to any authorized integrator
188 upon request by a dealer;
189 (B) Include all relevant endpoints to allow for access to all protected dealer data or to
190 integrate with protected dealer data; and
191 (C) Provide granularity and control necessary for dealers and authorized integrators to
192 integrate the data necessary pursuant to contract terms between the dealer and
193 authorized integrator.
194 (2) For purposes of this Code section, secure open application programming shall not
195 require that an application programming interface be available to the public or at no cost
196 to an authorized integrator, provided that no data access overcharge may be assessed in
197 connection with a secure open API.
- 8 -
25 LC 55 0490S
198 (d)(1) A dealer data systems vendor shall adopt and make available a standardized
199 integration framework that allows for integration through secure open APIs to authorized
200 integrators. In the event that application programming interfaces are no longer the
201 reasonable commercial or technical standard for secure data integration, a similar open
202 access integration method may be provided to the extent such method provides the same
203 or better secure integration to dealers and authorized integrators as a secure open API.
204 Any dealer data systems vendor that utilizes STAR standards or a comparable system
205 shall comply with the requirements of this subsection.
206 (2) A dealer data systems vendor and authorized integrator:
207 (A) Shall be authorized to integrate, or otherwise access, use, store, or share protected
208 dealer data to the extent only outlined in and authorized by the dealer data systems
209 vendor contract or authorized integrator contract;
210 (B) Shall provide that any dealer data systems vendor contract or authorized integrator
211 contract may be terminated upon no more than 90 days' notice from the dealer; and
212 (C) Shall, in order to prevent any risk of consumer harm or inconvenience, ensure a
213 secure transition of all protected dealer data to a successor dealer data systems vendor
214 or authorized integrator upon notice of a dealer's intent to terminate a dealer data
215 systems vendor contract or authorized integrator contract. The requirements of this
216 subparagraph may be satisfied by taking any of the following actions:
217 (i) Providing unrestricted access to all protected dealer data and all other data stored
218 in the dealer data system within a commercially reasonable time and in a format that
219 a successor dealer data systems vendor or authorized integrator is capable of
220 accessing and using;
221 (ii) Deleting or returning to the dealer all protected dealer data prior to termination
222 of the contract pursuant to any written directions of the dealer;

223 (iii) Providing a dealer, upon request, with a list of all entities with whom it is sharing
224 or has shared protected dealer data or to whom it has allowed access to protected
225 dealer data; and
226 (iv) Allowing a dealer to audit access to and use of any protected dealer data by the
227 dealer data systems vendor or authorized integrator access.
228 (3) Any dealer data systems vendor, authorized integrator, or third party acting
229 pursuant to a written contract with or on behalf of a dealer shall indemnify the dealer
230 for any third-party claims asserted against or damages incurred by the dealer from
231 complaints, claims, or actions arising out of the willful, negligent, or impermissible use

235 fines, penalties, litigation costs, defense costs, court costs, costs related to the disclosure
236 of security breaches, and attorneys' fees arising out of complaints, claims, civil, or
237 administrative actions.
238 (e) A manufacturer, franchisor, distributor, or affiliate thereof, or any third party acting on
239 behalf of a manufacturer, franchisor, distributor, or affiliate thereof; a data systems vendor;
240 or dealer shall not prohibit a dealer from regularly and continually monitoring or auditing
241 the specific data accessed from or written to a dealer data system or from complying with
242 applicable state and federal laws or any rules or regulations promulgated thereunder. This
243 subsection shall not impose an obligation on a manufacturer, franchisor, distributor, or
244 affiliate thereof, a data systems vendor, or a third party to perform such monitoring or
245 auditing.
246 (f) A manufacturer, franchisor, distributor, data systems vendor or any third party acting
247 on behalf of a manufacturer, franchisor, distributor, data systems vendor, or dealer shall not
248 prohibit a dealer from copying, storing, or backing up data stored on its dealer data
249 systems, or duplicating the critical components or functions thereof, for the purpose of
- 10 -
25 LC 55 0490S
250 allowing a dealer to restore business operations in the event of a security breach or other
251 event that renders a dealer data system inoperable. This subsection shall not impose an
252 obligation on a manufacturer, franchisor, distributor, data systems vendor, or third party
253 to provide such capabilities. This subsection shall not relieve a dealer from any contractual
254 obligation relating to the safeguard, storage, copy, use, ownership, or sharing of or access
255 to data.
256 (g) A dealer data systems vendor or authorized integrator shall not be held responsible for
257 any action taken directly by a dealer or for any appropriately taken action upon written
258 request of a dealer to the extent that such action prevents such vendor or integrator from
259 meeting any legal obligation regarding the protection of protected dealer data or results in
260 any liability as a consequence of such actions by the dealer.
261 (h) A dealer shall not be held responsible for any action taken directly by any of its dealer
262 data systems vendors or authorized integrators or for any appropriately taken action upon
263 written request of a dealer data systems vendor or authorized integrator to the extent that
264 such action prevents such dealer from meeting any legal obligation regarding the protection
265 of protected dealer data or results in any liability as a consequence of such actions by the
266 dealer data systems vendor or authorized integrator.
267 (i) The rights conferred on dealers in this Code section are not waivable and may not be
268 reduced or otherwise modified by any contract, agreement, or incentive program.
269 (j) Nothing in this Code section shall:
270 (1) Be interpreted to prevent a dealer or third party from discharging its obligations or
271 limiting responsibilities as a service provider to protect and secure protected dealer data
272 under an agreement or as otherwise required pursuant to federal, state, or local law;
273 (2) Give a new motor vehicle dealer any ownership or rights to share or use motor
274 vehicle diagnostic data beyond what is necessary to fulfill a dealer's obligation to provide
275 warranty, repair, or service work to its customers;
276 (3) Govern, restrict, or apply to data that exists outside of a dealer data system; or
- 11 -

277 (4) Relieve a manufacturer, franchisor, distributor, third party acting on behalf of a
278 manufacturer, franchisor, distributor, or affiliate thereof from the requirements of Code
279 Section 10-1-632."

The Senate Committee on Regulated Industries and Utilities offered the following
substitute to SB 81:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Article 22 of Chapter 1 of Title 10 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated,
2 relating to motor vehicle franchise practices, so as provide for protection of consumer data
3 in motor vehicle sales or lease transactions by affiliates of franchisors, manufacturers, and
4 distributors; to provide for procedures, conditions, and limitations on the use of motor
5 vehicle dealer data; to provide for standards for access and integration of data collected and
6 maintained by dealers and by third parties on behalf of dealers; to provide for applicability;
7 to provide for requirements of franchisors, manufacturers, and distributors in relation to
8 dealers; to provide for additional unlawful activities by franchisors; to revise legislative
9 findings; to provide for definitions; to amend Code Section 40-2-39 of the Official Code of
10 Georgia Annotated, relating to registration and licensing of new motor vehicle dealers,
11 temporary site permits, administrative fines, and penalty, so as to provide for activities which
12 qualify as activity as a new motor vehicle dealer; to provide for related matters; to repeal
13 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:

19 "10-1-621.
20 The General Assembly finds and declares that:
21 (1) The distribution and sale of motor vehicles within this state are vital to the general
22 economy of this state and to the public interest and public welfare;
23 (2) The provision for warranty service, recall service, and the repair of predelivery
24 transportation damages to motor vehicles is of substantial concern to the people of this
25 state;
26 (3) Robust competition among dealers benefits consumers through competitive pricing,
27 service, and convenient repair facilities, thus the The maintenance of full and fair
28 inter-brand and intra-brand competition among dealers and others is in the public interest;

32 of new automotive technology and support and maintenance of such after a sale, to
33 protect against the creation or perpetuation of monopolies and other practices that are
34 detrimental to public welfare and local businesses, and to provide stable employment to
35 the citizens of this state."

113 Said article is further amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
114 "10-1-633.
115 (a) As used in this Code section, the term:
116 (1) 'Authorized integrator' means a third party with whom a dealer has entered into a
117 written contract to perform a specific function which requires such third party to access
118 protected dealer data or write data to a dealer data system or both.
- 5 -

119 (2) 'Data access overcharge' means any charge to a dealer or person authorized to
120 perform integration that is greater than an amount which reimburses direct costs incurred
121 by a dealer data systems vendor for integration. When a dealer data systems vendor fails
122 to disclose the direct costs of integration and provide documentation of such costs, any
123 direct costs sought to be reimbursed shall be considered a data access overcharge.
124 (3) 'Dealer data system' means any software or hardware provided by a third party and
125 used by a dealer in its business operations to store, process, or maintain protected dealer
126 data.
127 (4) 'Dealer data systems vendor' means any third party that stores protected dealer data
128 pursuant to written contract with a dealer and shall include a dealer management system
129 or customer relations management system provided by a third party. Such term shall not
130 include a manufacturer, distributor, or affiliate subject to Code Section 10-1-632.
131 (5) 'Integration' means access by an authorized integrator to protected dealer data which
132 does not require access to any copyright protected material but allows for access to all
133 protected dealer data and which can be accomplished by any commercially reasonable
134 means not otherwise in violation of this Code section.
135 (6) 'Prior express written consent' means written consent provided by the dealer that is
136 contained in a document separate from any other consent, contract, franchise agreement,
137 or other writing and that specifically outlines a dealer's consent for an authorized
138 integrator to obtain protected dealer data, including the scope and duration of such
139 consent, and may be unilaterally revoked by the dealer upon 30 days' notice without
140 cause and immediately with cause.
141 (7) 'Protected dealer data' means any of the following data that is stored in a dealer data
142 system:
143 (A) Personal, financial, or other data pertaining to a consumer that is provided to a
144 dealer by a consumer;
145 (B) Motor vehicle diagnostic data; or
- 6 -

146 (C) Any other data relating to the business operations of a dealer stored or maintained
147 within a dealer data system.
148 (8) 'Secure open application programming interface' or 'secure open API' means an
149 application programming interface that allows an authorized integrator to integrate with
150 a dealer data system remotely and securely.
151 (9) 'STAR standards' means standards for the automotive retail industry established by
152 the Standards for Technology in Automotive Retail organization.
153 (10) 'Third party' includes service providers, vendors, including dealer data systems
154 vendors and authorized integrators, and any other individual or entity other than a dealer.
155 Such term shall not include any manufacturer; distributor; affiliate of a manufacturer or
156 distributor; a third party acting on behalf of or engaged contractually or by other

158 entity acting pursuant to federal, state, or local law; or any third party acting pursuant to
159 a valid court order.
