25 LC 112 2894
House Resolution 228
the 62nd, Cannon of the 58th, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Honoring the life and memory of Nellie Dunaway Duke; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, the State of Georgia mourns the loss of one of its most distinguished citizens
3 with the passing of Nellie Dunaway Duke on January 28, 2025; and
4 WHEREAS, Nellie Dunaway Duke was born on March 8, 1931, in Cave Springs, Georgia,
5 a beloved daughter of Jack and Emily Broom Dunaway; and
6 WHEREAS, she spent her early years in Rome, Georgia, where she attended Girl's High
7 School and played basketball during a time when female players were restricted to half-court
8 positions, excelling as a running guard through her skill and determination; and
9 WHEREAS, upon graduating from high school at 16, she played semi-professional women's
10 basketball for two years before meeting and marrying Henry Duke in December 1948, with
11 whom she shared 66 years of loving marriage; and
12 WHEREAS, Nellie Dunaway Duke was a tireless advocate and champion for women's rights
13 who played a pivotal role in the efforts to ratify the Equal Rights Amendment in Georgia and
H. R. 228

14 served for decades as the chair of the Georgia Commission on Women, often using her
15 homemade "Nellie's Jellies" as a calling card to engage lawmakers and governors; and
16 WHEREAS, beyond her advocacy, she was a dedicated servant leader who worked as the
17 director of Christian education for the First Methodist Church in Carrollton, the director of
18 the Teen Club at the Carrollton Recreation Department, and the West Georgia regional
19 director for the American Cancer Society; and
20 WHEREAS, she was blessed with five wonderful children, Gary, Carol, Kenneth, Michael,
21 and Alan; ten outstanding grandchildren, Jonathan, Jessica, Jennifer, Jesse, Seth, Sarah,
22 Emily, Amber, Ben, and Hannah; and ten magnificent great-grandchildren; and
23 WHEREAS, a compassionate and generous woman, Nellie Dunaway Duke will long be
24 remembered for her love of family, friendship, and community and will be missed by all who
25 had the great fortune of knowing her.
26 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
27 the members of this body join in honoring the life and memory of Nellie Dunaway Duke and
28 express their deepest and most sincere regret at her passing.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
30 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the
31 family of Nellie Dunaway Duke.
H. R. 228
