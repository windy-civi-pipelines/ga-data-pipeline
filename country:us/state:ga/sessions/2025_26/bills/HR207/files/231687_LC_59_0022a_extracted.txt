25 LC 59 0022
House Resolution 207
55th, and Bonner of the 73rd
A RESOLUTION
1 Creating the House Study Committee on the Eradication of Homelessness; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, homelessness imposes a tremendous economic and social cost to homeless
4 individuals, their families, businesses, neighborhoods, and government; and
5 WHEREAS, unacceptable levels of homelessness among adults, older adults, and families
6 remain an ongoing challenge facing the State of Georgia; and
7 WHEREAS, the factors that contribute to homelessness are many and complex, including
8 a lack of affordable housing, a shortage of family wage jobs, domestic violence, a shortage
9 of housing with supportive services, high levels of debt, substance abuse, mental illness,
10 access to affordable healthcare, and people exiting institutions and systems of care; and
11 WHEREAS, homelessness is a multifaceted problem requiring coordination of resources,
12 systems, and efforts from the private sector and from all levels of government; and
13 WHEREAS, the State of Georgia must find a long-term solution to homelessness; and
H. R. 207
- 1 -
25 LC 59 0022
14 WHEREAS, a study is needed to understand the extent of the problem of homelessness so
15 as to eradicate it.
16 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES:
17 (1) Creation of House study committee. There is created the House Study Committee
18 on the Eradication of Homelessness.
19 (2) Members and officers. The committee shall be composed of nine members of the
20 House of Representatives to be appointed by the Speaker of the House of Representatives
21 as follows:
22 (A) One member from the House Committee on Appropriations;
23 (B) One member from the House Committee on Budget and Fiscal Affairs Oversight;
24 (C) One member from the House Committee on Defense and Veterans Affairs;
25 (D) One member from the House Committee on Higher Education;
26 (E) One member from the House Committee on MARTOC;
27 (F) One member from the House Committee on Public Health;
28 (G) One member from the House Committee on Public Safety and Homeland Security;
29 (H) One member from the House Committee on Urban Affairs; and
30 (I) One additional member from the House of Representatives.
31 The Speaker of the House of Representatives shall designate one such member as
32 chairperson of the committee.
33 (3) Powers and duties. The committee shall undertake a study of the conditions, needs,
34 issues, and problems mentioned above or related thereto and recommend any action or
35 legislation which the committee deems necessary or appropriate.
36 (4) Meetings. The chairperson shall call all meetings of the committee. The committee
37 may conduct such meetings at such places and at such times as it may deem necessary or
38 convenient to enable it to exercise fully and effectively its powers, perform its duties, and
39 accomplish the objectives and purposes of this resolution.
H. R. 207
- 2 -
25 LC 59 0022
40 (5) Allowances and funding. The legislative members of the committee shall receive
41 the allowances provided for in Code Section 28-1-8 of the Official Code of Georgia
42 Annotated. The allowances authorized by this resolution shall not be received by any
43 member of the committee for more than five days unless additional days are authorized.
44 Funds necessary to carry out the provisions of this resolution shall come from funds
45 appropriated to the House of Representatives.
46 (6) Report.
47 (A) In the event the committee adopts any specific findings or recommendations that
48 include suggestions for proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file a report of the
49 same prior to the date of abolishment specified in this resolution, subject to
50 subparagraph (C) of this paragraph.
51 (B) In the event the committee adopts a report that does not include suggestions for
52 proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file the report, subject to subparagraph (C)
53 of this paragraph.
54 (C) No report shall be filed unless the same has been approved prior to the date of
55 abolishment specified in this resolution by majority vote of a quorum of the committee.
56 A report so approved shall be signed by the chairperson of the committee and filed with
57 the Clerk of the House of Representatives.
58 (D) In the absence of an approved report, the chairperson may file with the Clerk of the
59 House of Representatives a copy of the minutes of the meetings of the committee in lieu
60 thereof.
61 (7) Abolishment. The committee shall stand abolished on December 1, 2025.
