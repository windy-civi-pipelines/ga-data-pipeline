25 LC 39 4575S
By: Representatives Richardson of the 125th, Leverett of the 123rd, Thomas of the 21st, Clifton
of the 131st, Crowe of the 118th, and others
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 1 of Title 6 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to general
2 provisions relative to aviation, so as to prohibit certain actions by operators of unmanned
3 aircraft systems; to provide for a penalty; to provide for applicability; to provide for
4 definitions; to provide for related matters; to provide for an effective date; to repeal
5 conflicting laws; and for other purposes.
6 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
7 SECTION 1.
8 Chapter 1 of Title 6 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to general provisions
9 relative to aviation, is amended by revising Code Section 6-1-4, relating to unmanned aircraft
10 system defined, preemption for unmanned aircraft systems, and operations, as follows:
11 "6-1-4.
12 (a)(1) As used in this Code section, the term 'unmanned:
13 (1) 'Ticketed entertainment event' means any music, sporting, or performing arts event
14 or a public fireworks exhibition or display permitted pursuant to Chapter 10 of Title 25
15 held in a location with gated entries or barriers that prevent access to the general public
H. B. 58 (SUB)

16 and in which a revocable license that has been issued by an owner, operator, or lessee of
17 such property is required for attendance at such event.
18 (2) 'Unmanned aircraft system' means a:
19 (A) A powered, aerial vehicle that:
20 (A)(i) Does not carry a human operator and is operated without the possibility of
21 direct human intervention from within or on the aircraft;
22 (B)(ii) Uses aerodynamic forces to provide vehicle lift;
23 (C)(iii) Can fly autonomously or be piloted remotely; and
24 (D)(iv) Can be expendable or recoverable.
25 (2)(B) Such term shall not include a satellite.
26 (b) Any ordinance, resolution, regulation, or policy of any county, municipality, or other
27 political subdivision of this state regulating the testing or operation of unmanned aircraft
28 systems shall be deemed preempted and shall be null, void, and of no force and effect;
29 provided, however, that a county, municipality, or other political subdivision of this state
30 may:
31 (1) Enforce any ordinance that was adopted on or before April 1, 2017;
32 (2) Adopt an ordinance that enforces Federal Aviation Administration restrictions; or
33 (3) Adopt an ordinance that provides for or prohibits the launch or intentional landing
34 of an unmanned aircraft system from or on its public property except with respect to the
35 operation of an unmanned aircraft system for commercial purposes.
36 (c) The state, through agency or departmental rules and regulations, may provide for or
37 prohibit the launch or intentional landing of an unmanned aircraft system from or on its
38 public property.
39 (d)(1) It shall be unlawful to operate an unmanned aircraft system within 400 feet of or
40 above a ticketed entertainment event.
41 (2) A person who violates this subsection shall be guilty of the unauthorized operation
42 of an unmanned aircraft system and shall be punished as for a misdemeanor.
H. B. 58 (SUB)
- 2 -
25 LC 39 4575S
43 (3) This subsection shall not apply when a person who operates an unmanned aircraft
44 system:
45 (A) Has obtained consent by any person with legal authority over a ticketed
46 entertainment event;
47 (B) Is authorized by federal regulations to operate an unmanned aircraft system and is
48 operating such system in a lawful manner and consistent with federal regulations;
49 (C) Is an employee of the property where the ticketed entertainment event is held and
50 is conducting official business; or
51 (D) Is an employee of a water, sewer, electrical, telephone, cable, or other regulated
52 utility service provider and is conducting official business."
53 SECTION 2.
54 This Act shall become effective upon its approval by the Governor or upon its becoming law
55 without such approval.
56 SECTION 3.
57 All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Act are repealed.
