Section 1:
25 LC 56 0301S
substitute to HB 238:
A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
AN ACT
1 To amend Chapter 5 of Title 35 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to
2 Georgia Public Safety Training Center, so as to require the Georgia Public Safety Training
3 Center to create and maintain an at-risk adult training course focusing on officer response to
4 individuals with Alzheimer's disease or dementia; to provide for requirements for such
5 course; to provide for collaborative course development with the Division of Aging Services;
6 to provide for approval of the training program by the Georgia Peace Officer Standards and
7 Training Council; to provide for related matters; to repeal conflicting laws; and for other
8 purposes.
9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF GEORGIA:
10 SECTION 1.
11 Chapter 5 of Title 35 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to Georgia Public
12 Safety Training Center, is amended by adding a new Code section to read as follows:
13 "35-5-8.
14 (a) The Georgia Public Safety Training Center shall create and maintain an at-risk adult
15 training course available to all peace officers, emergency medical personnel, and
H. B. 238 (SUB)
- 1 -
25 LC 56 0301S
16 behavioral health co-responders relating to responding to at-risk adults, including those
17 individuals living with Alzheimer's disease or other forms of dementia.
18 (b) Such training course shall, at a minimum, provide training in:
19 (1) Investigating criminal violations involving benefits trafficking and related offenses;
20 (2) Investigating criminal violations involving the abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a
21 disabled adult or elder person;
22 (3) Responding to reports of missing persons;
23 (4) Interacting with victims of abuse, neglect, and exploitation in a victim centered and
24 trauma informed way; and
25 (5) Certain aspects of Alzheimer's disease and other forms of dementia and cognitive
26 impairment, including;
27 (A) Instruction in interacting with persons with Alzheimer's disease and other forms
28 of dementia;
29 (B) Techniques for recognizing the behavioral symptoms and characteristics of
30 Alzheimer's disease and other forms of dementia;
31 (C) Techniques for effective communication with persons with Alzheimer's disease and
32 other forms of dementia;
33 (D) Techniques for employing alternatives to physical restraints; and
34 (E) Protocols for contacting caregivers when an individual with Alzheimer's disease
35 or other form of dementia is found wandering or during an emergency situation.
36 (c) The training course provided for in this Code section shall be developed by the Georgia
37 Public Safety Training Center in collaboration with the Forensic Special Initiatives Unit
38 of the Division of Aging Services of the Department of Human Services.
39 (d) The number of hours and specific curriculum required for the training course provided
40 for in this Code section shall be approved by the Georgia Peace Officer Standards and
41 Training Council."
