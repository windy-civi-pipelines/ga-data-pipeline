25 LC 60 0139
House Resolution 593
of the 27th, and Williams of the 168th
A RESOLUTION
1 Creating the House Study Committee on Maternal, Prenatal, and Pediatric Care Access and
2 Funding; and for other purposes.
3 WHEREAS, maternal, prenatal, and pediatric care is critical to the health of mothers and the
4 health and development of infants; and
5 WHEREAS, Georgia currently ranks forty-ninth in maternal deaths in the United States; and
6 WHEREAS, prenatal care can help prevent complications and inform pregnant women about
7 steps they can take to protect their infant and ensure a healthy pregnancy; and
8 WHEREAS, significant variability in funding capacity of healthcare facilities and providers
9 across regions of the state affects access to maternal, prenatal, and pediatric healthcare; and
10 WHEREAS, it would be beneficial to conduct a study to evaluate maternal, prenatal, and
11 pediatric healthcare in this state, identify any barriers that may exist to access such
12 healthcare, explore potential additional sources of funding to improve access, identify any
13 workforce and infrastructure needs that may exist, and identify potential solutions.
H. R. 593
- 1 -
25 LC 60 0139
14 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES:
15 (1) Creation of House study committee. There is created the House Study Committee
16 on Maternal, Prenatal, and Pediatric Care Access and Funding.
17 (2) Members and officers. The committee shall be composed of five members of the
18 House of Representatives to be appointed by the Speaker of the House of
19 Representatives. The Speaker shall designate a member of the committee as chairperson
20 of the committee.
21 (3) Powers and duties. The committee shall undertake a study of the conditions, needs,
22 issues, and problems mentioned above or related thereto and recommend any action or
23 legislation which the committee deems necessary or appropriate.
24 (4) Meetings. The chairperson shall call all meetings of the committee. The committee
25 may conduct such meetings at such places and at such times as it may deem necessary or
26 convenient to enable it to exercise fully and effectively its powers, perform its duties, and
27 accomplish the objectives and purposes of this resolution. No fewer than two meetings
28 shall be conducted in a county with a rural hospital organization, as such term is defined
29 in Code Section 31-8-9.1 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated.
30 (5) Allowances and funding. The legislative members of the committee shall receive
31 the allowances provided for in Code Section 28-1-8 of the Official Code of Georgia
32 Annotated. The allowances authorized by this resolution shall not be received by any
33 member of the committee for more than five days unless additional days are authorized.
34 Funds necessary to carry out the provisions of this resolution shall come from funds
35 appropriated to the House of Representatives.
36 (6) Report.
37 (A) In the event the committee adopts any specific findings or recommendations that
38 include suggestions for proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file a report of the
39 same prior to the date of abolishment specified in this resolution, subject to
40 subparagraph (C) of this paragraph.
H. R. 593
- 2 -
25 LC 60 0139
41 (B) In the event the committee adopts a report that does not include suggestions for
42 proposed legislation, the chairperson shall file the report subject to subparagraph (C)
43 of this paragraph.
44 (C) No report shall be filed unless the same has been approved prior to the date of
45 abolishment specified in this resolution by majority vote of a quorum of the committee.
46 A report so approved shall be signed by the chairperson of the committee and filed with
47 the Clerk of the House of Representatives.
48 (D) In the absence of an approved report, the chairperson may file with the Clerk of the
49 House of Representatives a copy of the minutes of the meetings of the committee in lieu
50 thereof.
51 (7) Abolishment. The committee shall stand abolished on December 1, 2025.
