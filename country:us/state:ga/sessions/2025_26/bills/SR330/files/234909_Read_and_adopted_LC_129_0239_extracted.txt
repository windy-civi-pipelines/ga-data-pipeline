Senate Resolution 330
Hickman of the 4th and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Honoring the life and memory of Dr. William Nettles Wessinger; and for other purposes.
2 WHEREAS, the State of Georgia mourns the loss of one of its most distinguished citizens
3 with the passing of Dr. William Nettles Wessinger on February 20, 2025; and
4 WHEREAS, Dr. William Nettles Wessinger was born on March 25, 1947, in Columbia,
5 South Carolina, a beloved son of James Bryson and Eliza Nettles Wessinger; and
6 WHEREAS, he served as a guardian of this nation's freedom and liberty with the United
7 States Army, valiantly and courageously aiding his fellow Americans as a Flight Surgeon and
8 holding the rank of Captain prior to his honorable discharge; and
9 WHEREAS, after his military service, he was accepted into the Duke University School of
10 Medicine's orthopedic surgery residency, where he served as chief resident in his fourth year
11 and completed a Fellowship in Hand and Upper Extremities; and
12 WHEREAS, in 1981, he relocated to Savannah, Georgia, to begin his medical career with
13 Orthopedic Associates, which later merged with Optim Orthopedics, and for over 23 years,
S. R. 330

14 he provided exceptional care to his patients, forming lasting relationships and earning a
15 reputation as one of the finest orthopedic surgeons in the region; and
16 WHEREAS, transitioning into hospital administration, he further served as Senior Vice
17 President of Medical Affairs and Chief Medical Officer at Memorial Health, where he
18 contributed to the Quality Improvement Council; and
19 WHEREAS, he was widely respected for his expertise and contributions to the medical field,
20 earning membership in the American Society of Surgery of the Hand, the Piedmont
21 Orthopedic Society at Duke, and the Georgia Orthopedic Society; and
22 WHEREAS, he was united in love and marriage to his wife, Karen, and was blessed with two
23 remarkable children, Lucy and William, as well as two wonderful grandchildren, Noel and
24 Freya; and
25 WHEREAS, a compassionate and generous man, Dr. William Nettles Wessinger will long
26 be remembered for his love of family, friendship, and community and will be missed by all
27 who had the great fortune of knowing him.
28 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE that the members of this body
29 join in honoring the life and memory of Dr. William Nettles Wessinger and express their
30 deepest and most sincere regret at his passing.
31 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is authorized and directed
32 to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to the family of Dr.
33 William Nettles Wessinger.
