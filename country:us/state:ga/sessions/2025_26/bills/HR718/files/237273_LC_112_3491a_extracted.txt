25 LC 112 3491
House Resolution 718
of the 104th, Powell of the 33rd, and others
A RESOLUTION
1 Recognizing and commending Scott Maxwell on the occasion of his retirement; and for other
2 purposes.
3 WHEREAS, Scott Maxwell has rendered decades of exceptional service across the public
4 and private sectors, using his extensive media and legislative experience, strategic insight,
5 and relationships with decision-makers at all levels of government to influence public policy
6 and support clients throughout Georgia and beyond; and
7 WHEREAS, he is the founding principal of Mathews & Maxwell Inc., a respected
8 governmental affairs consulting firm he co-founded in 1997 that has twice been named the
9 most effective small governmental affairs firm in Georgia by James Magazine; and
10 WHEREAS, during his tenure at Mathews & Maxwell, he has led the development of short-
11 and long-term strategies to help clients achieve success in legislative, procurement, and
12 regulatory matters, representing sectors such as agribusiness, construction, healthcare,
13 manufacturing, transportation, and state procurement; and
14 WHEREAS, his consulting work has involved drafting policy papers, managing issue
15 education campaigns, testifying before legislative committees, monitoring the work of
H. R. 718

16 advisory groups and executive boards, and developing media strategies to address public
17 perception challenges; and
18 WHEREAS, he has further devoted nearly six years to public service in Washington, D.C.,
19 serving in multiple roles for United States Senator Sam Nunn and handling media
20 responsibilities for the Senate Armed Services Committee, where Senator Nunn served as
21 chairman; and
22 WHEREAS, his public service experience also includes work as assistant press secretary for
23 the Ginn for Governor Campaign in 1982, reading clerk for the Georgia State Senate in 1981,
24 and disaster public information officer for the United States Small Business Administration
25 from 1979 to 1980; and
26 WHEREAS, it is abundantly fitting and proper that the outstanding accomplishments of this
27 remarkable and distinguished individual be appropriately recognized.
28 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that
29 the members of this body recognize and commend Scott Maxwell for his decades of
30 exemplary career service; congratulate him upon the grand occasion of his retirement; and
31 extend the most sincere best wishes for continued health and happiness.
32 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized
33 and directed to make an appropriate copy of this resolution available for distribution to Scott
34 Maxwell.
